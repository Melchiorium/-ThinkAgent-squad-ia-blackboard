# CEO Evaluation - CareSync - Version 1

## 1. Business viability
The pain is real and acute: family care coordination is fragmented and error-prone. The one-relative, invitation-only wedge is disciplined and practical.  
Viability depends on trust. If families do not trust data handling and permission boundaries, adoption will stall immediately.

## 2. Profitability potential
A family-paid subscription model is plausible because the coordinator experiences direct pain and potential time savings.  
Profitability timing is uncertain: high-touch onboarding and support will suppress margins until repeat adoption and referral loops are proven.

## 3. Execution complexity
Technical complexity is medium due to sensitive-data controls, role permissions, audit trails, and reliable reminders.  
This is not a trivial task app: privacy posture and operational support quality are critical from day one.

## 4. Go-to-market realism
Founder-led concierge recruitment of Paris family coordinators is realistic for an early pilot.  
GTM credibility requires measurable behavioral migration from WhatsApp/calls, not just signups.

## 5. Competitive risk
Direct incumbents are weakly specialized (chat, calendars, generic task tools), which creates an opening.  
However, replacement risk is still meaningful because users may stick with existing habits unless CareSync delivers obvious weekly coordination relief.

## Major risks
- Privacy/compliance concerns around health-adjacent family data
- Low multi-participant adoption after coordinator signup
- Failure to replace existing communication habits
- Reminder channel reliability issues reducing trust
- Scope creep into complex healthcare workflows

## 6. Final investment decision
**APPROVE**

## 7. Global note
**7/10**

## Final recommendation
Approve a constrained pilot investment with strict conditions: one-relative scope, invitation-only access, explicit role boundaries, auditable access/change logs, and clear weekly-usage success metrics.  
Do not fund scope expansion until week-2 retention and participant activation thresholds are met.
