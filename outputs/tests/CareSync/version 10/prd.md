# CareSync Product Proposal

## Product Problem
Families coordinating care for elderly relatives face significant challenges with fragmented information, making it difficult to manage medical appointments, medication schedules, and caregiving responsibilities effectively. This experience leads to increased stress and potential oversight in care, compounded by issues of trust and data sensitivity.

## Initial Wedge
Develop a foundational platform that centralizes scheduling for medical appointments and medication reminders, addressing the critical need for coordination among family members and professional caregivers.

## First Target User
Adults managing care for elderly parents, particularly those living in different cities.

## Existing Alternatives And Switching Trigger
Families currently rely on disparate communication tools such as phone calls, messaging apps, and handwritten notes. The switching trigger will be the ease of use and consolidation of all care-related tasks in one platform, reducing frustration and boosting coordination efficiency.

## Core MVP Workflow
1. Create individual family accounts tied to a shared family dashboard.
2. Allow users to input and manage medical appointments.
3. Enable medication reminders to be set up and shared among family members.
4. Provide a messaging system for communication between family members and caregivers.
5. Implement a simplified onboarding process, including tutorial sessions for users with low digital literacy.

## In Scope
- Shared family dashboard for appointment and task overview.
- Calendar coordination for medical appointments.
- Medication reminders functionality.
- Basic messaging system for coordinating care.
- User management system with permissions for family members and caregivers.
- Accessibility features to support users with low digital literacy.
- Explicit outline of privacy controls and compliance measures that align with relevant regulations.

## Out Of Scope
- Advanced features such as secure document storage, emergency contact coordination, and detailed task management will be deferred to later stages.
- Features targeting professional caregivers and home healthcare agencies will be excluded from the MVP.
- Referral incentive systems, immediate user feedback mechanisms, and detailed onboarding materials beyond basic tutorials will be deferred until after initial deployment.
- Specific user roles and access levels will be explored in future iterations.

## Business Model Hypothesis
The platform will be monetized through a subscription model targeting families who use the service regularly for coordinating care for their elderly relatives.

## Critical Assumptions
- Families will prefer a centralized platform for care coordination over existing fragmented methods.
- Users can navigate and effectively use the platform despite potential low digital literacy.
- Care coordination needs for families do not require advanced features in the initial phase.
- The platform can establish a strong privacy and compliance strategy to foster user trust.

## How To Test Quickly
- Conduct user interviews and surveys with the target segment to assess interest in the MVP features.
- Create a low-fidelity prototype to gather feedback on usability and appeal.
- Test key workflows with a small group of users to validate the platform's value proposition.

## Acceptance Criteria
- Minimum of 100 active users within the first month of launch.
- At least 70% of users report that the platform effectively reduces care coordination stress.
- Successful implementation of core features with no critical bugs at launch.

## Risks And Failure Modes
- Users may not adopt the platform if it is perceived as complicated or untrustworthy with personal data.
- Low user engagement due to inadequate usability for the elderly population.
- Regulatory and compliance risks related to handling sensitive medical information.

## Product Readiness
Status: LIMITED

Blocking Gaps:
- Validating demand and usability with target users. [demand_validation]
- Addressing concerns related to data privacy and trust. [privacy_trust]

Required Improvements:
- Develop a clear plan for onboarding users with low digital literacy, including tutorial sessions. [onboarding]

## Recommendation
Proceed with building a concise MVP that focuses on medical appointment scheduling and medication reminders, incorporating a simplified onboarding process for low digital literacy users, with validation from potential users. Following initial deployment, further improvements and features can be added based on user feedback.
