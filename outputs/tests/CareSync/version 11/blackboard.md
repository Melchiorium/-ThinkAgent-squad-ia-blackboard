## Project Brief

Project Name: CareSync

Pitch:
A digital platform that helps families coordinate care for elderly relatives by centralizing medical appointments, medication reminders, caregiving tasks, and communication between family members and professional caregivers.

Context:
Many families struggle to manage care for aging parents or relatives, especially when multiple siblings or caregivers are involved. Information is often fragmented across phone calls, messaging apps, handwritten notes, and hospital documents.

At the same time, professional caregivers and home assistance providers often lack visibility into family decisions and day-to-day coordination.

The platform aims to simplify elderly care coordination while reducing stress for families.

Target Users:
- Adults managing care for elderly parents
- Family members living in different cities
- Professional caregivers
- Home nurses
- Elderly care agencies

Potential Use Cases:
- Scheduling medical appointments
- Medication reminders
- Shared task management
- Emergency contact coordination
- Sharing medical documents
- Tracking caregiving responsibilities

Platform Capabilities:
- Shared family dashboard
- Calendar coordination
- Notifications/reminders
- Secure document storage
- Messaging system
- Permission management for family members and caregivers

Constraints:
- High trust and privacy expectations
- Sensitive medical-related information
- Elderly users may have low digital literacy
- Potential legal/data compliance issues depending on country

Challenges:
- Convincing families to adopt a new platform
- Balancing simplicity with complex coordination needs
- Building trust around sensitive personal information
- Identifying a sustainable business model

Long-term Vision:
Become the operating system for family caregiving coordination and eventually expand into broader home healthcare services.

# CareSync MVP Proposal

## Product Problem
Families struggle to manage care for elderly relatives effectively due to fragmented information across various communication channels and tools, leading to stress and misunderstandings. Professional caregivers often lack insight into family dynamics and decisions, impacting the quality of care provided.

## Initial Wedge
The initial focus will be on a centralized family dashboard for scheduling medical appointments and medication reminders, addressing the most immediate pain points for families managing elderly care.

## First Target User
The primary target user for the first launch will be adults aged 30-50 who are managing care for their elderly parents or relatives, particularly those balancing responsibilities with siblings or other family members.

## Existing Alternatives And Switching Trigger
Currently, families rely on a combination of phone calls, messaging apps, paper notes, and email for coordination. The switching trigger will be the all-in-one, user-friendly integration of these tools into a single platform that enhances visibility and reduces daily management stress.

## Core MVP Workflow
1. User onboarding through email-based registration and profile setup.
2. Creating a shared family dashboard that allows users to:
   - Schedule medical appointments.
   - Set up medication reminders.
   - Manage caregiving tasks.
3. Enable secure messaging between family members and caregivers.
4. Provide notification reminders for appointments and medication times.

## In Scope
- Shared family dashboard
- Calendar coordination for medical appointments
- Medication reminders feature
- Secure messaging system
- Basic permission management for family members and caregivers

## Out of Scope
- Advanced features like document sharing and emergency contact coordination.
- Integrations with external healthcare systems or services.
- Detailed analytics or insights for caregivers.

## Business Model Hypothesis
The platform will operate on a subscription model for families, potentially offering tiered pricing based on the number of family members and caregivers included. Professional caregivers may pay for additional features that enhance their service offerings.

## Critical Assumptions
1. Users are willing to try a new platform for better coordination.
2. Families managing elderly care are willing to pay for a subscription service.
3. Privacy and security measures will adequately meet user trust needs.

## How To Test Quickly
Conduct interviews with target users to validate pain points and willingness to adopt a new solution. Create a prototype of the dashboard and gather feedback on usability and value propositions.

## Acceptance Criteria
- Successful onboarding of a minimum of 100 users within the first month of launch.
- At least 75% user satisfaction based on feedback regarding the dashboard's usability.
- Functionality of key features (scheduling and reminders) without significant bugs.

## Risks And Failure Modes
- Low user adoption if the onboarding process is not intuitive.
- Potential privacy breaches leading to loss of user trust.
- Families may prefer existing solutions due to habit and familiarity.
