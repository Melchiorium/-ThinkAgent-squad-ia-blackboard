## Project Brief

Project Name: CareSync

Pitch:
A digital platform that helps families coordinate care for elderly relatives by centralizing medical appointments, medication reminders, caregiving tasks, and communication between family members and professional caregivers.

Context:
Many families struggle to manage care for aging parents or relatives, especially when multiple siblings or caregivers are involved. Information is often fragmented across phone calls, messaging apps, handwritten notes, and hospital documents.

At the same time, professional caregivers and home assistance providers often lack visibility into family decisions and day-to-day coordination.

The platform aims to simplify elderly care coordination while reducing stress for families.

Target Users:
- Adults managing care for elderly parents
- Family members living in different cities
- Professional caregivers
- Home nurses
- Elderly care agencies

Potential Use Cases:
- Scheduling medical appointments
- Medication reminders
- Shared task management
- Emergency contact coordination
- Sharing medical documents
- Tracking caregiving responsibilities

Platform Capabilities:
- Shared family dashboard
- Calendar coordination
- Notifications/reminders
- Secure document storage
- Messaging system
- Permission management for family members and caregivers

Constraints:
- High trust and privacy expectations
- Sensitive medical-related information
- Elderly users may have low digital literacy
- Potential legal/data compliance issues depending on country

Challenges:
- Convincing families to adopt a new platform
- Balancing simplicity with complex coordination needs
- Building trust around sensitive personal information
- Identifying a sustainable business model

Long-term Vision:
Become the operating system for family caregiving coordination and eventually expand into broader home healthcare services.

# CareSync MVP Product Proposal

## Product Problem
Families struggle to coordinate care for elderly relatives, leading to confusion, stress, and inefficiencies in managing medical appointments and caregiving tasks. The challenge is compounded when family members live in different locations and when professional caregivers lack visibility into family dynamics.

## Initial Wedge
A centralized family dashboard that consolidates medical appointment scheduling and medication reminders for families managing care for elderly relatives.

## First Target User
Adult family members managing care for elderly parents, especially those living in different cities.

## Existing Alternatives And Switching Trigger
- **Alternatives:** Phone calls, messaging apps, handwritten notes, and fragmented document storage.
- **Switching Trigger:** A streamlined, user-friendly platform that offers a single source of truth for all caregiving activities.

## Core MVP Workflow
1. Create a shared family dashboard.
2. Schedule medical appointments with notifications for family members.
3. Set up medication reminders for elderly relatives.

## In Scope
- Shared family dashboard.
- Medical appointment scheduling.
- Medication reminders.
- Notifications for upcoming appointments and medications.

## Out of Scope
- Secure document storage.
- Messaging system.
- Permission management for family members and caregivers.
- Shared task management and emergency contact coordination.
- Features targeting professional caregivers or home agencies.

## MVP Build Vs Pilot Operations
### Must Build Now
- Shared family dashboard.
- Medical appointment scheduling.
- Medication reminders.
- Notification system.

### Manual Or Operational During Pilot
- Family onboarding and initial training to overcome digital literacy barriers.
- Manual entry for medical appointments and medications during initial user onboarding.

### Deferred Until After Proof
- Secure document storage.
- Messaging system.
- Permission management for different user roles.
- Shared task management features.

## Business Model Hypothesis
A freemium model where basic functionalities are available for free, with premium features (e.g., secure document storage, advanced reminders) offered through a subscription.

## Critical Assumptions
- Families will see value in a centralized platform for caregiving coordination.
- Users will adopt and regularly use the platform for it to be effective.
- The platform will meet high privacy and security expectations.

## How To Test Quickly
- Conduct user interviews and surveys to assess family needs and willingness to adopt the platform.
- Run a pilot with a small group of target users to gather feedback on the dashboard, appointment scheduling, and reminder functionalities.

## Acceptance Criteria
- At least 70% of pilot users report improved coordination of care for their elderly relatives.
- At least 80% usability scores in ease of use and clarity during initial onboarding.
- Successfully schedule and send reminders for a minimum of 50 medical appointments across users during the pilot.

## Risks And Failure Modes
- High barriers to adoption due to resistance to new technology.
- Failure to establish trust around data privacy, leading to low user engagement.
- Lack of clear value proposition might lead to user drop-off.
