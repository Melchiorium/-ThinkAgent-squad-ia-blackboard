# CareSync Product Proposal

## Product Problem
Families coordinating care for elderly relatives face fragmented information and communication, leading to confusion, stress, and inefficiencies. Professional caregivers lack insights into family decisions, hampering effective support.

## Initial Wedge
Centralized care coordination focused on scheduling medical appointments and medication reminders to address the most critical tasks families face in elderly care.

## First Target User
Adults managing care for elderly parents, especially those living away from them, needing a dependable method to coordinate care efforts among family members and caregivers.

## Existing Alternatives And Switching Trigger
- **Existing Alternatives**: Phone calls, messaging apps, shared calendars, and handwritten notes.
- **Switching Trigger**: A noticeable improvement in organizing care tasks and reducing miscommunication and stress.

## Core MVP Workflow
1. Users create a shared family dashboard.
2. Users can schedule medical appointments visible to all family members.
3. Users receive and send medication reminders for elderly relatives.
4. Users can communicate via a messaging system within the platform.

## In Scope
- Shared family dashboard
- Calendar for scheduling medical appointments
- Basic medication reminders
- Messaging system for communication

## Out of Scope
- Secure document storage
- Permission management for family members and caregivers
- Shared task management
- Emergency contact coordination
- Tracking caregiving responsibilities
- Advanced analytics or reports on caregiving efforts

## MVP Build Vs Pilot Operations
### Must Build Now
- Shared family dashboard
- Calendar functionality for scheduling medical appointments
- Basic medication reminders
- Messaging system

### Manual Or Operational During Pilot
- A minimal onboarding path with tutorial pop-ups specifically designed for users with low digital literacy.
- Monitoring user feedback through scheduled family check-ins.

### Deferred Until After Proof
- Secure document storage
- Permission management for additional family members and caregivers
- Emergency coordination features

## Business Model Hypothesis
CareSync could function on a subscription model, charging families a monthly fee to access premium features. Partnerships with healthcare providers should be explored for potential service integration.

## Critical Assumptions
- Families will adopt a new platform if it clearly shows value in improving care coordination.
- Users prioritize ease of use and effective communication features.
- Users will accept a subscription fee for enhanced convenience and peace of mind.

## How To Test Quickly
- Conduct surveys with target users to assess interest and feature prioritization.
- Create prototypes focused on the shared dashboard and messaging features for initial feedback.
- Run a pilot with a limited group of family caregivers to evaluate usability and perceived value.

## Acceptance Criteria
- At least 70% of pilot users report reduced stress related to care management.
- An 80% successful completion rate of scheduling and messaging tasks without guidance.
- Positive user feedback on the platform's navigation clarity and simplicity.

## Risks And Failure Modes
- Risk of low user adoption if the platform is perceived as overly complex.
- Privacy concerns could deter users from sharing sensitive information.
- High dependency on maintaining user engagement for consistent value.

## Product Readiness
Status: LIMITED

Blocking Gaps:
- Need for established trust-building features around privacy and data protection.

Required Improvements:
- Implementation of simple user interface elements specifically tailored for elderly and family users. [onboarding][privacy_trust]

## Recommendation
Proceed with the proposed MVP, focusing on the essential components of the shared dashboard, calendar coordination, medication reminders, and messaging system. Prioritize a strong onboarding process to assist adoption and address privacy concerns to enhance user trust.
