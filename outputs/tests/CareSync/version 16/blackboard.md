# Blackboard

## Project Brief

Project Name: CareSync

Pitch:
A digital platform that helps families coordinate care for elderly relatives by centralizing medical appointments, medication reminders, caregiving tasks, and communication between family members and professional caregivers.

Context:
Many families struggle to manage care for aging parents or relatives, especially when multiple siblings or caregivers are involved. Information is often fragmented across phone calls, messaging apps, handwritten notes, and hospital documents.

At the same time, professional caregivers and home assistance providers often lack visibility into family decisions and day-to-day coordination.

The platform aims to simplify elderly care coordination while reducing stress for families.

Target Users:
- Adults managing care for elderly parents
- Family members living in different cities
- Professional caregivers
- Home nurses
- Elderly care agencies

Potential Use Cases:
- Scheduling medical appointments
- Medication reminders
- Shared task management
- Emergency contact coordination
- Sharing medical documents
- Tracking caregiving responsibilities

Platform Capabilities:
- Shared family dashboard
- Calendar coordination
- Notifications/reminders
- Secure document storage
- Messaging system
- Permission management for family members and caregivers

Constraints:
- High trust and privacy expectations
- Sensitive medical-related information
- Elderly users may have low digital literacy
- Potential legal/data compliance issues depending on country

Challenges:
- Convincing families to adopt a new platform
- Balancing simplicity with complex coordination needs
- Building trust around sensitive personal information
- Identifying a sustainable business model

Long-term Vision:
Become the operating system for family caregiving coordination and eventually expand into broader home healthcare services.

## Initial PRD

# CareSync Product Proposal

## Product Problem
Families face challenges in coordinating care for elderly relatives due to fragmented information across multiple channels, leading to stress and inefficiency. Professional caregivers lack insight into family dynamics and decision-making, hindering effective care.

## Initial Wedge
The MVP will focus on a shared family dashboard that allows coordination of medical appointments and medication reminders, as these features directly address the most acute needs of primary caregivers.

## First Target User
Adults managing care for elderly parents who have siblings or other family members geographically distributed.

## Existing Alternatives And Switching Trigger
- Current alternatives include fragmented use of phone calendars, messaging apps, and paper notes.
- Switching trigger: A significant coordination event (e.g., a medical appointment or emergency) that highlights the limitations of current methods and emphasizes the value of centralization.

## Core MVP Workflow
1. **Onboarding**: Family members create accounts and set up their elderly relative's profile.
2. **Shared Dashboard**: Users access a centralized view of upcoming medical appointments.
3. **Medication Reminders**: Users input and receive notifications for medication schedules.
4. **Communication**: Families can send messages and share reminders through the system.

## In Scope
- User registration and profile setup
- Shared family dashboard displaying appointments
- Input for medication schedules and reminders
- Basic messaging system for family communication
- Permission management for sharing access with caregivers

## Out of Scope
- Advanced task management features
- Secure document storage and sharing
- Comprehensive calendar integration with external services
- Features aimed at professional caregivers or elderly users with low digital literacy

## MVP Build Vs Pilot Operations
### Must Build Now
- User registration and authentication
- Shared family dashboard
- Appointment scheduling functionality
- Medication reminder notifications

### Manual Or Operational During Pilot
- Initial onboarding assistance for families
- Manual collection of caregiver feedback
- Tracking user engagement and satisfaction through surveys

### Deferred Until After Proof
- Secure document storage and sharing capabilities
- Advanced messaging features and integrations
- User support for low digital literacy

## Business Model Hypothesis
Revenue generated through a freemium model, offering basic features for free and charging for premium features (e.g., secure document storage, advanced permissions).

## Critical Assumptions
- Families will find value in a centralized platform for care coordination.
- Users will adopt the platform readily if the onboarding process is simple and supports immediate needs.
- Trust can be established through privacy assurances and robust data protection measures.

## How To Test Quickly
- Conduct user interviews and surveys with target demographics to validate pain points.
- Launch a low-fidelity prototype to a small group of potential users for feedback on key features.
- Measure early engagement with the MVP to assess usage and satisfaction rates.

## Acceptance Criteria
- At least 70% of test users successfully set up an account and navigate the shared dashboard.
- Minimum of 50% engagement rate with set medication reminders.
- Positive feedback on utility and ease of use from at least 80% of pilot users.

## Risks And Failure Modes
- Users may not trust the platform to handle sensitive information.
- The complexity of real-world caregiving scenarios may exceed the MVP scope.
- Families may resist changing from familiar methods to a new digital platform.

# CareSync Product Proposal

## Product Problem
Families face challenges in coordinating care for elderly relatives due to fragmented information across multiple channels, leading to stress and inefficiency. Professional caregivers lack insight into family dynamics and decision-making, hindering effective care.

## Initial Wedge
The MVP will focus on a shared family dashboard that allows coordination of medical appointments and medication reminders, directly addressing the most acute needs of primary caregivers.

## First Target User
Adults managing care for elderly parents who have siblings or other family members geographically distributed.

## Existing Alternatives And Switching Trigger
- Current alternatives include fragmented use of phone calendars, messaging apps, and paper notes.
- Switching trigger: Significant coordination events (e.g., medical appointments or emergencies) that highlight the limitations of current methods and emphasize the value of centralization.

## Core MVP Workflow
1. **Onboarding**: Family members create accounts and set up their elderly relative's profile.
2. **Shared Dashboard**: Users access a centralized view of upcoming medical appointments.
3. **Medication Reminders**: Users input and receive notifications for medication schedules.
4. **Communication**: Families can send messages and share reminders through the system.

## In Scope
- User registration and profile setup
- Shared family dashboard displaying appointments
- Input for medication schedules and reminders
- Basic messaging system for family communication
- Permission management for sharing access with caregivers
- A minimal onboarding process that guides users through setup

## Out of Scope
- Advanced task management features
- Secure document storage and sharing
- Comprehensive calendar integration with external services
- Features aimed at professional caregivers or elderly users with low digital literacy
- User support documentation for onboarding and education

## MVP Build Vs Pilot Operations
### Must Build Now
- User registration and authentication
- Shared family dashboard
- Appointment scheduling functionality
- Medication reminder notifications
- Simplified onboarding process

### Manual Or Operational During Pilot
- Initial onboarding assistance for families
- Manual collection of caregiver feedback
- Tracking user engagement and satisfaction through surveys

### Deferred Until After Proof
- Secure document storage and sharing capabilities
- Advanced messaging features and integrations
- User support for low digital literacy

## Business Model Hypothesis
Revenue generated through a freemium model, offering basic features for free and charging for premium features (e.g., secure document storage, advanced permissions).

## Critical Assumptions
- Families will find value in a centralized platform for care coordination.
- Users will adopt the platform readily if the onboarding process is simple and supports immediate needs.
- Trust can be established through privacy assurances and robust data protection measures.

## How To Test Quickly
- Conduct user interviews and surveys with target demographics to validate pain points.
- Launch a low-fidelity prototype to a small group of potential users for feedback on key features.
- Measure early engagement with the MVP to assess usage and satisfaction rates.

## Acceptance Criteria
- At least 70% of test users successfully set up an account and navigate the shared dashboard.
- Minimum of 50% engagement rate with set medication reminders.
- Positive feedback on utility and ease of use from at least 80% of pilot users.
- Established privacy and data protection measures are communicated clearly during onboarding.

## Risks And Failure Modes
- Users may not trust the platform to handle sensitive information.
- The complexity of real-world caregiving scenarios may exceed the MVP scope.
- Families may resist changing from familiar methods to a new digital platform.
