## Project Brief

Project Name: CareSync

Pitch:
A digital platform that helps families coordinate care for elderly relatives by centralizing medical appointments, medication reminders, caregiving tasks, and communication between family members and professional caregivers.

Context:
Many families struggle to manage care for aging parents or relatives, especially when multiple siblings or caregivers are involved. Information is often fragmented across phone calls, messaging apps, handwritten notes, and hospital documents.

At the same time, professional caregivers and home assistance providers often lack visibility into family decisions and day-to-day coordination.

The platform aims to simplify elderly care coordination while reducing stress for families.

Target Users:
- Adults managing care for elderly parents
- Family members living in different cities
- Professional caregivers
- Home nurses
- Elderly care agencies

Potential Use Cases:
- Scheduling medical appointments
- Medication reminders
- Shared task management
- Emergency contact coordination
- Sharing medical documents
- Tracking caregiving responsibilities

Platform Capabilities:
- Shared family dashboard
- Calendar coordination
- Notifications/reminders
- Secure document storage
- Messaging system
- Permission management for family members and caregivers

Constraints:
- High trust and privacy expectations
- Sensitive medical-related information
- Elderly users may have low digital literacy
- Potential legal/data compliance issues depending on country

Challenges:
- Convincing families to adopt a new platform
- Balancing simplicity with complex coordination needs
- Building trust around sensitive personal information
- Identifying a sustainable business model

Long-term Vision:
Become the operating system for family caregiving coordination and eventually expand into broader home healthcare services.

# CareSync Product Proposal

## Product Problem
Families face significant challenges when coordinating care for elderly relatives, particularly when multiple family members or professional caregivers are involved. Current systems often result in fragmented information and miscommunication, leading to increased stress and potential oversight in care management. The CareSync platform aims to address these challenges by centralizing care coordination tools in one digital platform.

## Initial Wedge
The narrowest credible MVP wedge will focus on the most critical functionalities necessary for families to effectively coordinate care for elderly relatives. This will include scheduling medical appointments, medication reminders, and a shared family dashboard.

## First Target User
The first target users will be adults managing care for elderly parents, particularly those living in different cities from their relatives. This segment is likely to face the most complexity and could benefit significantly from streamlined communication and coordination tools.

## Existing Alternatives And Switching Trigger
Currently, families rely on fragmented solutions such as phone calls, messaging apps, and physical notes to manage care. Families would switch to CareSync primarily for its centralized management capabilities, improved visibility for all involved parties, and the ability to securely share medical documents – thus minimizing the risk of oversight.

## Core MVP Workflow
1. User sign-up and family member addition.
2. Creation of a shared family dashboard.
3. Scheduling of medical appointments and sending reminders.
4. Setting medication reminders with notifications.
5. Allocation and tracking of caregiving tasks among family members.

## In Scope
- User sign-up and family member management
- Shared family dashboard
- Appointment scheduling
- Medication reminders
- Task management features

## Out of Scope
- Integration with external health systems or electronic health records
- In-app messaging beyond task allocation
- Comprehensive resources for elderly user training
- Advanced features like video consultations

## Business Model Hypothesis
The primary revenue model will hypothesize that families will pay a subscription fee for enhanced features, such as additional storage for medical documents, advanced reminder functionalities, and premium support for onboarding elderly users.

## Critical Assumptions
- Families will perceive value in centralized caregiving tools and be willing to adopt a new platform.
- Users will be able to navigate the platform with a basic level of digital literacy.
- The solution will successfully build trust and ensure data privacy, meeting legal compliance in their respective regions.

## How To Test Quickly
- Conduct a user survey to validate willingness to pay among the target audience.
- Host focus groups to obtain feedback on proposed MVP features and usability.
- Run a concierge pilot with a select group of families to observe real-world usage patterns and pain points.

## Acceptance Criteria
- At least 60% of pilot users find the platform simplifies care coordination.
- Users report at least a 30% decrease in missed appointments and medication timings within the first month.
- Gathering feedback that shows at least 70% confidence in data security and privacy.

## Risks And Failure Modes
- High trust and privacy requirements could lead to fewer users being willing to adopt the platform if concerns are not adequately addressed.
- Low digital literacy among elderly users could hinder platform usability and adoption.
- Resistance from family members to change established communication habits.
- Potential legal and compliance issues regarding sensitive health information.
