# Evaluation CEO - CareSync Version 2

Sources used: `prd.md`, `architecture.md`, `architecture.pdf`, `gtm.md`.

## Final Decision: REVISE

## Global Note: 5/10

## Executive Verdict

CareSync v2 addresses a real coordination problem for families managing elderly care. The proposal is more disciplined than a broad care platform: appointment scheduling, medication reminders, task management, permissions, and concierge pilot.

It is still not investable as a full MVP build. The core blockers remain privacy/compliance, willingness to pay, and proof that families will switch from existing habits.

## Business Viability

The pain is real, especially for adult children coordinating care remotely. The wedge is sensible and the concierge pilot is the right next motion.

The proposal still has a switching problem. Families already use calls, messaging apps, calendars, and notes. CareSync must prove that centralization creates enough recurring value to overcome setup friction and multi-family-member adoption.

## Profitability Potential

The family subscription hypothesis is plausible but unproven. Premium support, advanced reminders, and document storage are mentioned, but no price, paid conversion target, CAC, support cost, compliance cost, or retention threshold is defined.

This cannot be treated as a business until willingness to pay is tested directly.

## Execution Complexity

The technical MVP is feasible, but sensitive-data handling raises the bar. The architecture and PDF correctly emphasize RBAC, encryption, monitoring, admin controls, compliance checks, and reminder reliability.

The weakness: document storage is still mentioned as a future/premium value lever while the core pilot does not yet prove appointment/reminder value. Keep the pilot data-minimal.

## Go-To-Market Realism

The GTM is realistic for learning: 15-20 families, concierge support, 3 interactions per week, qualitative feedback.

But it lacks a concrete recruitment channel. "Families managing care" is a segment, not an acquisition path. The proposal needs one source: caregiver association, employer caregiver group, senior center, clinic, or community.

## Competitive Risk

Competitive risk is meaningful: WhatsApp, shared calendars, notes, reminder apps, care portals, and agency tools. CareSync needs trust and workflow specificity to win.

## Major Risks

- Privacy concerns block adoption.
- Families do not switch from existing tools.
- Elderly users or relatives struggle with onboarding.
- Compliance costs exceed willingness to pay.
- Reminder failures break trust.
- Subscription conversion is weak.

## Final Recommendation

Revise. Approve only a controlled concierge pilot:

- one target geography or community channel
- 15-20 families
- minimal sensitive data collection
- role permissions from day one
- dashboard, appointments, medication reminders, and tasks only
- measure weekly active families, invited member activation, reminders used, tasks completed, stress reduction, retention, and willingness to pay

No full product build until privacy trust and paid value are validated.
