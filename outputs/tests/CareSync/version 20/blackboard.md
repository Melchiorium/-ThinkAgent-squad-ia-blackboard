## Project Brief

Project Name: CareSync

Pitch:
A digital platform that helps families coordinate care for elderly relatives by centralizing medical appointments, medication reminders, caregiving tasks, and communication between family members and professional caregivers.

Context:
Many families struggle to manage care for aging parents or relatives, especially when multiple siblings or caregivers are involved. Information is often fragmented across phone calls, messaging apps, handwritten notes, and hospital documents.

At the same time, professional caregivers and home assistance providers often lack visibility into family decisions and day-to-day coordination.

The platform aims to simplify elderly care coordination while reducing stress for families.

Target Users:
- Adults managing care for elderly parents
- Family members living in different cities
- Professional caregivers
- Home nurses
- Elderly care agencies

Potential Use Cases:
- Scheduling medical appointments
- Medication reminders
- Shared task management
- Emergency contact coordination
- Sharing medical documents
- Tracking caregiving responsibilities

Platform Capabilities:
- Shared family dashboard
- Calendar coordination
- Notifications/reminders
- Secure document storage
- Messaging system
- Permission management for family members and caregivers

Constraints:
- High trust and privacy expectations
- Sensitive medical-related information
- Elderly users may have low digital literacy
- Potential legal/data compliance issues depending on country

Challenges:
- Convincing families to adopt a new platform
- Balancing simplicity with complex coordination needs
- Building trust around sensitive personal information
- Identifying a sustainable business model

Long-term Vision:
Become the operating system for family caregiving coordination and eventually expand into broader home healthcare services.

# CareSync Product Proposal

## Product Problem
Families struggle to coordinate care for elderly relatives, often facing fragmented information and communication challenges that result in stress and inefficiencies. Current solutions fail to provide a centralized platform for managing appointments, medication, and communication between family members and caregivers.

## Initial Wedge
The initial MVP will focus on enabling families to coordinate medical appointments and medication reminders for elderly relatives, addressing a critical pain point with a straightforward solution.

## First Target User
Adults managing care for elderly parents who live in different cities, needing a simple way to centralize care coordination.

## Existing Alternatives And Switching Trigger
- **Existing Alternatives**: Phone calls, messaging apps, handwritten notes, separate calendar apps.
- **Switching Trigger**: Simplified and centralized care management that reduces communication errors and enhances visibility across family and caregiving networks.

## Core MVP Workflow
1. Users create an account and set up profiles for elderly relatives.
2. Users enter medical appointments and set reminders.
3. Users receive notifications for upcoming appointments and medication schedules.
4. Users can share tasks with family members involved in caregiving.

## In Scope
- Shared family dashboard
- Calendar coordination for medical appointments
- Medication reminders and notifications
- Basic messaging system for family communication
- User permission management for caregivers and family members

## Out of Scope
- Secure document storage (deferred until user engagement is proven)
- Emergency contact coordination (deferred)
- Advanced features like task management and sharing medical documents

## MVP Build Vs Pilot Operations
### Must Build Now
- Shared family dashboard
- Calendar coordination for medical appointments
- Medication reminders and notifications
- Basic messaging system
- User permission management

### Manual Or Operational During Pilot
- Onboarding support and training for families with low digital literacy
- Temporary data entry assistance (for initial setup and engagement)

### Deferred Until After Proof
- Secure document storage
- Emergency contact coordination
- Advanced task management features

## Business Model Hypothesis
The platform will operate on a subscription model for families and may offer premium features for professional caregivers and elderly care agencies.

## Critical Assumptions
- Families are willing to use a dedicated platform for coordinating care.
- Users trust the platform with sensitive medical information.
- Digital literacy barriers can be adequately addressed through onboarding support.

## How To Test Quickly
- Conduct interviews with target users to assess willingness to switch from existing solutions.
- Launch a pilot program with pre-identified users to gather feedback on usability and pain points.
- Measure engagement metrics (e.g., number of appointments tracked, reminders acknowledged).

## Acceptance Criteria
- At least 3 family members actively using the platform within the pilot.
- 80% of users report satisfaction with the ease of use.
- Successfully track and manage at least 10 medical appointments and medication reminders.

## Risks And Failure Modes
- Low adoption rate due to reluctance to switch from existing solutions.
- Privacy concerns leading to trust issues among users.
- Poor user experience for families with low digital literacy.
