## Go-To-Market Notes

- Primary launch motion: 6–8 week remote pilot with 2–3 real families to validate engagement, onboarding, and trust.
- MVP scope: privacy-first central hub with core features (dashboard, calendar/reminders, docs, messaging, permissions); guardrails to prevent scope creep.
- Onboarding and accessibility: design for low literacy and elderly users; plan initial language options.
- Monetization: freemium with paid tiers; test willingness to pay during/after pilot; explore elder-care agency partnerships for distribution.
- Pilot metrics: activation, first-week retention, feature usage; onboarding quality feedback; consent/permissions flows validated; basic privacy/compliance telemetry.
- Privacy and compliance focus: establish guardrails for cross-jurisdiction data handling; baseline encryption; incident response planning.
- Regulatory readiness: plan to align target launch regions with defined data ownership, consent, and access controls; identify owners and deadlines.
- GTM governance: lock the MVP scope and pilot plan to minimize scope creep; prepare for rapid iteration post-pilot based on validated learnings.

## Review Summary

- CareSync is scoped as a privacy-first MVP with core care-coordination features and a 6–8 week pilot for 2–3 real families.
- Open questions remain around MVP feature prioritization/scope confirmation, pricing thresholds, privacy/data residency across regions, and accessibility/language planning.
- Product readiness is LIMITED due to gaps in privacy, compliance, and onboarding design; requires regulatory readiness planning and a low-friction onboarding flow before broader rollout.
- The plan maintains a single, concrete primary launch motion (the pilot) with guardrails to prevent scope creep.

## Build Vs Pilot Operations

### Must Be Productized Now
- Web and mobile MVP app with core features: shared family dashboard, calendar, reminders, secure document storage, messaging, permissions.
- Elder care recipient profile with essential medical and contact details; emergency quick-contact access.
- Onboarding/invitation flow to create a care group and invite members; role-based permissions.
- Basic privacy controls, consent flow, and access controls; encryption in transit and at rest; incident response plan.
- Pilot instrumentation: activation, first-week retention, feature usage, and qualitative onboarding feedback.

### Can Stay Manual Or Operational During Pilot
- Manual recruitment and onboarding of 2–3 pilot families; manual data entry for elder profiles and caregiver contacts; pilot-level support channels.

### Deferred Until After Proof
- EHR/telehealth integrations; offline mode; multi-language support beyond MVP; global regulatory coverage; advanced analytics and optimization features.

## Critical Assumptions

- Families will adopt a centralized care coordination tool when privacy and simplicity are demonstrated.
- Users will keep caregiver/contact data updated and actively engage with reminders and tasks.
- Privacy controls and consent flows meet user expectations across jurisdictions.
- Freemium monetization is viable after initial adoption; elder-care agency partnerships can broaden distribution.

## Requested Changes

- Finalize MVP scope and feature prioritization by a defined date to prevent scope creep.
- Produce a regulatory readiness plan for target launch regions, with mitigations.
- Define a low-friction onboarding flow with accessibility considerations and initial language options.
- Clarify data residency, ownership, and consent management across jurisdictions.
- Outline a concrete pricing strategy and monetization thresholds to validate viability in pilot.

## Risks

- Privacy/data protection risks across multiple jurisdictions.
- Low user adoption due to perceived complexity or distrust.
- Compliance delays slowing broader rollout.
- Dependence on users maintaining accurate caregiver and contact data.
- Scope creep beyond MVP boundaries.

## Open Questions

- MVP feature prioritization and scope confirmation.
- Pricing strategy and monetization thresholds to validate viability.
- Privacy and data residency approach across potential launch regions.
- Plan for accessibility improvements and language support.

## Why This Could Fail Even With Good Execution

- Privacy or data protection incidents across regions erode trust and block rollout.
- Onboarding friction reduces adoption among non-tech-savvy elder caregivers.
- Regulatory delays or misalignment stall launch timelines.
- Data quality issues undermine perceived value.
- Monetization timing or value realization misaligned with user expectations.
