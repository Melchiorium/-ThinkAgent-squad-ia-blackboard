## Project Brief

Project Name: CareSync

Pitch:
A digital platform that helps families coordinate care for elderly relatives by centralizing medical appointments, medication reminders, caregiving tasks, and communication between family members and professional caregivers.

Context:
Many families struggle to manage care for aging parents or relatives, especially when multiple siblings or caregivers are involved. Information is often fragmented across phone calls, messaging apps, handwritten notes, and hospital documents.

At the same time, professional caregivers and home assistance providers often lack visibility into family decisions and day-to-day coordination.

The platform aims to simplify elderly care coordination while reducing stress for families.

Target Users:
- Adults managing care for elderly parents
- Family members living in different cities
- Professional caregivers
- Home nurses
- Elderly care agencies

Potential Use Cases:
- Scheduling medical appointments
- Medication reminders
- Shared task management
- Emergency contact coordination
- Sharing medical documents
- Tracking caregiving responsibilities

Platform Capabilities:
- Shared family dashboard
- Calendar coordination
- Notifications/reminders
- Secure document storage
- Messaging system
- Permission management for family members and caregivers

Constraints:
- High trust and privacy expectations
- Sensitive medical-related information
- Elderly users may have low digital literacy
- Potential legal/data compliance issues depending on country

Challenges:
- Convincing families to adopt a new platform
- Balancing simplicity with complex coordination needs
- Building trust around sensitive personal information
- Identifying a sustainable business model

Long-term Vision:
Become the operating system for family caregiving coordination and eventually expand into broader home healthcare services.

# CareSync MVP Product Proposal

## Product Problem
Families managing the care of elderly relatives often face fragmented communication and coordination. This leads to stress, confusion, and potential neglect of care tasks. Existing methods, such as phone calls, messaging apps, and handwritten notes, lack organization and transparency, which complicates collaboration among family members and caregivers.

## Initial Wedge
The narrowest credible MVP wedge is the shared family dashboard that focuses on scheduling medical appointments and medication reminders. This addresses the immediate coordination pain points families face.

## First Target User
Adults managing care for elderly parents, particularly those who live in different cities from their relatives. This group is likely motivated to adopt a centralized solution for better coordination and communication.

## Existing Alternatives And Switching Trigger
Currently, families use disparate tools like phone messaging, calendar apps, and paper notes. The switching trigger would be the convenience of a single platform that consolidates these functions, reducing the need for multiple communications and system management.

## Core MVP Workflow
1. Family members create a shared account.
2. Users input medical appointments and medication schedules.
3. Notifications are sent to remind users of upcoming appointments and medication times.
4. Users can share relevant documents securely within the platform.

## In Scope
- Shared family dashboard for appointment scheduling.
- Medication reminders.
- Basic notifications and alerts.
- User-friendly interface for non-tech-savvy users.

## Out of Scope
- Advanced task management features.
- In-depth professional caregiver integrations and complex workflows.
- Direct messaging between family members (limited to notifications).
- Third-party integrations at launch.

## Business Model Hypothesis
CareSync could use a subscription-based model for families, with a tier offering additional features for professional caregivers or home care agencies. Realistically, families would pay for a tool that simplifies care management and enhances communication.

## Critical Assumptions
- Families will find value in centralized care management.
- Users trust the platform with sensitive information and understand privacy measures.
- The app's usability is adequate for elderly users with low digital literacy.

## How To Test Quickly
Conduct user interviews and surveys with the target audience to validate their interest in the platform and willingness to pay for such a service. Create a prototype of the shared dashboard to gather early feedback on usability.

## Acceptance Criteria
- At least 75% of test users report that the interface is easy to use.
- Users can successfully schedule at least three appointments and receive reminders without technical issues during testing.
- Families express a clear preference for using the platform over their current coordination methods.

## Risks And Failure Modes
- Low adoption rates if users do not see immediate benefits.
- Security and privacy concerns could lead to distrust.
- Potential challenges with elderly users adapting to the digital platform.
