## Project Brief

Project Name: CareSync

Pitch:
A digital platform that helps families coordinate care for elderly relatives by centralizing medical appointments, medication reminders, caregiving tasks, and communication between family members and professional caregivers.

Context:
Many families struggle to manage care for aging parents or relatives, especially when multiple siblings or caregivers are involved. Information is often fragmented across phone calls, messaging apps, handwritten notes, and hospital documents.

At the same time, professional caregivers and home assistance providers often lack visibility into family decisions and day-to-day coordination.

The platform aims to simplify elderly care coordination while reducing stress for families.

Target Users:
- Adults managing care for elderly parents
- Family members living in different cities
- Professional caregivers
- Home nurses
- Elderly care agencies

Potential Use Cases:
- Scheduling medical appointments
- Medication reminders
- Shared task management
- Emergency contact coordination
- Sharing medical documents
- Tracking caregiving responsibilities

Platform Capabilities:
- Shared family dashboard
- Calendar coordination
- Notifications/reminders
- Secure document storage
- Messaging system
- Permission management for family members and caregivers

Constraints:
- High trust and privacy expectations
- Sensitive medical-related information
- Elderly users may have low digital literacy
- Potential legal/data compliance issues depending on country

Challenges:
- Convincing families to adopt a new platform
- Balancing simplicity with complex coordination needs
- Building trust around sensitive personal information
- Identifying a sustainable business model

Long-term Vision:
Become the operating system for family caregiving coordination and eventually expand into broader home healthcare services.

# CareSync MVP Product Proposal

## Product Problem
Families often face struggles in coordinating care for elderly relatives due to fragmented information across various channels, leading to miscommunication, missed appointments, and increased stress. Professional caregivers also lack visibility into family dynamics and daily care coordination.

## Initial Wedge
The narrowest credible MVP wedge will focus on **medical appointment scheduling and medication reminders**. This is essential to demonstrate the value of centralized care management while addressing critical pain points.

## First Target User
The primary target user for the first launch will be **adults aged 30-50 managing care for elderly parents**, particularly those who live away from their parents and face challenges in coordinating care due to distance.

## Existing Alternatives And Switching Trigger
Existing alternatives include:
- Phone calls and messaging apps for coordination.
- Shared calendars among family members.
- Paper documents for medical information.

The switching trigger will be the **need for a centralized solution that reduces friction in communication**, offers reminders, and provides a shared view of caregiving responsibilities to ensure nothing falls through the cracks.

## Core MVP Workflow
1. **User Onboarding**: Setting up family accounts and inviting members.
2. **Scheduling Medical Appointments**: Users can input appointments with reminders.
3. **Medication Reminders**: Users can enter medications for elderly relatives and receive alerts for dosages.

## In Scope
- Shared family dashboard.
- Calendar coordination for medical appointments.
- Notifications and reminders for medications.
- User onboarding and account management.

## Out of Scope
- Comprehensive document sharing for all medical information.
- In-depth messaging features beyond appointment reminders.
- Complex permissions management until proven necessity.
- Features for professional caregivers beyond viewing timers for appointments.

## Business Model Hypothesis
The business model will initially hinge on a **subscription fee** per family, offering features that enhance coordination. Potential for future partnerships with healthcare providers could also create additional revenue streams.

## Critical Assumptions
- Families will find value in having a centralized digital platform for coordination.
- Users are willing to pay for improved management of elderly care.
- Users can navigate the platform with basic instructions despite low digital literacy.

## How To Test Quickly
- Conduct interviews or surveys with target users to validate the need for scheduling and medication reminders.
- Create a landing page with a sign-up form to gauge interest and collect emails for early feedback.

## Acceptance Criteria
- At least 50 engaged users during the beta phase.
- Average of 75% adherence to medication reminders in test families.
- At least 5 confirmed scheduled appointments through the platform.

## Risks And Failure Modes
- Users may not adopt the platform due to resistance to change or complexity.
- Concerns about privacy and trust with sensitive information.
- Technical issues affecting reminder notifications could reduce reliability.
