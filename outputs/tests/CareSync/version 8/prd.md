# CareSync Product Proposal

## Product Problem
Families face significant challenges in coordinating care for elderly relatives due to fragmented information management. With responsibilities often divided among multiple siblings or caregivers, it leads to stress and inefficiency. There is a need for a centralized digital platform to streamline communication and organization among family members and professional caregivers.

## Initial Wedge
Centralized medical appointment scheduling and medication reminders for families coordinating the care of elderly parents.

## First Target User
Adults managing care for elderly parents, particularly those with siblings or caregivers situated in different regions.

## Existing Alternatives And Switching Trigger
Current alternatives include phone calls, messaging apps, handwritten notes, and various hospital documents, all lacking integration. Families may switch to CareSync for improved organization, reduced stress, and enhanced visibility into care coordination.

## Core MVP Workflow
1. Centralized calendar for scheduling medical appointments.
2. Medication reminders (push notifications).
3. Shared task management feature allowing family members to allocate caregiving tasks.

## In Scope
- Shared family dashboard for visibility of tasks and appointments.
- Calendar coordination with reminders.
- Basic messaging system for communication among family members and caregivers.
- Onboarding features tailored for elderly users, focusing on simplicity and support (including a simple educational guide).

## Out of Scope
- Advanced features like secure document storage and complex permission management.
- Integration with external medical systems or insurance providers.
- Comprehensive user onboarding features beyond basic education on using the platform.

## Business Model Hypothesis
CareSync may charge a subscription fee to families or caregivers for premium features or a tiered pricing model based on the number of users in a family group.

## Critical Assumptions
- Families will find value in simplifying care coordination and be willing to pay for a centralized solution.
- Users will have access to smartphones or tablets, enabling them to utilize the digital platform.
- High user trust in the platform's privacy and security measures regarding sensitive medical data.

## How To Test Quickly
- Conduct user interviews and surveys with potential users to validate the core features (scheduling, reminders).
- Perform usability testing with a simplified version of the dashboard.
- Prototype the core workflow and present it to target users for feedback.

## Acceptance Criteria
- At least 70% of users report that the platform significantly reduces stress related to coordinating care.
- Successful completion of at least 5 test user iterations with feedback integrated into the core features.
- Zero security breaches and strong user feedback on trust regarding privacy and information handling.
- Effective onboarding perceived by elderly users as easy and supportive.
- A clear decision point on proceeding, revising, or rejecting the MVP based on user engagement metrics.

## Risks And Failure Modes
- Resistance from families to adopt a new platform.
- Potential data privacy and compliance issues that could undermine trust.
- Low engagement levels among elderly users due to digital literacy challenges.
- Misalignment of the platform’s features with actual user needs.

Status: LIMITED

## Recommendation
Proceed with a concierge pilot to validate the critical assumptions. Engage with a small group of target users to closely observe behaviors and capture feedback on the MVP features. Measure user engagement through clear success metrics, allowing for the decision to proceed, revise, or reject the MVP based on user feedback.
