## Project Brief

Project Name: CareSync

Pitch:
A digital platform that helps families coordinate care for elderly relatives by centralizing medical appointments, medication reminders, caregiving tasks, and communication between family members and professional caregivers.

Context:
Many families struggle to manage care for aging parents or relatives, especially when multiple siblings or caregivers are involved. Information is often fragmented across phone calls, messaging apps, handwritten notes, and hospital documents.

At the same time, professional caregivers and home assistance providers often lack visibility into family decisions and day-to-day coordination.

The platform aims to simplify elderly care coordination while reducing stress for families.

Target Users:
- Adults managing care for elderly parents
- Family members living in different cities
- Professional caregivers
- Home nurses
- Elderly care agencies

Potential Use Cases:
- Scheduling medical appointments
- Medication reminders
- Shared task management
- Emergency contact coordination
- Sharing medical documents
- Tracking caregiving responsibilities

Platform Capabilities:
- Shared family dashboard
- Calendar coordination
- Notifications/reminders
- Secure document storage
- Messaging system
- Permission management for family members and caregivers

Constraints:
- High trust and privacy expectations
- Sensitive medical-related information
- Elderly users may have low digital literacy
- Potential legal/data compliance issues depending on country

Challenges:
- Convincing families to adopt a new platform
- Balancing simplicity with complex coordination needs
- Building trust around sensitive personal information
- Identifying a sustainable business model

Long-term Vision:
Become the operating system for family caregiving coordination and eventually expand into broader home healthcare services.

# CareSync MVP Proposal

## Product Problem
Families struggle with fragmented communication and coordination when managing care for elderly relatives. Current solutions are often inadequate, leading to stress, missed appointments, and communication breakdowns between family members and professional caregivers.

## Initial Wedge
Target the specific need for centralized task management among families with elderly relatives, particularly focusing on medical appointment scheduling and medication reminders.

## First Target User
Adult children managing care for their aging parents, especially those who do not live in the same city as their parents.

## Existing Alternatives And Switching Trigger
Current alternatives include phone calls, messaging apps (e.g., WhatsApp, text), paper schedules, and individual caregiver software. Users may switch to CareSync for a single, integrated platform that simplifies coordination and provides transparency among family members and caregivers.

## Core MVP Workflow
1. Users can create a shared family dashboard.
2. Users can schedule and manage medical appointments.
3. Users receive medication reminders.
4. Users can communicate through a messaging system with family and caregivers.

## In Scope
- Shared family dashboard
- Calendar coordination for medical appointments
- Medication reminders
- Basic messaging system for family and caregivers

## Out of Scope
- Emergency contact coordination
- Sharing medical documents
- Advanced permission management
- Integration with healthcare systems or external calendars

## Business Model Hypothesis
CareSync will operate on a subscription model targeting families, providing different tiers for family accounts and professional caregivers with potential partnerships with elderly care agencies.

## Critical Assumptions
- Families are willing to adopt a new digital solution for elderly care coordination.
- The platform effectively improves coordination and reduces stress for families.
- Users find value in having appointment scheduling and medication management in one platform.

## How To Test Quickly
Conduct user interviews and surveys with target users to validate their willingness to adopt a new solution and identify their biggest pain points. Develop a simple prototype to test with family members managing elderly relatives.

## Acceptance Criteria
- Minimum of 3 families using the platform for managing care.
- At least 80% of users should find the platform simplifies their caregiving responsibilities, based on user feedback.
- Schedule and remind users of a minimum of 10 medical appointments without significant issues.

## Risks And Failure Modes
- Families may not trust the platform with sensitive medical information.
- The interface may not be simple enough for elderly users.
- Competition may provide similar services, making user acquisition difficult.
