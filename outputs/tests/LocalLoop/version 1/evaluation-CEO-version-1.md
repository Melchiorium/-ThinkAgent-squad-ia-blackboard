# Evaluation CEO - LocalLoop Version 1

Sources used: `prd.md`, `architecture.md`, `architecture.pdf`, `gtm.md`.

## Final Decision: REVISE

## Global Note: 4/10

## Executive Verdict

LocalLoop targets a real desire to support independent local businesses, but the proposal underestimates how hard it is to build a two-sided local marketplace against Google Maps, Yelp, Instagram, TikTok, loyalty apps, and local newsletters.

The idea is testable, but not investable at the current level of proof.

## Business Viability

The user problem is understandable, but not urgent. Young urban professionals may like curated local recommendations, yet they already have many discovery channels.

The merchant problem is real, but small businesses are skeptical buyers with limited budget. The proposal needs proof that merchants will pay for visibility and promotions before a marketplace build.

## Profitability Potential

The subscription model for local businesses is weak without evidence. Small merchants often resist new monthly software unless ROI is obvious.

The proposal lacks pricing, merchant ROI assumptions, churn expectations, sales cost, and proof that promotions drive measurable foot traffic.

## Execution Complexity

The technical build is manageable: profiles, merchant onboarding, geolocation, recommendations, promotions, and reviews.

The architecture PDF is too generic. It shows no explicit flows and no operations/control points, while the markdown correctly states that merchant performance, promotion approval, and data quality require admin tooling.

## Go-To-Market Realism

Direct merchant outreach is the right first motion. Onboarding 10 businesses in one neighborhood is a reasonable pilot target.

The user acquisition side remains thin. The product cannot be tested only with signups; it must prove repeated discovery, deal redemption, and merchant-perceived value.

## Competitive Risk

Competitive risk is high. Google Maps, Yelp, social media, influencers, local blogs, and neighborhood groups already capture discovery behavior.

Personalization and “support local” messaging are not enough unless the offers are exclusive and useful.

## Major Risks

- Merchants do not pay.
- Users do not return after initial curiosity.
- Recommendations are poor due to sparse data.
- Promotions are low quality or irrelevant.
- Manual merchant onboarding becomes costly.
- Existing platforms copy any successful local promotion mechanic.

## Final Recommendation

Revise.

Do not build a full marketplace yet. Run a single-neighborhood concierge test with manually curated deals and prove merchant ROI through redemptions before investing in recommendation infrastructure.
