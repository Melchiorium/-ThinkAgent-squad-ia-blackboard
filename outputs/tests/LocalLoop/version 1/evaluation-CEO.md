# CEO Evaluation - LocalLoop - Version 1

## 1. Business viability
The use case is concrete: nearby independent coffee/lunch discovery with immediate offer and repeat-visit loop.  
The concept is viable only in a tightly bounded micro-market with curated supply density. Without that, it becomes another low-value local directory.

## 2. Profitability potential
Merchant subscription potential is plausible if incremental foot traffic is measurable.  
Short-term profitability is limited because onboarding, curation, and dispute handling are manually intensive. Merchant willingness to pay is not yet proven.

## 3. Execution complexity
Technical scope is moderate and correctly simplified (single redemption model, basic analytics, admin controls).  
Execution risk is primarily operational reliability at the point of redemption; in-store friction can destroy trust quickly.

## 4. Go-to-market realism
Merchant-first, founder-led GTM in one Paris micro-market is realistic and appropriate.  
GTM realism depends on strict sequencing: supply quality first, then localized demand. Broad city expansion before proof would be a major error.

## 5. Competitive risk
Competitive pressure is strong from Google Maps, Instagram, delivery platforms, and existing habits.  
Defensibility is low unless LocalLoop creates repeat behavior and clear merchant ROI beyond generic discovery tools.

## Major risks
- Thin or stale local supply making the feed feel empty
- Redemption friction at checkout causing failed trust moments
- Low incremental value versus incumbent discovery channels
- Merchants churn if ROI is ambiguous
- Product perceived as coupon directory, not habit loop

## 6. Final investment decision
**REVISE**

## 7. Global note
**6/10**

## Final recommendation
Proceed only with a strict concierge pilot in one named micro-market and one category pair.  
Approve broader investment only after proving repeat visits, reliable redemption operations, and merchant retention tied to measurable incremental traffic.
