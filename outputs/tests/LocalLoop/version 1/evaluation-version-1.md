# Evaluation Report - LocalLoop Version 1

Sources used: `prd.md`, `architecture.md`, `architecture.pdf`, `gtm.md`.

## Final Decision: REVISE

## Global Note: 4/10

## Executive Verdict

LocalLoop targets a real local discovery problem, but the proposal still looks too much like another local directory with deals. The project needs proof that consumers will use it repeatedly and that merchants will pay for measurable ROI.

The architecture PDF is especially weak: it shows no explicit flow captured and no admin/ops control points, while the Markdown claims merchant dashboards, feedback loops, and internal monitoring. That inconsistency weakens readiness.

## Business Viability

Consumers may want curated local recommendations, but they already use Google Maps, Yelp, Instagram, TikTok, local groups, and word of mouth.

The target user is too broad: young urban professionals aged 25-35. The launch should focus on one neighborhood and one merchant category.

The merchant pain is plausible, but "visibility" is not enough. LocalLoop must prove redemptions, repeat visits, or customer acquisition ROI.

## Profitability Potential

The merchant subscription model is plausible but unproven.

Missing:

- merchant price point
- paid conversion rate
- merchant acquisition cost
- redemption targets
- repeat visit targets
- churn assumptions
- operational cost to onboard and maintain merchant data

Small merchants will not pay for another profile unless the platform drives measurable foot traffic.

## Execution Complexity

The MVP is technically feasible, but still too broad: geolocation, personalization, merchant profiles, promotions, feedback/reviews, loyalty incentives.

Manual curation is correctly mentioned and should dominate the pilot. A full recommendation engine is premature.

The PDF's lack of explicit flows and ops controls is a red flag for a marketplace-like local product where stale merchant data and poor offers can quickly destroy trust.

## Go-To-Market Realism

The GTM correctly secures merchants first and proposes direct outreach.

However, assumptions remain weak. Onboarding 10 merchants is plausible, but 10 merchants are not enough unless concentrated in one neighborhood and one use case. A 5% merchant interest conversion means the team may need significant outreach just to seed a weak supply base.

The consumer acquisition plan is underdeveloped beyond grassroots marketing.

## Competitive Risk

Competitive risk is high. LocalLoop competes with Google Maps, Yelp, Groupon, Instagram, TikTok, local newsletters, loyalty tools, and merchant-owned channels.

The product has no moat unless it secures exclusive local offers and measurable redemption behavior.

## Major Risks

- Consumers do not need another local discovery app.
- Merchants do not pay without clear ROI.
- Deals are not exclusive or compelling.
- Merchant data becomes stale.
- Recommendations are not meaningfully personalized.
- Local density is too weak.
- Reviews and loyalty add complexity before validation.

## Final Recommendation

Revise before full build.

Pilot conditions:

- one neighborhood
- one merchant category
- 10-15 merchants
- manually curated recommendations
- simple redemption code instead of full loyalty
- no review system unless needed for pilot trust
- one consumer acquisition channel
- measure offer views, redemptions, repeat redemptions, merchant satisfaction, merchant willingness to pay, and weekly user retention

LocalLoop needs proof of merchant ROI and repeat consumer usage before it deserves investment.
