## Project Brief

Project Name: LocalLoop

Pitch:
A mobile application that helps people discover local independent businesses through personalized recommendations, neighborhood deals, and loyalty rewards.

Context:
Many consumers want to support local businesses but often default to large platforms because discovering small local stores, restaurants, services, and events is fragmented and inconvenient.

At the same time, independent businesses struggle with visibility and customer retention but often lack the budget or expertise to run digital marketing campaigns.

The platform helps consumers discover relevant local businesses while giving small businesses simple tools to attract and retain nearby customers.

Target Users:
- Urban residents
- Young professionals
- Families
- Tourists
- Independent local businesses

Potential Use Cases:
- Discover nearby coffee shops
- Find local restaurants with promotions
- Discover niche stores
- Access loyalty rewards
- Local event discovery
- Neighborhood recommendations

Platform Capabilities:
- Personalized recommendations
- Geolocation
- Merchant profiles
- Promotions/offers
- Loyalty system
- Reviews
- Local event feed

Constraints:
- Limited initial budget
- Need enough local business supply in one city before expanding
- Must avoid competing directly with massive horizontal platforms

Challenges:
- Convincing local businesses to join
- Maintaining recommendation quality
- Avoiding platform saturation with irrelevant offers

Long-term Vision:
Become the default discovery platform for local commerce and neighborhood experiences.

# LocalLoop MVP Product Proposal

## Product Problem
Consumers find it difficult to discover and support local independent businesses due to a fragmented and inconvenient search experience, often resulting in reliance on large platforms. Independent businesses struggle with visibility and retention, lacking the necessary tools for effective digital marketing.

## Initial Wedge
The initial wedge will focus on personalized local business discovery through recommendations based on user preferences and location, offering neighborhood deals and loyalty rewards.

## First Target User
Urban residents aged 25-40, particularly young professionals and families who are interested in supporting local businesses and exploring their neighborhoods.

## Existing Alternatives And Switching Trigger
Current alternatives include general platforms like Yelp or Google Maps, but they lack personalization for local businesses. The switching trigger will be offering better-targeted local recommendations and loyalty rewards that enhance the consumer's experience and benefit local businesses.

## Core MVP Workflow
1. User signs up and creates a profile with preferences.
2. User inputs their location for geolocation services.
3. Receive personalized recommendations for nearby independent businesses.
4. Access promotions and loyalty rewards offered by these businesses.
5. Provide reviews and feedback on their experiences.

## In Scope
- User sign-up and profile creation
- Geolocation capabilities
- Personalized business recommendations
- Merchant profiles with promotions and rewards
- Review system for users to rate their experiences

## Out Of Scope
- Complex event discovery features
- Integration of large-scale digital marketing tools
- Comprehensive analytics and reporting features for businesses
- User-generated content beyond reviews (e.g., photos, extensive posts)

## Business Model Hypothesis
Local businesses will pay a subscription fee to get visibility on the platform, plus additional fees for promotional features (e.g., boosted visibility or special offers). Consumers will use the app for free but will benefit from loyalty rewards.

## Critical Assumptions
1. Urban residents prefer discovering local businesses over large platforms.
2. Local businesses are willing to pay for improved visibility.
3. Personalized recommendations will attract users to the platform and encourage use.
4. Users will utilize loyalty rewards to enhance repeat visits.

## How To Test Quickly
- Conduct surveys and interviews with potential users to validate interest in the app.
- Pilot the app with a select group of local businesses to measure user engagement and retention.
- Use A/B testing on recommendation algorithms to refine the personalization process.

## Acceptance Criteria
- At least 100 local businesses onboarded in the first city.
- 1,000 app downloads with a 20% active user rate within the first month post-launch.
- Gather feedback with an average user rating of at least 4 out of 5 stars on loyalty features.

## Risks And Failure Modes
- Low user adoption could indicate a mismatch in expectations or insufficient marketing.
- Local businesses may not see a return on investment, leading to high churn rates.
- Maintaining the quality of recommendations could be challenging with increasing business participation.
