# LocalLoop MVP Product Proposal

## Product Problem
Consumers struggle to discover local independent businesses amidst the dominance of large platforms. Simultaneously, small businesses lack visibility and affordable tools to engage with nearby consumers effectively.

## Initial Wedge
Focus on urban residents seeking neighborhood coffee shops and restaurants with promotional offers while initially engaging a limited set of local independent businesses.

## First Target User
Urban residents, particularly young professionals and families looking for local dining and shopping options.

## Existing Alternatives And Switching Trigger
- Alternatives: Yelp, Google Maps, and large food delivery platforms that offer broad visibility but lack personalization for local businesses.
- Switching Trigger: LocalLoop must provide a more tailored discovery experience focused on personalization and loyalty rewards that these alternatives do not emphasize.

## Core MVP Workflow
1. Users install the LocalLoop app.
2. Users input location and preferences.
3. The app recommends a list of local independent businesses with current promotions.
4. Users can view merchant profiles, including reviews and loyalty offer details.
5. Users engage with selected businesses and redeem promotions.

## In Scope
- Personalized recommendations based on user location and preferences
- Geolocation feature to detect nearby businesses
- Basic merchant profiles with essential business information and current promotions
- Simple loyalty system for users who make purchases
- User reviews feature for feedback on businesses
- Streamlined onboarding path for initial users to facilitate easy app adoption

## Out of Scope
- Evaluation of events or community activities beyond initial business offerings
- Detailed analytics tools for businesses (e.g., advanced marketing features)
- Nationwide expansion beyond the initial city
- Extensive digital marketing services for businesses

## MVP Build Vs Pilot Operations
### Must Build Now
- Personalized recommendations
- Geolocation
- Merchant profiles
- Promotions/offers
- Simple loyalty system
- Streamlined user onboarding path

### Manual Or Operational During Pilot
- Direct outreach to local businesses for onboarding
- Manual validation of promotions and reviews
- Customer support for users navigating app features

### Deferred Until After Proof
- Advanced business tools and analytics
- Comprehensive rewards system enhancements
- Feature improvements based on user feedback

## Business Model Hypothesis
LocalLoop will monetize through a subscription model for businesses and potentially a small transaction fee on rewards redeemed by users.

## Critical Assumptions
- Urban residents will prefer a more personalized app over general search platforms.
- Local businesses will be willing to engage with the app if it proves beneficial for customer acquisition and retention.
- The existence of sufficient independent businesses in the chosen pilot city to validate the concept.

## How To Test Quickly
- Conduct qualitative user interviews with the target audience to refine user preferences and overall usability.
- Run a pilot with a handful of local businesses to test user engagement with promotions.
- Measure user retention and feedback on business interactions within the app.

## Acceptance Criteria
- Users consistently find relevant local businesses within their proximity.
- A minimum of 10 local businesses participate in the pilot, with at least 30% offering promotions.
- At least 70% of users provide positive feedback regarding the recommendations and ease of app use.
- Success metrics will include user engagement rates and retention metrics during the pilot.

## Risks And Failure Modes
- Low engagement from target users due to insufficient app features or poor UX.
- Difficulty onboarding enough local businesses to provide valuable content.
- Ineffective promotion leading to user attrition and a lack of loyalty adoption.

## Product Readiness
Status: LIMITED

Blocking Gaps:
- Insufficient validation of local business interest and willingness to engage.
- Lack of comprehensive feedback from users regarding app usability.

Required Improvements:
- Enhance the onboarding strategy for users [onboarding]
- Conduct user testing for validating key features [user_testing]

## Recommendation
Proceed with development focused on the initial wedge, enhance the onboarding strategy for users, and strategically onboard local businesses. Initiate user feedback sessions to validate the core offering and make necessary adjustments to the MVP for effective launch.
