## Project Brief

Project Name: LocalLoop

Pitch:
A mobile application that helps people discover local independent businesses through personalized recommendations, neighborhood deals, and loyalty rewards.

Context:
Many consumers want to support local businesses but often default to large platforms because discovering small local stores, restaurants, services, and events is fragmented and inconvenient.

At the same time, independent businesses struggle with visibility and customer retention but often lack the budget or expertise to run digital marketing campaigns.

The platform helps consumers discover relevant local businesses while giving small businesses simple tools to attract and retain nearby customers.

Target Users:
- Urban residents
- Young professionals
- Families
- Tourists
- Independent local businesses

Potential Use Cases:
- Discover nearby coffee shops
- Find local restaurants with promotions
- Discover niche stores
- Access loyalty rewards
- Local event discovery
- Neighborhood recommendations

Platform Capabilities:
- Personalized recommendations
- Geolocation
- Merchant profiles
- Promotions/offers
- Loyalty system
- Reviews
- Local event feed

Constraints:
- Limited initial budget
- Need enough local business supply in one city before expanding
- Must avoid competing directly with massive horizontal platforms

Challenges:
- Convincing local businesses to join
- Maintaining recommendation quality
- Avoiding platform saturation with irrelevant offers

Long-term Vision:
Become the default discovery platform for local commerce and neighborhood experiences.

# LocalLoop Product Proposal

## Product Problem
- Consumers struggle to discover and support local independent businesses due to fragmented information.
- Independent businesses lack visibility and affordable marketing tools, leading to difficulty in attracting and retaining customers.

## Initial Wedge
- Focus on urban residents seeking personalized recommendations for nearby coffee shops and restaurants with promotions.

## First Target User
- Urban residents, particularly young professionals aged 25-35 who are tech-savvy and inclined to support local businesses.

## Existing Alternatives And Switching Trigger
- Alternatives include large platforms like Yelp, Google Maps, and social media.
- Switching trigger: Ease of discovering curated, relevant local offers that support community engagement and loyalty.

## Core MVP Workflow
1. User downloads the app and creates a profile.
2. User enters preferences for types of businesses (e.g., coffee shops, restaurants).
3. The app uses geolocation to recommend personalized businesses with current promotions.
4. Users can view merchant profiles, redeem offers, and collect loyalty rewards.
5. Users provide reviews for businesses, enhancing recommendation quality.

## In Scope
- User registration and profile creation.
- Geolocation-based personalized recommendations.
- Merchant profiles showcasing promotions.
- Simple loyalty rewards system (e.g., stamp cards).
- Basic review system for user feedback.

## Out of Scope
- Advanced features such as in-app payments, detailed analytics for businesses, and full local event integration.
- Comprehensive marketing campaigns for businesses.
- Features for tourists or families will be deferred for future versions.

## MVP Build Vs Pilot Operations
### Must Build Now
- User registration and user profile.
- Geolocation services for personalized recommendations.
- Merchant promotion displays and basic loyalty feature.

### Manual Or Operational During Pilot
- Initial outreach and onboarding for local businesses will require manual management.
- Customer service support to ensure usability and feedback collection.

### Deferred Until After Proof
- Enhanced analytics for businesses.
- Full strength local event discovery and integration.
- Personalization for families and tourists.

## Business Model Hypothesis
- Revenue through partnerships with local businesses (subscription or commission-based model) for visibility and loyalty reward programs.

## Critical Assumptions
- Users will prefer a dedicated app for local business discovery over existing platforms.
- Local businesses will see value in joining the platform without heavy investment.
- The target user demographic is willing to change behavior to support local businesses.

## How To Test Quickly
- Launch MVP in one urban area with a small set of local businesses and measure user engagement and retention.
- Collect qualitative feedback from users and businesses during initial launches.

## Acceptance Criteria
- At least 100 user registrations within the first month.
- Minimum of 5 local businesses onboarded with live promotions.
- 75% of users redeem promotions and provide feedback.

## Risks And Failure Modes
- Low user adoption due to competition from established platforms.
- Difficulty onboarding sufficient local businesses.
- Poor recommendation relevancy leading to user dissatisfaction.
