# LocalLoop Product Proposal

## Product Problem
- Consumers struggle to discover and support local independent businesses due to fragmented information.
- Independent businesses lack visibility and affordable marketing tools, leading to difficulty in attracting and retaining customers.

## Initial Wedge
- Focus on urban residents seeking personalized recommendations for nearby coffee shops and restaurants with promotions.

## First Target User
- Urban residents, particularly young professionals aged 25-35 who are tech-savvy and inclined to support local businesses.

## Existing Alternatives And Switching Trigger
- Alternatives include large platforms like Yelp, Google Maps, and social media.
- Switching trigger: Ease of discovering curated, relevant local offers that support community engagement and loyalty.

## Core MVP Workflow
1. User downloads the app and creates a profile.
2. User enters preferences for types of businesses (e.g., coffee shops, restaurants).
3. The app uses geolocation to recommend personalized businesses with current promotions.
4. Users can view merchant profiles and redeem offers.

## In Scope
- User registration and profile creation.
- Geolocation-based personalized recommendations.
- Merchant profiles showcasing promotions.

## Out of Scope
- Advanced features such as loyalty rewards, detailed analytics for businesses, and full local event integration.
- Comprehensive marketing campaigns for businesses.
- Features for tourists or families will be deferred for future versions.

## MVP Build Vs Pilot Operations
### Must Build Now
- User registration and user profile.
- Geolocation services for personalized recommendations.
- Merchant promotion displays.

### Manual Or Operational During Pilot
- Initial outreach and onboarding for local businesses will require manual management.
- Customer service support to ensure usability and feedback collection.

### Deferred Until After Proof
- Enhanced analytics for businesses.
- Full strength local event discovery and integration.
- Personalization for families and tourists.

## Business Model Hypothesis
- Revenue through partnerships with local businesses (subscription or commission-based model) for visibility and promotions.

## Critical Assumptions
- Users will prefer a dedicated app for local business discovery over existing platforms.
- Local businesses will see value in joining the platform without heavy investment.
- The target user demographic is willing to change behavior to support local businesses.

## How To Test Quickly
- Launch MVP in one urban area with a small set of local businesses and measure user engagement.
- Collect qualitative feedback from users and businesses during initial launches.

## Acceptance Criteria
- At least 100 user registrations within the first month.
- Minimum of 5 local businesses onboarded with live promotions.

## Risks And Failure Modes
- Low user adoption due to competition from established platforms.
- Difficulty onboarding sufficient local businesses.
- Poor recommendation relevancy leading to user dissatisfaction.

## Product Readiness
Status: LIMITED

Blocking Gaps:
- Sufficient local business interest and engagement.

Required Improvements:
- Develop effective outreach strategies for local businesses. [demand_validation]

## Recommendation
Proceed with MVP development focusing on essential features that foster user engagement and onboarding with local businesses while closely monitoring feedback for iterative improvements.
