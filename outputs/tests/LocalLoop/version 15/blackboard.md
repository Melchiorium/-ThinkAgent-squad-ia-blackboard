## Project Brief

Project Name: LocalLoop

Pitch:
A mobile application that helps people discover local independent businesses through personalized recommendations, neighborhood deals, and loyalty rewards.

Context:
Many consumers want to support local businesses but often default to large platforms because discovering small local stores, restaurants, services, and events is fragmented and inconvenient.

At the same time, independent businesses struggle with visibility and customer retention but often lack the budget or expertise to run digital marketing campaigns.

The platform helps consumers discover relevant local businesses while giving small businesses simple tools to attract and retain nearby customers.

Target Users:
- Urban residents
- Young professionals
- Families
- Tourists
- Independent local businesses

Potential Use Cases:
- Discover nearby coffee shops
- Find local restaurants with promotions
- Discover niche stores
- Access loyalty rewards
- Local event discovery
- Neighborhood recommendations

Platform Capabilities:
- Personalized recommendations
- Geolocation
- Merchant profiles
- Promotions/offers
- Loyalty system
- Reviews
- Local event feed

Constraints:
- Limited initial budget
- Need enough local business supply in one city before expanding
- Must avoid competing directly with massive horizontal platforms

Challenges:
- Convincing local businesses to join
- Maintaining recommendation quality
- Avoiding platform saturation with irrelevant offers

Long-term Vision:
Become the default discovery platform for local commerce and neighborhood experiences.

# LocalLoop MVP Proposal

## Product Problem
Consumers struggle to find and engage with local independent businesses, often defaulting to larger platforms. Independent businesses lack visibility and efficient marketing tools to connect with their community, impacting customer retention and engagement.

## Initial Wedge
Enable urban residents to discover local independent coffee shops and restaurants through personalized recommendations and promotions, helping support local economies while providing easily accessible deals.

## First Target User
Urban residents aged 25-40, particularly young professionals and families, who are interested in supporting local businesses and seeking convenience in discovering neighborhood options.

## Existing Alternatives And Switching Trigger
- **Alternatives**: Google Maps, Yelp, Facebook local groups, TripAdvisor.
- **Switching Trigger**: A seamless user experience that provides better, personalized local business recommendations and exclusive neighborhood deals unavailable on broader platforms.

## Core MVP Workflow
1. User signs up and provides preferences (type of business, interests).
2. User sees personalized home screen featuring local independent coffee shops and restaurants.
3. Users can view promotions available from these businesses.
4. Users can navigate to selected businesses, access loyalty rewards, and leave reviews.

## In Scope
- User registration and profile setup.
- Personalized recommendations engine for coffee shops and restaurants based on user preferences.
- Geolocation to show nearby businesses.
- Merchant profiles with basic information and offers.
- Loyalty system to track user rewards.
- Access to user-generated reviews for trust-building.

## Out of Scope
- Events feed and promotions from non-coffee shop/restaurant businesses.
- Detailed analytics for businesses.
- Advanced features like social sharing or community forums.
- Integration with third-party payment systems.

## MVP Build Vs Pilot Operations
### Must Build Now
- User registration and profile setup.
- Personalized recommendations engine.
- Geolocation functionality.
- Basic merchant profiles.
- Loyalty rewards tracking.

### Manual Or Operational During Pilot
- Outreach to local businesses for early partnerships.
- Manual tracking of loyalty rewards before full integration.

### Deferred Until After Proof
- Expansion of categories beyond coffee shops and restaurants.
- Full-fledged event discovery features.
- Advanced analytics for business users.

## Business Model Hypothesis
Generate revenue through partnerships with local businesses who pay for promotional listings and potentially a commission on loyalty rewards redeemed.

## Critical Assumptions
- There is sufficient interest from local businesses to participate.
- Users will engage with the app to discover new local businesses.
- The recommendation algorithm will effectively provide relevant options to users.

## How To Test Quickly
- Conduct surveys and focus groups with local residents to validate interest.
- Pilot with a small group of local businesses to gather feedback on the app and its functionalities.
- Measure user engagement through app analytics.

## Acceptance Criteria
- At least 100 local independent businesses signed up for the pilot.
- 75% of pilot users report discovering at least one new business.
- User retention rate exceeds 60% after the first month.

## Risks And Failure Modes
- Difficulty in convincing local businesses to join the platform.
- The recommendation engine may not accurately classify user preferences.
- Users may find offers irrelevant, leading to low engagement.
