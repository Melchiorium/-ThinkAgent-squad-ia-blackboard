# Evaluation CEO - LocalLoop Version 2

Sources used: `prd.md`, `architecture.md`, `architecture.pdf`, `gtm.md`.

## Final Decision: REVISE

## Global Note: 5/10

## Executive Verdict

LocalLoop v2 is more focused than a generic local directory because it starts with coffee shops/restaurants, promotions, loyalty, and a concierge merchant pilot.

It is still not investable as a full mobile app. The plan assumes merchants will pay and users will switch from Yelp/Google Maps without proving redemption, repeat usage, or merchant ROI.

## Business Viability

The local discovery pain is plausible but not intense. Consumers already have many tools. Merchants already receive many digital marketing pitches.

The wedge around coffee shops/restaurants is better, but 50 businesses in one city is too ambitious before proving the model with 10-15 merchants in one neighborhood.

## Profitability Potential

Revenue hypotheses include subscriptions, commissions on loyalty redemptions, and sponsored promotions. That is too many models too early.

Missing: merchant price, redemption economics, paid conversion, merchant CAC, churn, repeat redemption targets, and support costs.

## Execution Complexity

The architecture is feasible: mobile app, backend API, geolocation, recommendations, merchant profiles, offers, loyalty.

But the MVP is too heavy. Loyalty, recommendations, reviews, local events, and merchant promotion management can balloon quickly. The PDF shows better ops controls than v1: admin dashboard, monitoring, CRM, engagement tracking.

Still, a manual concierge pilot should precede a full app.

## Go-To-Market Realism

The GTM correctly secures coffee shops/restaurants first. But its assumptions are aggressive: 15-20 businesses in one month from 40 targets implies 50% conversion. That is optimistic without strong existing relationships.

The launch threshold of 50 businesses and 200 active users is too high for the first proof stage.

## Competitive Risk

High. Google Maps, Yelp, Groupon, Instagram, TikTok, local newsletters, merchant loyalty tools, and word of mouth are strong alternatives.

LocalLoop needs exclusive offers and measurable merchant ROI to matter.

## Major Risks

- Merchants do not pay.
- Users do not need another discovery app.
- Promotions are not compelling.
- Loyalty mechanics add complexity before proof.
- Recommendation quality is weak.
- Merchant data grows stale.

## Final Recommendation

Revise. Run a smaller pilot:

- one neighborhood
- 10-15 cafes/restaurants
- manually curated recommendations
- simple redemption codes, not full loyalty
- no reviews/events initially
- track views, redemptions, repeat redemptions, merchant ROI, merchant willingness to pay, and weekly active users

Full build should wait for merchant ROI proof.
