# LocalLoop Product Proposal

## Product Problem
Consumers want to support local businesses but struggle to discover them due to fragmented information and overwhelming competition from large platforms. Independent businesses seek visibility and customer retention but lack resources for effective marketing.

## Initial Wedge
Enable targeted local discovery for urban residents through personalized recommendations and local promotions from independent businesses.

## First Target User
Urban residents aged 25-40 looking for local dining and shopping experiences.

## Existing Alternatives And Switching Trigger
- **Alternatives:** Google Maps, Yelp, and large delivery platforms.
- **Switching Trigger:** Unique personalized experiences and exclusive deals from local businesses not available on larger platforms.

## Core MVP Workflow
1. User downloads and registers on the app.
2. User grants geolocation permission.
3. User receives personalized recommendations and local promotions.
4. User visits local businesses using the promotions.
5. User earns loyalty rewards for repeat visits.

## In Scope
- Personalized recommendations based on user location.
- Geolocation feature to find nearby businesses.
- Merchant profiles for local businesses with offers.
- Promotions and loyalty system for users.
- Basic user reviews for businesses.
- Onboarding local businesses through a straightforward registration process.

## Out of Scope
- Local event discovery (deferred to later releases).
- Complex merchant marketing tools (e.g., analytics dashboards).
- Additional user demographics profiling beyond basic recommendations.
- Detailed analytics for businesses (deferred).

## MVP Build Vs Pilot Operations
### Must Build Now
- Personalized recommendations.
- Geolocation capabilities.
- Basic merchant profiles.
- Promotions and loyalty rewards.
- Onboarding mechanism for local businesses.

### Manual Or Operational During Pilot
- Manual outreach to local businesses for onboarding.
- Collection of user feedback regarding recommendations and promotions.

### Deferred Until After Proof
- Advanced features like event discovery and detailed analytics for businesses.

## Business Model Hypothesis
Revenue from local businesses will be generated through subscription fees for promotional placements and a small percentage of loyalty rewards redeemed at stores.

## Critical Assumptions
- Users will prefer discovering local businesses over larger platforms.
- Local businesses will be willing to participate in the platform for visibility and customer retention.
- The recommended businesses will meet the quality expectations of users.

## How To Test Quickly
- Conduct surveys with target users about their interest in the app.
- Roll out a pilot in one urban area with limited businesses to test user engagement and satisfaction.
- Measure engagement metrics with the loyalty reward system.

## Acceptance Criteria
- At least 100 active users in the pilot area within the first month.
- 70% user retention after three months.
- Positive feedback from at least 80% of users on recommendation relevance.

## Risks And Failure Modes
- Low user adoption due to competition from established platforms.
- Poor engagement from businesses leading to insufficient supply.
- Inability to maintain recommendation quality, resulting in user frustration.

## Product Readiness
Status: LIMITED

Blocking Gaps:
- Need for a critical mass of engaged local businesses [local_business_engagement].
- A robust method for ensuring recommendation quality [recommendation_quality].

Required Improvements:
- Focus on user feedback loops for continuous improvement [user_feedback_loop].

## Recommendation
Proceed with the MVP development focused on the core features of localized discovery and promotional offers to validate the concept and address potential user and business engagement challenges.
