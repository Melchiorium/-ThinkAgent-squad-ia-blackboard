# Blackboard

## Project Brief

Project Name: LocalLoop

Pitch:
A mobile application that helps people discover local independent businesses through personalized recommendations, neighborhood deals, and loyalty rewards.

Context:
Many consumers want to support local businesses but often default to large platforms because discovering small local stores, restaurants, services, and events is fragmented and inconvenient.

At the same time, independent businesses struggle with visibility and customer retention but often lack the budget or expertise to run digital marketing campaigns.

The platform helps consumers discover relevant local businesses while giving small businesses simple tools to attract and retain nearby customers.

Target Users:
- Urban residents
- Young professionals
- Families
- Tourists
- Independent local businesses

Potential Use Cases:
- Discover nearby coffee shops
- Find local restaurants with promotions
- Discover niche stores
- Access loyalty rewards
- Local event discovery
- Neighborhood recommendations

Platform Capabilities:
- Personalized recommendations
- Geolocation
- Merchant profiles
- Promotions/offers
- Loyalty system
- Reviews
- Local event feed

Constraints:
- Limited initial budget
- Need enough local business supply in one city before expanding
- Must avoid competing directly with massive horizontal platforms

Challenges:
- Convincing local businesses to join
- Maintaining recommendation quality
- Avoiding platform saturation with irrelevant offers

Long-term Vision:
Become the default discovery platform for local commerce and neighborhood experiences.

# LocalLoop Product Proposal

## Product Problem
Consumers often want to support local businesses but find it challenging to discover them due to fragmented information and the convenience of large platforms. Local businesses, on the other hand, face visibility challenges and lack resources for effective digital marketing, making customer retention difficult.

## Initial Wedge
The narrowest credible MVP wedge is to focus on urban residents looking to discover local coffee shops and restaurants with promotions and loyalty rewards. This allows us to validate the core functionality of personalized recommendations and promotions.

## First Target User
Urban residents aged 25-40, particularly young professionals and families, who seek out local dining and entertainment options that are both convenient and community-oriented.

## Existing Alternatives And Switching Trigger
Users currently rely on large platforms like Yelp and Google Maps for local business discovery. A compelling reason to switch would be a more personalized experience focusing exclusively on local businesses, featuring tailored offers, loyalty rewards, and an emphasis on community engagement that larger platforms do not prioritize.

## Core MVP Workflow
1. User signs up and sets preferences for local business categories (e.g., coffee shops, restaurants).
2. User receives personalized recommendations based on geolocation.
3. Users can view promotions/offers from local businesses.
4. Users can collect loyalty rewards by engaging with businesses.

## In Scope
- Personalized recommendations based on user preferences.
- Geolocation feature to pinpoint nearby businesses.
- Merchant profiles showcasing local businesses (limited feature set).
- Promotions/offers display for engaging local businesses.
- Simple loyalty system for user retention.

## Out of Scope
- Reviews and ratings features (to be developed after initial engagement proof).
- Local event discovery (for future versions, based on user feedback).
- Additional business categories beyond coffee shops and restaurants (initially).
  
## Business Model Hypothesis
Local businesses would pay a subscription for premium features such as enhanced visibility, special promotions, and customer analytics, while users would benefit from personalized recommendations and loyalty rewards at no direct cost.

## Critical Assumptions
1. Users prefer personalized recommendations over generic listings and will engage with the app.
2. Local businesses will see value in joining to gain visibility and would be willing to pay for premium services.
3. A sufficient number of local businesses in a specific urban area will be willing to join.

## How To Test Quickly
- Conduct surveys/interviews with users to gauge interest in the app and preferred features before development.
- Pilot with a small set of local coffee shops and restaurants to gauge their willingness to participate and offer promotions.
  
## Acceptance Criteria
- At least 100 active users interacting with the app weekly within the first month after launch.
- At least 10 local businesses signed up for the MVP before the public launch.
- Users report a high satisfaction rate with personalized recommendations.

## Risks And Failure Modes
- Low uptake by local businesses.
- Users may not engage with the app if recommendations are irrelevant.
- Significant competition from larger platforms that could overshadow the app's visibility.

## Product Readiness
Status: LIMITED

Blocking Gaps:
- Limited engagement with potential users for feedback.
- Need confirmations from local businesses regarding interest and participation.

Required Improvements:
- Conduct user and business feedback sessions to refine the offering.
- Ensure the MVP is user-friendly and solves the specific problems identified.

## Recommendation
Proceed to a research and concierge pilot to validate user interest and local business participation before full MVP development. Engage with a select few businesses to gather insights and further refine the product based on real-world interactions.
