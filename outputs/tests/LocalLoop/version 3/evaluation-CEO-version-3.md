# Evaluation CEO - LocalLoop Version 3

Sources used: `prd.md`, `architecture.md`, `architecture.pdf`, `gtm.md`.

## Final Decision: REVISE

## Global Note: 5/10

## Executive Verdict

LocalLoop v3 improves focus by narrowing to coffee shops/restaurants and deferring reviews and event discovery. That is the right direction.

However, the proposal remains a local marketplace/discovery product without proof of merchant ROI or consumer repeat usage. It is still a pilot candidate, not a full build.

## Business Viability

The problem is real but not severe enough on its own. Users already use Google Maps, Yelp, Instagram, TikTok, local newsletters, and friends.

The switching trigger is tailored local offers and community connection. That must be proven through actual redemptions, not surveys.

## Profitability Potential

The merchant subscription model remains speculative.

Missing: price, paid conversion, merchant churn, redemptions per merchant, repeat-visit value, and cost to maintain promotions. "Premium visibility and analytics" are not compelling until the platform drives traffic.

## Execution Complexity

The architecture is sensible: client-server, geolocation, recommendations, merchant profiles, loyalty, consent management, manual promotions.

The PDF aligns well with the Markdown and shows useful control points: manual promotion management, data auditing, feedback collection. That is an improvement.

Still, the recommendation engine and loyalty system should remain lightweight until redemption behavior is proven.

## Go-To-Market Realism

The GTM is better: 15 businesses approached, 10 active offerings, manual concierge onboarding.

The weak point is the 50% business engagement assumption. That is optimistic unless the team has warm local relationships. User acquisition also remains underdeveloped beyond the merchant channel.

## Competitive Risk

High. The market is crowded and established alternatives own habitual local discovery.

Only exclusive offers, local relationships, and measurable repeat visits create defensibility.

## Major Risks

- Merchants participate but do not pay.
- Users redeem once and churn.
- Recommendations are not better than Google/Yelp.
- Loyalty rewards are not compelling.
- Manual promotion ops do not scale.
- Consumer acquisition is too weak.

## Final Recommendation

Revise. Validate with a narrow concierge pilot:

- one neighborhood
- 10 merchants in one category
- exclusive offers
- simple redemption tracking
- no full loyalty engine initially
- one consumer acquisition channel
- measure redemption rate, repeat redemption, weekly retention, merchant satisfaction, merchant willingness to pay, and offer ROI

LocalLoop v3 is cleaner, but not yet investable.
