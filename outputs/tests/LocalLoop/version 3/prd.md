# LocalLoop Product Proposal

## Product Problem
Consumers want to support local businesses but face challenges discovering them due to fragmented information and the convenience offered by large platforms. Local businesses struggle with visibility and often lack the resources for effective digital marketing, leading to low customer retention.

## Initial Wedge
The narrowest credible MVP wedge focuses on urban residents seeking to discover local coffee shops and restaurants that provide promotions and loyalty rewards. This setup allows for the validation of personalized recommendations and promotional engagement.

## First Target User
Urban residents aged 25-40, particularly young professionals and families, looking for convenient and community-oriented dining and entertainment options.

## Existing Alternatives And Switching Trigger
Current users rely on platforms like Yelp and Google Maps for local discovery. The compelling trigger to switch would be a personalized experience focused exclusively on local businesses, featuring tailored offers, loyalty rewards, and deeper community engagement often overlooked by larger platforms.

## Core MVP Workflow
1. User signs up and selects preferences for local business categories (coffee shops, restaurants).
2. Using geolocation, users receive personalized recommendations.
3. Users can view and engage with promotions and offers from local businesses.
4. A simple loyalty rewards system allows users to collect and redeem rewards.

## In Scope
- Personalized recommendations based on user preferences.
- Geolocation to locate nearby businesses.
- Merchant profiles with essential information.
- Display of promotions/offers from local businesses.
- Basic loyalty system for user engagement.
- Simple onboarding for users and businesses.
- User data privacy controls and consent management.

## Out of Scope
- User reviews and ratings features (to be considered post-MVP).
- Local event discovery (deferred for future iterations based on feedback).
- Expanded business categories beyond coffee shops and restaurants (initial focus).

## Business Model Hypothesis
Local businesses would pay a subscription fee for premium features, such as enhanced visibility and customer analytics, while users access personalized recommendations and loyalty rewards for free.

## Critical Assumptions
1. Users will engage with personalized recommendations rather than generic listings.
2. Local businesses will find value in increased visibility and will subscribe for premium features.
3. Sufficient local business participation exists in the initial targeted urban area.

## How To Test Quickly
- Conduct surveys and interviews with potential users to gauge interest in the app’s features before development.
- Execute a pilot with select coffee shops and restaurants to assess their willingness to participate and offer promotions.

## Acceptance Criteria
- Achieve at least 100 active users weekly within the first month post-launch.
- Secure participation from 10 local businesses before public launch.
- A majority of users report a high satisfaction rate with personalized recommendations.

## Risks And Failure Modes
- Low adoption rates from local businesses.
- Potential disengagement from users if recommendations are irrelevant.
- Competition from larger platforms may limit visibility and efforts.

## Product Readiness
Status: LIMITED

Blocking Gaps:
- Insufficient feedback engagement with potential users.
- Lack of confirmations from local businesses about interest in participating.

Required Improvements:
- Conduct feedback sessions with users and businesses for requirement refinement.
- Establish clear data privacy compliance strategies appropriate to the features offered.

## Recommendation
Proceed with a research and concierge pilot approach to validate user interest and local business participation. Engage a small subset of businesses to collect insights that further refine the product based on real-world interactions.
