# LocalLoop MVP Product Proposal

## Product Problem
Consumers want to support local businesses but struggle to discover them due to the fragmented nature of local commerce. Independent businesses face challenges in visibility and customer retention. There exists an opportunity to create a centralized platform that connects urban residents with local independent businesses through tailored recommendations and promotions.

## Initial Wedge
The narrowest credible MVP wedge is focused on the **urban residents segment**, specifically targeting young professionals. This will allow us to validate the core value of personalized local discovery while ensuring sufficient local business supply to meet user demand.

## First Target User
Young professionals living in urban areas who are eager to explore their neighborhood and support local independent businesses.

## Existing Alternatives And Switching Trigger
Current alternatives include large platforms like Yelp and Google Maps, which provide business listings but lack personalized recommendations and local deals. Users are likely to switch to LocalLoop if it offers a more curated experience, unique promotions, and loyalty rewards that benefit them directly while supporting local businesses.

## Core MVP Workflow
1. User opens the app and sets their location.
2. User receives personalized recommendations for local businesses based on their preferences and location.
3. User can view promotions and loyalty rewards offered by these businesses.
4. User can access basic business profiles and leave reviews.
5. Clear onboarding process for first-time business owners to present their offers and promotions.

## In Scope
- Personalized recommendations based on user preferences
- Geolocation for local business discovery
- Basic merchant profiles showcasing promotions and offers
- Simple loyalty rewards system
- User reviews for businesses

## Out of Scope
- Advanced features such as a comprehensive local event feed and complex marketing tools for businesses
- Integration with massive horizontal platforms
- Support for non-urban segments (families and tourists) in the initial launch

## Business Model Hypothesis
Revenue will be generated through subscription fees from local businesses for featured listings and promotional placements within the app. Alternatively, we might explore transaction fees from sales made through the app.

## Critical Assumptions
1. Young urban professionals are actively seeking local independent businesses and will use an app to facilitate this discovery.
2. Local businesses will see value in obtaining visibility through a dedicated platform and be willing to pay for it.
3. The platform can maintain a high quality of recommendations without entering into saturation or irrelevant offers.

## How To Test Quickly
- Conduct user interviews and surveys with young urban professionals to understand their needs and pain points regarding discovering local businesses.
- Run a pilot program with selected local businesses to gauge their interest in joining the platform and willingness to share promotions.
- Create a basic prototype focused on core recommendation features for user testing.

## Acceptance Criteria
- At least 100 local businesses sign up for the platform.
- Achieve a minimum of 500 downloads within the first month.
- Obtain an NPS (Net Promoter Score) of 30 or above from user feedback regarding the relevance of recommendations.

## Risks And Failure Modes
- Risk of insufficient local business supply leading to poor user experience.
- The challenge of maintaining high-quality recommendations, which could diminish user trust.
- Market saturation with irrelevant offers could decrease user engagement.

## Product Readiness
Status: LIMITED

- Uncertainty around local businesses' willingness to engage with the platform for visibility. [demand_validation]
