## Project Brief

Project Name: LocalLoop

Pitch:
A mobile application that helps people discover local independent businesses through personalized recommendations, neighborhood deals, and loyalty rewards.

Context:
Many consumers want to support local businesses but often default to large platforms because discovering small local stores, restaurants, services, and events is fragmented and inconvenient.

At the same time, independent businesses struggle with visibility and customer retention but often lack the budget or expertise to run digital marketing campaigns.

The platform helps consumers discover relevant local businesses while giving small businesses simple tools to attract and retain nearby customers.

Target Users:
- Urban residents
- Young professionals
- Families
- Tourists
- Independent local businesses

Potential Use Cases:
- Discover nearby coffee shops
- Find local restaurants with promotions
- Discover niche stores
- Access loyalty rewards
- Local event discovery
- Neighborhood recommendations

Platform Capabilities:
- Personalized recommendations
- Geolocation
- Merchant profiles
- Promotions/offers
- Loyalty system
- Reviews
- Local event feed

Constraints:
- Limited initial budget
- Need enough local business supply in one city before expanding
- Must avoid competing directly with massive horizontal platforms

Challenges:
- Convincing local businesses to join
- Maintaining recommendation quality
- Avoiding platform saturation with irrelevant offers

Long-term Vision:
Become the default discovery platform for local commerce and neighborhood experiences.

# LocalLoop MVP Product Proposal

## Product Problem
Many consumers desire to support local businesses but currently rely on large platforms because discovering independent stores and services is fragmented and inconvenient. Conversely, independent businesses face challenges in visibility and customer retention, lacking the resources for effective digital marketing.

## Initial Wedge

## First Target User
Urban residents, specifically young professionals aged 25-40 who actively seek unique local experiences and support independent businesses.

## Existing Alternatives And Switching Trigger
- **Existing Alternatives:** Google Maps, Yelp, and large food delivery services like DoorDash that provide listings of local businesses.
- **Switching Trigger:** Users will switch to LocalLoop for its personalized recommendations and exclusive deals aimed specifically at promoting independent businesses, along with a loyalty rewards system enhancing their overall consumer experience.

## Core MVP Workflow
1. Users create a profile indicating preferences (food types, services etc.).
2. The app utilizes geolocation to suggest nearby independent coffee shops and restaurants.
3. Users view available promotions and loyalty rewards from interested merchants.
4. Users can leave reviews and feedback on their experiences.

## In Scope
- User profile creation and preference selection.
- Geolocation-based display of local businesses.
- Merchant profiles showcasing promotions and loyalty rewards.
- User reviews and ratings for businesses.
- Simple loyalty program integration.

## Out of Scope
- Comprehensive listings of all local businesses (focus only on selected coffee shops and restaurants initially).
- Advanced marketing analytics for businesses.
- Community event integration (initial focus on businesses only).

## Business Model Hypothesis
LocalLoop will generate revenue through a subscription model for businesses that want premium visibility and access to analytic tools, alongside a commission on customer loyalty rewards redeemed through the app.

## Critical Assumptions
- Urban residents are actively seeking local business alternatives to large platforms.
- Small businesses will see value in joining the platform for customer acquisition.
- The recommendations system can maintain a high quality of relevance and personalization.

## How To Test Quickly
- Conduct surveys and focus groups with potential users to gauge interest in features and willingness to switch.
- Pilot a small-scale version of the app with selected local coffee shops and restaurants to assess engagement and traction.

## Acceptance Criteria
- At least 100 users on the app within the first month.
- Engagement rate of at least 30% of users redeeming rewards in the first 30 days.
- Positive feedback from at least 75% of participating businesses regarding customer engagement.

## Initial Wedge
The narrowest credible MVP wedge is targeting urban residents seeking to discover local independent coffee shops and restaurants through personalized recommendations and offers.

## First Target User
Urban residents, specifically young professionals aged 25-40 who actively seek unique local experiences and support independent businesses.

2. The app utilizes geolocation to suggest nearby independent coffee shops and restaurants.
