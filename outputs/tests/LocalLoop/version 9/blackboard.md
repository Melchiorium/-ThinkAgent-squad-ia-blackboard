## Project Brief

Project Name: LocalLoop

Pitch:
A mobile application that helps people discover local independent businesses through personalized recommendations, neighborhood deals, and loyalty rewards.

Context:
Many consumers want to support local businesses but often default to large platforms because discovering small local stores, restaurants, services, and events is fragmented and inconvenient.

At the same time, independent businesses struggle with visibility and customer retention but often lack the budget or expertise to run digital marketing campaigns.

The platform helps consumers discover relevant local businesses while giving small businesses simple tools to attract and retain nearby customers.

Target Users:
- Urban residents
- Young professionals
- Families
- Tourists
- Independent local businesses

Potential Use Cases:
- Discover nearby coffee shops
- Find local restaurants with promotions
- Discover niche stores
- Access loyalty rewards
- Local event discovery
- Neighborhood recommendations

Platform Capabilities:
- Personalized recommendations
- Geolocation
- Merchant profiles
- Promotions/offers
- Loyalty system
- Reviews
- Local event feed

Constraints:
- Limited initial budget
- Need enough local business supply in one city before expanding
- Must avoid competing directly with massive horizontal platforms

Challenges:
- Convincing local businesses to join
- Maintaining recommendation quality
- Avoiding platform saturation with irrelevant offers

Long-term Vision:
Become the default discovery platform for local commerce and neighborhood experiences.

## Product Problem
Consumers face difficulty discovering local independent businesses due to fragmentation in information sources. As a result, they often default to larger platforms. Meanwhile, independent businesses lack visibility and the means to market themselves effectively, leading to reduced customer retention.

The initial wedge for LocalLoop is personalized recommendations for local businesses based on user preferences and geolocation.

Urban residents aged 25-40 who are interested in supporting local businesses but currently rely on larger platforms for discovery.

## Existing Alternatives and Switching Trigger
Current alternatives include large platforms like Yelp and Google Maps, which often prioritize larger chains and have less focus on local businesses. Users would switch to LocalLoop for more relevant recommendations, exclusive local deals, and loyalty rewards that cater specifically to independent businesses.

## Core MVP Workflow
1. User signs up and inputs preferences (e.g., types of businesses they are interested in).
2. User receives personalized recommendations for local businesses based on their preferences and geolocation.
3. Users can view promotions from local businesses and access loyalty rewards.
4. Users can interact with merchant profiles, leaving reviews and navigating to businesses.

## In Scope
- **Personalized recommendations based on user inputs and location**
- **Merchant profiles featuring descriptions, promotions, and reviews**
- **Loyalty rewards system linked to local businesses**
- **Geolocation functionality to discover nearby businesses**
- **Basic local event feed for neighborhood happenings**

## Out of Scope
- Comprehensive event management capabilities
- Advanced CRM tools for businesses
- Extensive marketing campaign features
- User-generated content beyond reviews

## Business Model Hypothesis
LocalLoop would generate revenue through a subscription model where businesses pay for enhanced visibility and access to customer analytics and potentially a commission-based model on loyalty rewards redeemed through the app.

## Critical Assumptions
- Local independent businesses are willing to participate and pay for increased visibility.
- Users prefer personalized recommendations over generic lists.
- There is a sufficient density of local businesses in the initial city to make the platform viable.
- Users will engage with the loyalty rewards system.

## How to Test Quickly
- Conduct surveys and interviews with potential users to validate their pain points and interest.
- Pilot the app with a limited number of local businesses to gather initial feedback.
- Track user engagement with the recommendations and loyalty features within a small market.

## Acceptance Criteria

2. 5-10 local businesses onboarded in the pilot city.
3. Users frequently engage with the recommendations and loyalty program (10% engagement rate in the first month).
