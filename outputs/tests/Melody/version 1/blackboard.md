# Blackboard

## Project Brief

Project Name: Melody

Pitch:
A social and dating platform built around music compatibility.

The app helps music lovers connect through shared musical tastes, discover new artists, attend concerts together, find band members, and participate in local music communities.

Context:
Music plays a central role in many people's identity, relationships, and social lives. Traditional dating apps rarely account for deep lifestyle compatibility, and music communities are fragmented across multiple platforms.

Melody aims to combine relationship matching, music discovery, and community interaction into one platform.

Target Users:
- Music enthusiasts
- Amateur musicians
- Concert-goers
- Singles looking for deeper compatibility
- People looking for band members
- Music communities

Potential Use Cases:
- Dating based on music compatibility
- Finding concert partners
- Discovering new artists
- Finding musicians to jam with
- Buying/selling music equipment
- Joining local music communities

Platform Capabilities:
- Music taste onboarding
- Compatibility scoring
- Messaging
- Concert discovery
- Community groups
- Music marketplace
- Artist recommendations

Constraints:
- Need access to music preference data
- Must avoid becoming "just another dating app"
- Need strong early retention loop
- Limited initial funding

Challenges:
- Building enough user density
- Prioritizing features for MVP
- Competing with existing dating/social platforms
- Defining a sustainable business model

Long-term Vision:
Become the leading social ecosystem for people whose identity is deeply tied to music.

## Product Problem
Traditional dating apps and social platforms overlook the importance of music compatibility, leading to missed connections among music lovers. There is a need for a platform that merges relationship matching with music discovery, fostering deeper connections through shared musical interests.

The initial wedge for Melody will focus on music compatibility-based dating, leveraging user music tastes to match singles.

## First Target User
Music enthusiasts looking for deeper compatibility in their romantic relationships will be the first target users.

## Existing Alternatives And Switching Trigger
Existing alternatives include general dating apps (e.g., Tinder, Bumble) and music-centric platforms (e.g., Meetup for concerts). Users may switch to Melody if it offers a unique value proposition focused on music compatibility, enriched matching features, and a vibrant music community.

## Core MVP Workflow
1. **User Onboarding**: Collect user music preferences through a questionnaire integrating data from existing music platforms (like Spotify).
2. **Compatibility Scoring**: Generate compatibility scores based on shared music tastes.
3. **Matching**: Present matches to users based on scores for potential dating connections.
4. **Messaging**: Enable users to communicate and organize concerts or musical events together.

## In Scope
- Music taste onboarding
- Compatibility scoring
- Messaging features
- Music compatibility-focused matching

- Features related to finding concert partners, community groups, music marketplace, and artist recommendations will be outside the MVP scope to maintain focus on dating functionality.
- Buying/selling music equipment is also out of scope as it diverges from dating purposes.

## Business Model Hypothesis
Melody will adopt a freemium model where basic features are free, with premium features (like enhanced visibility or advanced matchmaking options) available via subscription.

## Critical Assumptions
1. Users value music compatibility as a significant factor in relationship matching.
2. Users will provide accurate and comprehensive music preferences.
3. Initial users can be recruited effectively with limited marketing resources.

- Conduct user interviews or surveys to validate interest in music compatibility as a dating feature.
- Create a landing page to gauge interest and collect sign-ups before developing the MVP.

- Successfully onboard at least 100 users in the first month.

# Melody Product Proposal

## Product Problem
Traditional dating apps and social platforms overlook the importance of music compatibility, leading to missed connections among music lovers. There is a clear need for a platform that merges relationship matching with music discovery, fostering deeper connections through shared musical interests.

## Initial Wedge
The initial wedge for Melody will focus specifically on music compatibility-based dating, leveraging user music tastes to match singles more effectively than conventional dating applications.

## First Target User
Music enthusiasts seeking deeper compatibility in romantic relationships will be the primary target users.

## Existing Alternatives And Switching Trigger
Current alternatives include general dating apps (e.g., Tinder, Bumble) and music-centric platforms (e.g., Meetup for concerts). Users may switch to Melody if it promises a unique value proposition centered on music compatibility, an enriched matching experience, and access to a vibrant music community.

## Core MVP Workflow
1. **User Onboarding**: Collect user music preferences through a streamlined questionnaire focused on key music genres.
2. **Compatibility Scoring**: Generate compatibility scores based on shared music tastes using data from a selected third-party music data provider.
3. **Matching**: Present matches to users based on compatibility scores for potential romantic connections.
4. **Messaging**: Enable users to communicate through a simple chat feature for coordinating meetings at concerts or events.

## In Scope
- Music taste onboarding: Simplified questionnaire to gather key music genre preferences.
- Compatibility scoring: Basic scoring algorithm using user preferences integrated from a designated music data provider.
- Messaging features: Direct communication through an essential chat interface.
- Music compatibility-focused matching: Implement a straightforward matching system utilizing compatibility scores.

## Out of Scope
- Features related to finding concert partners, community groups, a music marketplace, artist recommendations, and buying/selling music equipment are excluded from the MVP scope to maintain a focused approach on dating functionality.

## Business Model Hypothesis
Melody will operate on a freemium model where basic dating features are free, with premium offerings (like enhanced visibility or advanced matching options) available via subscription.

## Critical Assumptions
1. Users place significant value on music compatibility as an important factor in their romantic relationships.
2. Users will provide accurate and comprehensive details regarding their music preferences.
3. The onboarding process and initial features will adequately engage users to ensure continued interest.

## How To Test Quickly
- Conduct targeted user interviews to validate interest in music compatibility as a dating feature.
- Develop a landing page to gauge user interest and capture early sign-ups prior to MVP development.

## Acceptance Criteria
- Successfully onboard at least 100 active users within the first month.
- Achieve a match rate of over 30% based on calculated compatibility scores.
- Maintain a retention rate of at least 50% over the first three months.

## Risks And Failure Modes
- Limited initial user density may impede effective matching.
- Users might not disclose complete or truthful music preferences.
- Competitors with established user bases may limit Melody's traction and growth opportunities.
