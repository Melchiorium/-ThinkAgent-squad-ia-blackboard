# Evaluation CEO - Melody Version 1

Sources used: `prd.md`, `architecture.md`, `architecture.pdf`, `gtm.md`.

## Final Decision: REVISE

## Global Note: 5/10

## Executive Verdict

Melody has a coherent niche: dating based on music compatibility. The wedge is understandable, emotionally resonant, and technically feasible.

It is not approved because dating products are extremely density-dependent, acquisition-heavy, and hard to monetize without strong network effects. The proposal has not proven that music compatibility is strong enough to overcome those hurdles.

## Business Viability

Music taste can matter in attraction and identity. That gives the concept a plausible hook.

The problem is whether it is a primary dating decision driver or only a nice profile attribute. If it is the latter, established dating apps can absorb the feature easily.

## Profitability Potential

Freemium subscriptions are standard in dating, but monetization depends on density, match quality, retention, and repeat engagement.

The proposal does not define pricing, premium feature value, CAC, gender balance dynamics, moderation cost, or churn assumptions.

## Execution Complexity

The core build is feasible: onboarding, compatibility scoring, matching, and messaging.

The PDF is weak: it shows generic system blocks and no explicit operational controls, while the markdown correctly calls for moderation, reporting, permissions, privacy, and user safety. Dating products need those controls from day one.

## Go-To-Market Realism

Grassroots marketing through local music events and venues is directionally right. The target of 100-150 signups per month may be possible in one city, but signups do not equal match density.

The GTM needs a launch geography, user density threshold, and activation metric based on conversations or dates, not only profile completion.

## Competitive Risk

Competitive risk is high. Tinder, Bumble, Hinge, and niche communities can add music prompts or Spotify integrations.

Melody needs a community or event-driven wedge that incumbents cannot easily replicate through a profile field.

## Major Risks

- Insufficient user density.
- Music compatibility does not drive dating behavior.
- Gender imbalance damages experience.
- Messaging requires moderation and safety controls.
- Third-party music data access is undefined.
- Incumbents copy the feature.

## Final Recommendation

Revise.

Test the wedge manually with a local music-community landing page or event-based matching pilot before building the full app. Approve only if early users demonstrate repeated engagement and real conversations from music-based matches.
