# CEO Evaluation - Melody - Version 1

## 1. Business viability
The wedge is clear: Paris concert-companion matching based on music taste. This is focused enough for pilot testing.  
But market viability is fragile because matching products fail quickly without density, and the product can be perceived as a niche dating clone with weak differentiation.

## 2. Profitability potential
Future monetization paths exist (freemium controls, partnerships), but there is no near-term revenue certainty.  
Profitability is highly contingent on reaching critical local density first; without repeat usage, monetization is irrelevant.

## 3. Execution complexity
Technical execution is manageable for MVP. The architecture is simple and sensible.  
Operational complexity is significant: manual moderation, trust and safety handling, and density curation will require sustained effort.

## 4. Go-to-market realism
Founder-led, one-scene Paris launch is the right GTM shape and is realistic for a pilot.  
The GTM fails if scope expands before density proof. Any attempt to broaden to dating/community/marketplace too early will kill focus.

## 5. Competitive risk
Competitive risk is very high against entrenched alternatives (dating apps + event apps + social communities).  
Defensibility is weak unless Melody demonstrates meaningfully better match quality and conversion in a specific scene.

## Major risks
- Insufficient Paris scene density for reliable matching
- Weak compatibility signal from music preferences alone
- Reversion to incumbent apps with better liquidity
- Early trust/safety incidents damaging retention
- Product identity confusion (music tool vs dating app)

## 6. Final investment decision
**REVISE**

## 7. Global note
**5/10**

## Final recommendation
Do not approve scale investment. Run a single-scene Paris pilot with strict density and conversion gates.  
Approve next funding only if match-to-message and week-2 retention outperform generic alternatives for the same cohort.
