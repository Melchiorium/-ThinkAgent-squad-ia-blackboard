# Evaluation Report - Melody Version 1

Sources used: `prd.md`, `architecture.md`, `architecture.pdf`, `gtm.md`.

## Final Decision: REVISE

## Global Note: 4/10

## Executive Verdict

Melody is a niche dating concept based on music compatibility. The idea is emotionally intuitive, but the proposal does not yet prove that music taste is strong enough to pull users away from established dating apps.

The dating category is unforgiving: local density, safety, moderation, and retention matter more than novelty. Melody is not ready for a full MVP build.

## Business Viability

Music compatibility may be a useful dating signal, but the proposal does not prove it is a primary selection criterion.

The target user is too broad: "music enthusiasts seeking deeper compatibility." The GTM narrows to urban singles aged 25-35, but still does not choose one city, genre scene, or community.

Without local density, dating apps fail. The proposal recognizes this but does not define a density threshold.

## Profitability Potential

The freemium subscription model is speculative.

Missing:

- subscription price
- paid conversion target
- retention target
- user acquisition cost
- local density requirement
- trust/safety cost
- moderation cost
- music data provider cost

Premium visibility or advanced matching only sells if there are enough desirable matches. That is not yet demonstrated.

## Execution Complexity

The MVP includes onboarding, compatibility scoring, matching, messaging, moderation, music data provider integration, and privacy controls.

The architecture Markdown is moderately useful, but the architecture PDF is weak: it lists "No explicit flow captured" and "Admin / Ops Control Points: None", which directly conflicts with the Markdown's need for moderation, reporting, and support controls.

For a dating app, missing explicit safety and moderation flows is a serious gap.

## Go-To-Market Realism

The GTM direction is sensible: grassroots local music events and venue partnerships.

But it still lacks a concrete launch wedge:

- no launch city
- no first music scene
- no specific venue/community list
- no gender/orientation balance strategy
- no safety plan for meetups
- no minimum active user density

The proposal's 100-150 monthly signups from events may not produce enough active, compatible local matches.

## Competitive Risk

Competitive risk is very high. Melody competes with Tinder, Bumble, Hinge, niche dating apps, Spotify-linked profiles, Instagram, TikTok, and offline music communities.

Established dating apps can copy music prompts quickly. Melody needs a local community moat, not just a feature.

## Major Risks

- Music compatibility is not a strong enough switching trigger.
- Local density is too low for meaningful matching.
- Messaging safety is underdeveloped.
- Compatibility scoring feels shallow.
- Music data integration is unclear.
- Acquisition costs are too high.
- Monetization is unsupported.

## Final Recommendation

Revise before build.

Pilot conditions:

- one city
- one music scene or genre community
- 100-200 concentrated profiles
- manual or lightweight compatibility test
- safety basics: reporting, blocking, moderation
- no dependency on automated music provider integration
- measure profile completion, match acceptance, first-message rate, repeat usage, and willingness to pay

Melody should validate the dating thesis before investing in a real app.
