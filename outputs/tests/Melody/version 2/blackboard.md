## Project Brief

Project Name: Melody

Pitch:
A social and dating platform built around music compatibility.

The app helps music lovers connect through shared musical tastes, discover new artists, attend concerts together, find band members, and participate in local music communities.

Context:
Music plays a central role in many people's identity, relationships, and social lives. Traditional dating apps rarely account for deep lifestyle compatibility, and music communities are fragmented across multiple platforms.

Melody aims to combine relationship matching, music discovery, and community interaction into one platform.

Target Users:
- Music enthusiasts
- Amateur musicians
- Concert-goers
- Singles looking for deeper compatibility
- People looking for band members
- Music communities

Potential Use Cases:
- Dating based on music compatibility
- Finding concert partners
- Discovering new artists
- Finding musicians to jam with
- Buying/selling music equipment
- Joining local music communities

Platform Capabilities:
- Music taste onboarding
- Compatibility scoring
- Messaging
- Concert discovery
- Community groups
- Music marketplace
- Artist recommendations

Constraints:
- Need access to music preference data
- Must avoid becoming "just another dating app"
- Need strong early retention loop
- Limited initial funding

Challenges:
- Building enough user density
- Prioritizing features for MVP
- Competing with existing dating/social platforms
- Defining a sustainable business model

Long-term Vision:
Become the leading social ecosystem for people whose identity is deeply tied to music.

### Tech Summary

The primary feasibility challenge for the Melody platform is securing access to reliable music preference data which is crucial for onboarding and creating compatibility scores. A monolithic architecture focused on a API-integrated MVP is recommended to expedite development and validation.

## Tech Recommendations

- Establish partnerships with one or more music API providers for data access.
- Refine the onboarding experience to ensure ease of use and engagement.
- Prioritize the development of compatibility scoring algorithms based on user feedback.
- Create a basic marketing strategy to attract initial users to the platform.
- Develop a user feedback mechanism for iterative improvements.


## Tech Risks

- **Data access risk**: Failure to secure partnerships with music platforms may hinder development.
- **User engagement risk**: Low user retention due to inadequate focus on music compatibility features.
- **Operational risk**: Manual event recommendations may become unmanageable as user base grows.


## Tech Open Questions

- Which specific music APIs would provide the best user preference data and integration capabilities?
- What minimum set of music preferences should be collected during onboarding for effective compatibility scoring?
- How will user safety and content moderation be enforced, especially in messaging?


### Growth Summary

The main launch challenge for Melody is achieving user density quickly enough to create meaningful matches based on music compatibility. The recommended GTM direction focuses on leveraging local music communities through targeted events to acquire initial users, helping validate the interest and effectiveness of the platform before public launch.

## Growth Recommendations

- Integrate a user-friendly onboarding flow that emphasizes music preference gathering and highlights the value of compatibility scoring.
- Develop event-driven functionalities that encourage users to meet in person or at music-related gatherings as part of activation.
- Simplify compatibility scoring algorithms based on music preferences to be more straightforward for initial users.
- Building a feedback collection mechanism for early adopters to continuously refine the product experience.
- Ensure that messaging capabilities include options for sharing music and event invitations.


## Growth Risks

- Low initial user density resulting in inadequate matching opportunities.
- Potential lack of engagement from users if they do not see the value brought by music compatibility.
- Misalignment between the value users expect versus what the platform delivers early on.

# Melody Product Proposal

## Product Problem
Current dating and social platforms often overlook the importance of shared musical interests, making it difficult for users to find deeper lifestyle compatibility. Fragmented music communities increase the challenge of connecting with like-minded individuals and discovering new music in a cohesive environment.

## Initial Wedge
The initial wedge for Melody is the ability to connect singles based on music compatibility, leveraging shared tastes to create meaningful relationship opportunities. This feature directly addresses the gap in existing platforms where music is not considered a fundamental factor in matching.

## First Target User
The first target user segment will be singles who are passionate about music and seeking deeper connections through shared interests in music genres and artists.

## Existing Alternatives And Switching Trigger
Existing alternatives include conventional dating apps (e.g., Tinder, Bumble) and niche music platforms (e.g., Bandmix, Meetup). Users may switch to Melody if they feel limited by the broad, superficial matching systems of dating apps and seek a platform that integrates music into relationship building and community engagement.

## Core MVP Workflow
1. User onboarding to collect music preferences and interests.
2. Compatibility scoring based on shared musical tastes.
3. Matching singles based on scores with messaging capabilities.
4. Recommendations for concerts and music events based on user profiles.

## In Scope
- Music taste onboarding
- Compatibility scoring
- Messaging between matched users
- Event discovery for concerts relevant to user interests

## Out of Scope
- Buying/selling music equipment
- Extensive community group features
- Band member searches
- Music marketplace transactions
- Advanced artist recommendations

## Business Model Hypothesis
The primary revenue stream will be from premium features offering advanced matching options and enhanced messaging capabilities, with secondary revenue from partnerships with concert organizers and local venues.

## Critical Assumptions
- Users will prioritize music compatibility as a key factor in dating.
- There is a significant market of singles seeking deeper connections through music.
- Users are willing to provide music preference data for improved matching.

## How To Test Quickly
- Conduct surveys and interviews with potential users to validate interest in music-based matchmaking.
- Develop a simple landing page that outlines the app's concepts to gauge initial interest through sign-ups.

## Acceptance Criteria
- At least 100 users complete the onboarding process.
- 70% of users report satisfaction with the compatibility scoring process.
- At least 30% of matches result in messaging interactions within the first week.

## Risks And Failure Modes
- Low user density may lead to reduced matching opportunities.
- Users may find the music compatibility feature trivial or miss its intended value.
- The combined functionality could dilute focus, making the platform less appealing as a dating app.

Fragmented music communities increase the challenge of connecting with like-minded individuals and discovering new music in a cohesive environment.

The initial wedge for Melody is to connect singles based on music compatibility, leveraging shared tastes to create meaningful relationship opportunities. This feature directly addresses the gap in existing platforms where music is not considered a fundamental factor in matching.

## First Target User
The first target user segment will be singles who are passionate about music and seeking deeper connections through shared interests in music genres and artists.

## Core MVP Workflow
1. User onboarding to collect music preferences focused on specific genres and artists.
2. Compatibility scoring based on shared musical tastes.
3. Matching singles based on scores with messaging capabilities.
4. Recommendations for concerts and music events based on user profiles.

## In Scope
- Music taste onboarding focusing on genre and artist preferences.
- Compatibility scoring between matches solely based on user preferences.
- Messaging between matched users.
- Event discovery for concerts relevant to user interests based on onboarding data.

## Out of Scope
- Buying/selling music equipment.
- Extensive community group features.
- Searches for band members or musicians for jamming.
- Music marketplace transactions.
- Advanced artist recommendations beyond initial onboarding.

## Business Model Hypothesis
The primary revenue stream will come from premium features offering enhanced matching options and messaging capabilities. Additional revenue might be explored through partnerships with concert organizers and local venues. Validation of the business model's sustainability will be performed through user interest in premium features during initial user testing.

## Critical Assumptions
- Users will prioritize music compatibility as a key factor in dating.
- A significant market exists for singles seeking deeper connections through music.
- Users are willing to provide music preference data for improved matching.
- Securing partnerships with music platforms for data access is feasible.

## How To Test Quickly
- Conduct surveys and interviews with potential users to validate interest in music-based matchmaking.
- Develop a simple landing page that outlines the app's concepts to gauge initial interest through sign-ups.
- Identify potential music API providers and assess data integration and partnership opportunities.

## Acceptance Criteria
- At least 100 users complete the onboarding process.
- 70% of users report satisfaction with the compatibility scoring process.
- At least 30% of matches result in messaging interactions within the first week.
- Secured agreement with at least one music data API provider before launch.

## Risks And Failure Modes
- Low user density may lead to reduced matching opportunities.
- Users may not appreciate the music compatibility feature as valuable.
- The platform's dual functionality may dilute focus, deterring users primarily seeking dating options.
- Uncertainty regarding user satisfaction metrics related to matching and compatibility.

## Product Readiness

- Insufficient data on user preference and behavior concerning music compatibility.
- Need to establish partnerships with music platforms for data access.

Required Improvements:
- More research on user motivations for prioritizing music in relationships.

- Establish definitive agreements with music APIs for necessary data access.
