# Evaluation CEO - Melody Version 2

Sources used: `prd.md`, `architecture.md`, `architecture.pdf`, `gtm.md`.

## Final Decision: REVISE

## Global Note: 4/10

## Executive Verdict

Melody v2 has a coherent niche: dating through music compatibility, with event discovery as an engagement hook. The GTM correctly points toward local music communities and events.

But the dating market requires dense local liquidity, trust/safety, retention, and strong differentiation. The proposal does not prove that music compatibility is enough to make users switch from existing dating apps.

## Business Viability

The insight is plausible but unproven. Music can signal identity, but it may be a conversation starter rather than a core dating decision factor.

The target is still broad: music lovers aged 20-35 in urban areas. The launch needs one city and one scene or genre community.

## Profitability Potential

The premium matching/messaging model is speculative. Partnerships with venues may help later, but no venue will pay before there is audience density.

Missing: subscription price, paid conversion, CAC, retention, match-to-message rate, moderation cost, music API cost.

## Execution Complexity

The architecture is feasible but includes onboarding, scoring, matching, messaging, event discovery, moderation, analytics, and external music APIs.

The PDF aligns with the Markdown and shows needed moderation/analytics controls. But event discovery should be manual and secondary until matching value is proven.

## Go-To-Market Realism

The GTM is more realistic than broad social ads: local events, meetups, music communities. But assumptions are thin: 50 attendees, 10% conversion means 5 signups per event. That is far from enough dating liquidity.

The pilot must concentrate 100-200 profiles in one local niche before testing matching.

## Competitive Risk

Very high. Tinder, Bumble, Hinge, niche dating apps, Instagram, Spotify-linked profiles, and offline music scenes all compete.

Melody's feature can be copied easily. Its only possible moat is local community density.

## Major Risks

- Music compatibility is not a strong switching trigger.
- Low local density kills matching.
- Messaging safety is underdeveloped.
- API data access blocks scoring.
- Event discovery distracts from core dating validation.
- Monetization is unproven.

## Final Recommendation

Revise. Proceed only with a narrow pilot:

- one city
- one music scene
- manual/simple onboarding
- 100-200 concentrated profiles
- basic compatibility scoring
- safety basics: block, report, moderation
- measure profile completion, match acceptance, first-message rate, repeat usage, retention, and willingness to pay

Do not build a full dating app yet.
