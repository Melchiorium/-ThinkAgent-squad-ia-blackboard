# Evaluation CEO - Melody Version 3

Sources used: `prd.md`, `architecture.md`, `architecture.pdf`, `gtm.md`.

## Final Decision: REVISE

## Global Note: 4/10

## Executive Verdict

Melody v3 improves scope by removing concert discovery and community groups from MVP, which is good. It also mentions concierge matching before full build.

However, the PRD still sets unrealistic launch targets: 1,000 users in the first month and 70% messaging engagement. The GTM proposes referrals as the first motion, but referral loops do not work before users experience strong value.

## Business Viability

The niche is plausible but not proven. Music compatibility may matter, but it has not been validated as a primary dating criterion.

The target remains too broad: singles 18-35 in urban music scenes. A launch must select one city and one scene.

## Profitability Potential

Freemium remains speculative. Profile boosts and exclusive events only monetize after there is user density and repeated engagement.

No price, CAC, paid conversion, retention, moderation cost, or event economics are defined.

## Execution Complexity

The architecture is simpler than v2: onboarding, scoring, matching, messaging, monitoring/admin. The PDF supports this with clear flows and admin controls.

Still, messaging in dating requires safety and moderation from day one. Manual matching should be used before building scoring and matching systems.

## Go-To-Market Realism

The referral-based GTM is weak as a first motion. Users cannot refer two friends on average before the product proves it creates good matches.

The GTM does not choose a market, scene, or acquisition channel strong enough to seed dating liquidity. It also asks for 100 active pilot users, while the PRD asks for 1,000 users post-launch.

## Competitive Risk

Very high. Major dating apps can copy music compatibility prompts. Offline music communities already create social interaction.

Melody needs local density and brand/community energy, not just matching logic.

## Major Risks

- Referral loop fails without initial value.
- Music compatibility is not enough to switch.
- User density too low for dating.
- Safety/moderation burden underestimated.
- Unrealistic engagement targets.
- Monetization is unsupported.

## Final Recommendation

Revise. Replace referral-first GTM with a community-seeded pilot:

- one city
- one music scene
- 100-200 local profiles
- manual matching or lightweight prototype
- clear safety controls
- measure match acceptance, first-message rate, repeat sessions, retention, and willingness to pay

Melody v3 is cleaner in scope but still not investable.
