## Go-To-Market Notes

**Main Market Bottleneck**  
The primary bottleneck is establishing sufficient user density and engagement within a target audience that values music compatibility over conventional dating options.

**Side of the Market to Secure First**  
Focus first on individual singles who are music enthusiasts aged 18-35 actively interested in forming deeper connections through shared musical tastes.

**Initial Target Audience**  
Urban singles, specifically in music-centric cities, who enjoy live music events, actively participate in local music scenes, and are comfortable with online dating.

**Positioning**  
Position Melody as a finely-tuned dating platform centered around music compatibility, offering a more personalized experience that leverages shared musical interests, rather than merely a broad dating application.

**First Acquisition Motion**  
Implement a referral program where users who successfully onboard and refer friends earn perks such as premium features or exclusive early access to local concerts.

**Operating Assumptions for the First Acquisition Motion**  
- Expect each user to refer on average 2 friends within the first month.
- Aim for an engagement conversion rate of 15% from referred friends.
- Allocate approximately 10 hours per week for outreach to music community leaders to support initial referrals.

**Switching Trigger**  
Users will switch to Melody from existing platforms if the value of personalized matching based on music preferences outweighs the generic experiences provided by other dating apps.

**First Activation Loop**  
Users perform the onboarding by entering their music preferences, match with another user, and then send a message or connect for a concert, resulting in repeated actions as they engage and attend events together.

**What Must Exist Before Public Launch**  
A solid initial cohort of at least 100 active users from the pilot phase showing positive feedback on music compatibility's significance, alongside a well-structured onboarding and matching process.

## Review Summary
The primary challenge for Melody’s launch lies in achieving critical mass and user engagement through its music-centric dating approach. The recommended direction is to implement a referral acquisition model targeting urban music enthusiasts to generate initial traction while focusing on user safety and clear onboarding.

## Critical Assumptions
1. Users prioritize music compatibility highly enough to engage with a dedicated platform.
2. A minimum of 70% user engagement in messaging must be maintained post-matching.
3. The referral program will yield a conversion rate of 15% from referred friends.
4. Initial interest from a pilot group confirms users will willingly share music preferences.
5. Sufficient music-driven local events exist to facilitate community activities and enhance engagement.

## Requested Changes
1. Develop a structured onboarding process that encourages users to share their music preferences while ensuring privacy.
2. Implement a referral rewards system that incentivizes early user growth.
3. Create messaging guidelines focused on enhancing user trust and comfort during initial interactions.
4. Design a feedback mechanism within the app to capture user sentiment on the music-compatibility experience.
5. Integrate basic concert discovery features within the pilot to strengthen user engagement and community building.

## Risks
1. Low initial user retention and engagement can lead to a shrinking user base.
2. Strong competition from established dating apps with larger user bases.
3. Challenges in reliably obtaining and validating music preference data for effective matching.
4. Trust and safety concerns during user interactions may lead to negative experiences.
5. Users may find the KPIs unrealistically ambitious if the onboarding or matching is subpar.

## Open Questions
1. How will user privacy be managed when collecting and displaying music preferences?
2. What specific incentives will effectively motivate users to participate in the referral program?
3. Which urban markets should be the primary focus for initial outreach efforts?
4. What user feedback mechanisms can be established to iterate on the onboarding and matching process?
5. How can we effectively measure the success of user activation and retention from the very start?

## Why This Could Fail Even With Good Execution
Even with competent execution, if the assumption that users care enough about music compatibility to choose Melody does not hold true, the platform risks failing to gain traction despite a polished product and effective marketing efforts.

## GTM Readiness

Status: LIMITED

- Unvalidated user interest and willingness to share music preferences for effective matching.
- Need for concrete retention strategies to keep users engaged post-launch.
