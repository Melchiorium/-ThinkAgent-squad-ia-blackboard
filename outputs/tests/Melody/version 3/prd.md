# Melody Product Proposal

## Product Problem
Traditional dating apps and social platforms do not adequately address the lifestyle compatibility of music enthusiasts. With music being a significant aspect of identity and social interaction, there is an opportunity to create a niche platform that fosters connections based on musical tastes, facilitating deeper relationships and community engagement.

## Initial Wedge
The initial wedge for Melody will be the dating feature focused on music compatibility, allowing singles to connect based on shared musical interests and preferences. This will help differentiate the platform from other dating apps while tapping into the inherent social nature of music enthusiasts.

## First Target User
The first target user for launch will be singles aged 18-35 who are passionate about music and looking for deeper connections based on shared tastes. This segment has the highest potential for engagement and user density in urban areas with vibrant music scenes.

## Existing Alternatives And Switching Trigger
Existing alternatives include mainstream dating apps like Tinder, Bumble, and OkCupid, which emphasize a broad appeal without focusing on specific lifestyle interests. Users may switch to Melody for a more personalized experience centered on music compatibility, community building, and shared concerts.

## Core MVP Workflow
The core workflow for the MVP will include:
1. Onboarding process for users to input music preferences.
2. Compatibility scoring based on user inputs.
3. Profile matching to connect users with similar music tastes.
4. Messaging functionality to facilitate initial communication.

## In Scope
- Music taste onboarding process optimized with clear and concise questions for better data collection.
- Compatibility scoring mechanism.
- Basic messaging capabilities with user safety guidelines.
- User profile creation and matching algorithm.

## Out of Scope
- Marketplace features (buying/selling music equipment).
- Concert discovery and community groups.
- Extensive artist recommendations beyond initial onboarding.
- Advanced social features such as forums or blog posts.

## Business Model Hypothesis
The initial business model will focus on freemium offerings, where basic matching and messaging services are free, and premium features (e.g., advanced promotions, profile boosts, exclusive events) require subscription or one-time fees. Revenue will also be explored through partnerships with concert venues and local music businesses.

## Critical Assumptions
- Users will be willing to share their music preferences for better matching.
- Sufficient interest in direct music-based dating and socializing to drive initial user acquisition.
- The platform can achieve necessary user density in urban regions to foster engagement.

## How To Test Quickly
- Conduct user interviews and surveys to validate the importance of music compatibility in dating.
- Launch a simple landing page to gauge interest and collect emails from potential users.
- Create a concierge pilot where selected users manually connect based on music compatibility to validate the concept before full build-out.

## Acceptance Criteria
- At least 1,000 users onboarded in the first month post-launch.
- A minimum of 70% user engagement in messaging functionality after matching.
- Positive user feedback on the significance of music compatibility in matchmaking through surveys.

## Risks And Failure Modes
- Lower-than-expected user engagement could lead to a lack of matching opportunities.
- Competing platforms quickly adapting features could overshadow early market entry.
- Difficulty in acquiring access to music preference data may hinder onboarding.
- Safety and comfort concerns of users during communication may affect user adoption.

## Product Readiness
Status: LIMITED

Blocking Gaps:
- Validation of user interest and willingness to share music preferences.
- Clarity on initial retention strategies to engage users post-launch.

Required Improvements:
- Refine the onboarding process based on user insights to optimize data collection for music preferences.
- Implement clear user safety guidelines for messaging.
- Develop a structured referral acquisition model to boost early user growth.

## Recommendation
Proceed with refining the onboarding process and user safety guidelines while conducting initial user research to validate key assumptions. If results are favorable, move towards a concierge pilot to manually facilitate connections, ensuring foundational viability before full MVP development.
