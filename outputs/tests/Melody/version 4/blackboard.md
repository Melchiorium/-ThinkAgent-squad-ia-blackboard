# Blackboard

## Project Brief

Project Name: Melody

Pitch:
A social and dating platform built around music compatibility.

The app helps music lovers connect through shared musical tastes, discover new artists, attend concerts together, find band members, and participate in local music communities.

Context:
Music plays a central role in many people's identity, relationships, and social lives. Traditional dating apps rarely account for deep lifestyle compatibility, and music communities are fragmented across multiple platforms.

Melody aims to combine relationship matching, music discovery, and community interaction into one platform.

Target Users:
- Music enthusiasts
- Amateur musicians
- Concert-goers
- Singles looking for deeper compatibility
- People looking for band members
- Music communities

Potential Use Cases:
- Dating based on music compatibility
- Finding concert partners
- Discovering new artists
- Finding musicians to jam with
- Buying/selling music equipment
- Joining local music communities

Platform Capabilities:
- Music taste onboarding
- Compatibility scoring
- Messaging
- Concert discovery
- Community groups
- Music marketplace
- Artist recommendations

Constraints:
- Need access to music preference data
- Must avoid becoming "just another dating app"
- Need strong early retention loop
- Limited initial funding

Challenges:
- Building enough user density
- Prioritizing features for MVP
- Competing with existing dating/social platforms
- Defining a sustainable business model

Long-term Vision:
Become the leading social ecosystem for people whose identity is deeply tied to music.

# Melody Product Proposal

## Product Problem
Current dating and social platforms fail to address the deep lifestyle compatibility that music represents. Music enthusiasts struggle to find relationships, partners for concerts, and community interactions that reflect their musical tastes. Melody aims to bridge these gaps by creating a unique platform that connects individuals through shared musical interests, enabling deeper and more meaningful connections.

## Initial Wedge
The narrowest credible MVP wedge will focus on music-based dating and finding concert partners. This will allow users to establish connections based on shared musical preferences and participate in concerts with like-minded individuals.

## First Target User
The first target user segment will be singles looking for deeper compatibility based on their music preferences.

## Existing Alternatives And Switching Trigger
- Existing Alternatives: Traditional dating apps (e.g., Tinder, Bumble) and music community platforms (e.g., Meetup, Bandsintown).
- Switching Trigger: Users would switch to Melody if it provides a more tailored matching experience that leverages their musical tastes, offers concert attendance opportunities, and connects them to local music communities.

## Core MVP Workflow
1. User sign-up and onboarding to input music preferences.
2. Matching algorithm to identify compatible users.
3. Messaging functionality to facilitate communication.
4. Concert discovery feature to find events and connect with potential concert partners.

## In Scope
- Music taste onboarding
- Compatibility scoring and matching
- Messaging between matched users
- Concert discovery features

## Out of Scope
- Marketplace for buying/selling music equipment
- Community groups for music genres
- In-depth artist recommendations
- Extensive non-dating use cases, such as finding band members

## Business Model Hypothesis
The primary business model will be subscription-based for premium features, with a potential for partnerships with concert venues and artists to offer targeted promotions to users.

## Critical Assumptions
- Users are willing to share music preferences and data for better matching.
- There is sufficient user density in targeted markets to make matching effective.
- The feature set meets the primary desire for deeper music-related connections.

## How To Test Quickly
- Conduct user interviews and surveys to validate interest in the music-based dating and concert partner features.
- Create a landing page to gauge interest with a sign-up form or waitlist functionality.

## Acceptance Criteria
- 70% of users should complete onboarding.
- At least 50% of matches should initiate a conversation.
- 30% of users should attend a concert with a match within the first month.

## Risks And Failure Modes
- Limited user density in the initial stages could hinder matching effectiveness.
- Users may not find enough of their preferred artists or concerts on the platform.
- High competition from established dating and social platforms.
