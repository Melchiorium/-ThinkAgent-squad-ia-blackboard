# Evaluation CEO - Melody Version 4

Sources used: `prd.md`, `architecture.md`, `architecture.pdf`, `gtm.md`.

## Final Decision: REVISE

## Global Note: 4/10

## Executive Verdict

Melody version 4 expands the product from music-based dating into concert partner discovery. That makes the experience more vivid, but it also increases scope and operational dependency before the core dating wedge is proven.

The proposal should be revised down to a narrower validation test.

## Business Viability

The niche is plausible: singles who care deeply about music may want romantic or social matches around shared taste.

The problem is that the PRD now mixes dating, concert discovery, and local music community. That may improve storytelling but weakens MVP focus. The team has not proven which job users actually care about most.

## Profitability Potential

Subscriptions and venue partnerships are possible, but both depend on density and demonstrated engagement.

The proposal lacks a clear path from first user activity to revenue. Concert partnerships also require local sales effort and may distract from proving user retention.

## Execution Complexity

The build now includes onboarding, matching, messaging, concert discovery, external APIs, moderation, user feedback, and privacy controls.

The PDF confirms meaningful blocks and admin controls, but the external API dependency is central. Without reliable event data and music preference data, the product loses value quickly.

## Go-To-Market Realism

Venue partnerships and event-based acquisition are logical. The issue is the assumed success metric: 30% of users attending a concert with a match in the first month is highly unrealistic for an MVP.

The GTM should measure profile completion, match quality, message initiation, and intent to meet before requiring real-world concert attendance.

## Competitive Risk

Competition is high from dating apps, Bandsintown, Meetup, venue communities, Discord groups, and social media.

The feature set is easy to imitate unless Melody builds a uniquely dense local community.

## Major Risks

- User density is too low for matching.
- Concert discovery creates scope creep.
- External event/music APIs are unreliable or costly.
- Users like the idea but do not change dating behavior.
- Safety and moderation are underdeveloped.
- Venue partnerships take too much effort for early traction.

## Final Recommendation

Revise.

Reduce the MVP to music-based matching plus basic messaging in one city or one music scene. Treat concert discovery as a manual activation experiment, not a core product requirement.
