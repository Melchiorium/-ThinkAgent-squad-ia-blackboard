# Evaluation CEO - Melody Version 5

Sources used: `prd.md`, `architecture.md`, `architecture.pdf`, `gtm.md`.

## Final Decision: REVISE

## Global Note: 5/10

## Executive Verdict

Melody version 5 is a reasonable niche dating proposal. It has a clearer first user, a tighter music compatibility wedge, and a pragmatic technical plan using manual concert discovery.

It still needs revision because it assumes user density and monetization without enough proof. Dating marketplaces are unforgiving; a clever matching premise is not enough.

## Business Viability

The target segment is plausible: singles aged 25-35 who identify strongly with music. The emotional hook is stronger than a generic social app.

The unproven point remains whether users will prioritize music compatibility enough to join and keep using a new dating product.

## Profitability Potential

Subscription premium features and affiliate concert ticket revenue are plausible but speculative.

The product must first prove activation and retention. Affiliate revenue should be treated as upside, not core economics.

## Execution Complexity

The MVP build is manageable: onboarding, profiles, compatibility scores, messaging, and a manually curated concert module.

The PDF supports a practical architecture with admin dashboard, logging, moderation, and core modules. The missing piece is a concrete model for collecting and validating music preference data.

## Go-To-Market Realism

Manual outreach through music events, communities, and local musicians is realistic for a test. The 100 active users target is a reasonable early validation milestone.

The 500 active users in the first month acceptance criterion is too aggressive unless there is a strong pre-existing audience or partnership.

## Competitive Risk

High. Established dating apps have distribution, trust, and habit. They can add music prompts or integrations.

Melody needs to create a local scene-based community, not just a compatibility score.

## Major Risks

- Not enough users in the same city or scene.
- Music compatibility is interesting but not decisive.
- Onboarding becomes too long.
- Compatibility scores feel shallow or confusing.
- Messaging requires safety and moderation investment.
- Referral loops fail without strong user delight.

## Final Recommendation

Revise.

Approve only a small validation sprint: one city, one music community, manual onboarding, and a goal of proving match-to-conversation behavior. Do not invest in broader product build until density and retention are demonstrated.
