## Go-To-Market Notes

- **Main Market Bottleneck**: The primary bottleneck is achieving sufficient early user density for the platform to facilitate meaningful interactions based on music compatibility. 

- **Side of the Market to Secure First**: Focus on singles aged 25-35 in urban areas who prioritize deep musical connections, as their profiles likely create shared interest necessary for initial viability.

- **2 to 3 Structural GTM Decisions**:
  1. Emphasize the music compatibility feature as the primary value proposition to differentiate from typical dating apps.
  2. Implement a manual concert discovery feature that leverages community involvement to accelerate content creation and enhance engagement.
  3. Initiate a referral program to incentivize early users to invite friends, thereby building density and network effects.

- **Initial Target Audience**: Singles aged 25-35 who are keen music enthusiasts looking for deeper relational ties through shared musical tastes.

- **Positioning**: Melody is positioned as a unique platform that enhances dating experiences by prioritizing musical compatibility, transcending standard dating applications by fostering community interactions around music.

- **First Acquisition Motion**: Launch an early-access program with a manual outreach strategy (using personal networks, music events, or local meetups) to onboard initial users and gather feedback. This will involve leveraging existing music communities and local musicians.

- **Operating Assumptions for the First Acquisition Motion**: Target to onboard at least 100 active users through the manual outreach program in the first month, aiming for 50% of these users to interact with the music discovery features within two weeks of signup.

- **Switching Trigger**: Users will switch from existing dating and social platforms as they realize that those platforms do not offer tailored experiences reflecting their unique musical identities, as well as access to shared local music experiences.

- **First Activation Loop**: The activation loop can consist of users onboarding by rating their music preferences, generating compatibility scores, interacting with matches, and discovering local concert events—essentially focusing on repeated engagement through music discovery and messaging until users initiate a concert plan together.

- **What Must Exist Before Public Launch**: Clear validation of music preference onboarding and compatibility scoring must yield engaging and user-friendly experiences, with at least 100 active users providing feedback on interactions and functionalities, signaling readiness for a larger launch.

## Review Summary
The main launch challenge lies in achieving sufficient initial user density and engagement to validate Melody's value proposition around music compatibility. The recommended direction is to start with manual outreach for user acquisition while focusing on the core features of music-based matching.

## Critical Assumptions
1. Users will find significant value in compatibility based on music tastes for relationship-building.
2. The urban music enthusiast demographic will engage frequently if enough users join.
3. Manual concert discovery leads to higher retention and user satisfaction.
4. The referral program effectively drives user growth.
5. A minimum of 100 active users will demonstrate product-market fit.

## Requested Changes
1. Include a feedback mechanism within the app to assess user satisfaction and feature usage post-launch.
2. Clarify referral program details and incentives during onboarding to motivate early users.
3. Enhance UX/UI of the compatibility scoring display to facilitate user comprehension.
4. Prioritize concert discovery as a prominent feature within the onboarding experience.
5. Develop a clear communication plan for users upon matching to encourage rapid interactions.

## Risks
1. Low user retention if features outside dating do not appeal to target users.
2. Data collection issues leading to inaccurate or confusing compatibility scores.
3. High competition with established platforms resulting in low initial users.
4. User fatigue if the onboarding process is too lengthy or complex.
5. Difficulty in sourcing and curating relevant concert content consistently.

## Open Questions
1. What specific data points are most compelling to users when assessing music compatibility?
2. How will we define and measure success for the concert discovery feature?
3. What incentives will be most effective in driving referral program participation?
4. How can initial testing be structured to ensure diverse user feedback?
5. What will be the process for handling and curating concert data from community inputs?

## Why This Could Fail Even With Good Execution
Even with strong execution, if user assumptions about the importance of music compatibility in dating do not align with actual user behavior, engagement may wane, leading to insufficient traction and growth.
