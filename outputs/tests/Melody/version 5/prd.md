# Melody Product Proposal

## Product Problem
Existing dating and social platforms often overlook the deep lifestyle compatibility that music provides, leading to mismatched connections and missed opportunities for music enthusiasts. By consolidating music compatibility, discovery, and community interaction into one platform, Melody aims to serve a specific need that is currently unaddressed.

## Initial Wedge
The narrowest credible MVP wedge is centered around music-based relationship matching through compatibility scoring and messaging, targeting singles looking for deeper connections through their music taste.

## First Target User
The first target user for the initial launch is singles aged 25-35 who identify as music enthusiasts and are looking for dating opportunities based on shared musical interests.

## Existing Alternatives And Switching Trigger
Existing alternatives include traditional dating apps (e.g., Tinder, Bumble) and music-oriented social platforms (e.g., BandLab, Meetup). Users would switch to Melody for its unique focus on musical compatibility, enhanced interaction for concert discovery, and community engagement, which are not thoroughly covered by competitors.

## Core MVP Workflow
1. User onboarding to collect music preferences using a robust questionnaire focused on musical taste.
2. Generation of compatibility score based on shared music tastes.
3. User profiles prominently display music preferences.
4. Messaging functionality for interaction.
5. Initial concert discovery feature showing local events through manual sourcing.

## In Scope
- Music taste onboarding
- Compatibility scoring
- User profiles with music preferences
- Messaging
- Concert discovery feature with a manual process for sourcing events

## Out of Scope
- Music marketplace
- Community groups
- Artist recommendations
- Buying/selling music equipment
- Formal band member search functionality

## Business Model Hypothesis
The primary business model is subscription-based, monetizing through premium features such as enhanced search capabilities and access to exclusive events. Additionally, affiliate marketing from concert ticket sales could provide revenue.

## Critical Assumptions
1. Users will prioritize music compatibility in dating decisions.
2. Adequate user density can be achieved in key urban areas for initial launch.
3. Collecting music preference data will be straightforward and will provide value for users.
4. Users will be incentivized to invite friends through a referral program.

## How To Test Quickly
- Conduct user interviews and surveys focused on music significance in relationships.
- Deploy landing pages to gauge interest in music compatibility features.
- Create simple prototypes to test onboarding and messaging interactions with a small user group.

## Acceptance Criteria
- Successful onboarding with a 70% completion rate.
- At least 500 active users within the first month of launch.
- Users reporting at least two meaningful interactions within the app per week.

## Risks And Failure Modes
- High user drop-off due to lack of appealing features outside of dating.
- Difficulty in accurately gathering music preference data.
- Overlapping functionalities with existing apps may lead to user confusion.

## Product Readiness
Status: LIMITED

Blocking Gaps:
- Uncertainty regarding user density required for scalable interactions.
- Validation required on user preferences for music-based features and communication methods.

Required Improvements:
- Validate key assumptions through targeted user research and testing. [user_research]
- Develop a clear strategy for building initial user density, including a referral program. [growth_strategy]
- Establish minimum success metrics for MVP projection—500 active users within the first month as a go/no-go decision after launch. [metrics_validation]

## Recommendation
Proceed with an MVP build focusing on music compatibility and messaging features while conducting user research to test key assumptions and enhance the offering around user density.
