## Go-To-Market Notes
### Main Market Bottleneck
- **User density and validated interest**: Establishing enough initial users who find value in music-based matching to create meaningful interactions.

### Side of the Market to Secure First
- **Music enthusiasts aged 18-35**: This group is likely to be more passionate and more likely to engage deeply for both dating and community-building, offering the best early engagement potential.

### Structural GTM Decisions
1. **Focus on relationship matching first** rather than broader community features to simplify the MVP and dial in on user needs.
2. **Leverage an early community of music enthusiasts**, utilizing their networks and shared interests as a critical acquisition tool.
3. **Utilize a freemium model**, where initial basic services are free, attracting users but requiring them to pay for premium features that enhance their experience.

### Initial Target Audience
- Music enthusiasts aged 18-35 who are looking for deeper compatibility in relationships and are already familiar with dating apps.

### Positioning
- Melody positions itself not just as a dating app but as a social platform where users connect deeply through shared music preferences, differentiating itself from traditional dating platforms.

### First Acquisition Motion
- **Host local music events** in collaboration with bars or concert venues, attracting singles to experience music together and onboard users by having them register for the app on-site.

### Operating Assumptions for the First Acquisition Motion
- Users attending music events are likely to register for the app during the event.
- Engaging around live music creates meaningful connections, leading to organic sign-ups and app usage.
- At least 30% conversion rate of event participants to app users is a target.

### Switching Trigger
- Users from established dating platforms (e.g., Tinder, Bumble) will switch when seeking a more meaningful connection based on shared music interests rather than superficial characteristics often present in traditional dating apps.

### First Activation Loop
- After users sign up and go through onboarding:
  - Users will receive matches based on compatibility scores.
  - Encouraged to message each other, attend a concert together, and explore artist recommendations.

### What Must Exist Before Public Launch
- Refined onboarding for music taste, functioning compatibility scoring, basic messaging capabilities, and the concert discovery feature.

### Must Be Productized Now
- Music taste onboarding
- Compatibility scoring
- Messaging
- Concert discovery

### Can Stay Manual Or Operational During Pilot
- User support in onboarding and feature guidance
- Event coordination and user engagement at local music events 

### Deferred Until After Proof
- Marketplace for music equipment
- Community interactions
- Artist recommendations

## Review Summary
The main challenge for Melody is to prove sufficient interest and engagement from a defined user base passionate about music. The recommended direction is to prioritize local music events as an acquisition motion, drive initial sign-ups, and validate user interest through engagement in a focused manner.

## Critical Assumptions
- Users attending music events will seek deeper connections through the app.
- There is substantial user interest in using music as a basis for relationship compatibility.
- The engagement rate from live events will translate into a strong app retention rate.

## Requested Changes
1. Specify **local music events** as the primary acquisition motion with clear participation and conversion metrics.
2. Define a **concrete demand validation experiment**, such as hosting a focus group at a local music venue to gather insights on user preferences.
3. Reiterate the target conversion rate from event attendees to app sign-ups as **30%**.

## Risks
1. **Low engagement post-event**, where users may download but not actively use the app.
2. **Difficulty in sustaining initial momentum** if early users do not refer others.
3. **Risk of user mismatch** in the music preference data leading to lower satisfaction.
4. **Competitive saturation** in the dating app market may hinder sustained growth.
5. **Inability to effectively communicate differentiation**, causing potential users to overlook the unique value of Melody.

## Open Questions
1. What specific features will appeal most to users after initial onboarding?
2. How will ongoing user engagement be measured and supported post-launch?
3. Which local music venues or events will be prioritized for partnerships?
4. What strategies can be used to retain users beyond the initial match?
5. How will we gather feedback effectively to iterate on core functionalities?

## Why This Could Fail Even With Good Execution
If the assumptions around music compatibility as a driving factor for user engagement do not hold true, even a well-executed launch could result in a lack of sustained user interest and growth, leading to short-lived market presence.
