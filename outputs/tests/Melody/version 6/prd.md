# Melody Product Proposal

## Product Problem
Current dating and social platforms fail to account for the deep lifestyle compatibility that music brings to people's relationships. There is a fragmented market where music enthusiasts seek connections but cannot find a dedicated space that combines dating, community interaction, and music discovery.

## Initial Wedge
Focus on relationship matching based on music compatibility to prove concept viability quickly.

## First Target User
Singles aged 18-35 who are passionate about music and looking for deeper compatibility in relationships.

## Existing Alternatives And Switching Trigger
Existing dating apps (e.g., Tinder, Bumble) do not cater specifically to music. Users may switch to Melody when seeking a more meaningful connection based on shared music interests rather than superficial attributes.

## Core MVP Workflow
1. User signs up and selects their music preferences.
2. System generates compatibility scores with potential matches.
3. Users can discover concert events based on shared interests.
4. Users engage in messaging with compatible matches.

## In Scope
- User onboarding for music taste
- Compatibility scoring algorithm based on music preferences
- Core messaging functionality
- Concert discovery feature integrated with an existing event API

## Out of Scope
- Music marketplace
- Community groups
- Artist recommendations
- Buying/selling music equipment
- Advanced features beyond core matching and discovery

## MVP Build Vs Pilot Operations
### Must Build Now
- Music taste onboarding
- Compatibility scoring
- Messaging
- Concert discovery

### Manual Or Operational During Pilot
- User support in onboarding and feature guidance

### Deferred Until After Proof
- Marketplace for music equipment
- Community interactions
- Artist recommendations

## Business Model Hypothesis
Freemium model with premium features offered for advanced matching capabilities and exclusive concert access. Revenue could also come from partnerships with local venues and music events.

## Critical Assumptions
- Users are willing to share music preferences to find compatible matches.
- Music compatibility is a substantial driver for relationship success.
- There's sufficient interest in concerts and events among users.

## How To Test Quickly
- Launch a landing page to gauge interest and collect email sign-ups.
- Conduct surveys with potential users to validate the importance of music in dating.
- Prototype the onboarding and compatibility scoring to gather feedback.

## Acceptance Criteria
- At least 500 sign-ups within the first month.
- Average compatibility score of 70% or higher among matched users.
- Minimum 50% of users messaging each other after the first match.

## Risks And Failure Modes
- Low user acquisition and engagement.
- Difficulty in generating meaningful compatibility scores.
- Oversaturation of dating apps leading to user fatigue.

## Product Readiness
Status: LIMITED

Blocking Gaps:
- Lack of validated user interest and music preference data [demand_validation]

Required Improvements:
- Further market research on user preferences and pain points [market_motion]

## Recommendation
Proceed with the proposed MVP scope focusing on relationship matching through music compatibility. Validate user interest through rapid testing and iterating on core functionalities before expanding features.
