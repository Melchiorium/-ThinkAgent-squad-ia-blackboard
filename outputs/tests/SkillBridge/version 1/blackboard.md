## Project Brief

Project Name: SkillBridge

Pitch:
A digital platform designed to help young graduates and career switchers gain practical experience through short-term real-world projects proposed by small companies, startups, and non-profits.

Context:
Many graduates struggle to get hired because they lack professional experience.
At the same time, small organizations often need help on short projects but cannot afford full-time hires or expensive agencies.

The platform aims to connect both sides by allowing organizations to publish short missions (2 to 8 weeks) that candidates can complete remotely or on-site.

Target Users:
- Recent graduates
- Career changers
- Small businesses
- Startups
- Associations / NGOs

Potential Use Cases:
- UX audit for a small startup
- Social media campaign for a local business
- Data cleanup project
- Website redesign
- Market research mission
- Administrative process optimization

Platform Capabilities:
- User profiles
- Project posting
- Matching system
- Messaging
- Deliverable submission
- Feedback and reputation system

Constraints:
- Limited initial budget
- Need to build trust between both sides
- Must avoid becoming a low-quality freelance marketplace
- Legal/employment boundaries may vary depending on country

Challenges:
- Attract enough companies early on
- Ensure project quality
- Prevent abuse from companies looking for free labor
- Create enough value for candidates to stay engaged

Long-term Vision:
Become an alternative path to traditional internships and junior hiring by helping people build credible experience faster.

# SkillBridge MVP Product Proposal

## Product Problem
Recent graduates and career switchers often face difficulties entering the job market due to a lack of professional experience. Simultaneously, small organizations and startups require additional support on short-term projects but cannot afford full-time hires or paying agency fees. SkillBridge aims to bridge this gap by enabling organizations to post projects and providing candidates with opportunities to gain valuable experience.

## Initial Wedge
The initial MVP will focus on facilitating connections between small businesses and recent graduates seeking to complete short-term projects. This narrow scope addresses the pressing need for practical experience on both sides, ensuring a clear starting point for product development.

## First Target User
The first target user for the launch will be recent graduates. They are more likely to seek opportunities to gain experience and are actively looking for ways to build their resumes.

## Existing Alternatives And Switching Trigger
Current alternatives include traditional internship programs, freelance marketplaces (like Upwork), and volunteer positions. The switching trigger for users will be the unique offering of structured, short-term projects that provide real-world experience, along with a feedback system that ensures quality and accountability on both sides, addressing concerns about low-quality work often found on generic freelance platforms.

## Core MVP Workflow
1. User registration and profile creation for candidates and organizations.
2. Organizations create and post short-term projects with clear expectations.
3. Candidates browse available projects and submit applications.
4. Matching system facilitates connections between candidates and organizations.
5. Candidates complete projects and submit deliverables.
6. Both parties provide feedback, building trust and reputation.

## In Scope
- User registrations and profile creation
- Project posting by organizations
- Basic matchmaking functionality
- Messaging system for communication
- Deliverable submission process
- Feedback and reputation rating system

## Out of Scope
- Advanced matching algorithms
- In-depth analytics for companies or candidates
- Extensive manual or automated vetting processes
- Support for large corporations or complex project management capabilities

## Business Model Hypothesis
SkillBridge will operate on a commission basis, charging small organizations a fee for successfully completed projects. This model leverages the value derived from filling the experience gap for candidates while providing a low-cost solution for companies.

## Critical Assumptions
1. Recent graduates and career switchers are willing to sign up for a platform that provides short-term project opportunities.
2. Small organizations will find value in providing short-term projects rather than employing interns or freelancers.
3. Both candidates and organizations will adhere to the feedback system, helping build trust.

## How To Test Quickly
Conduct interviews and surveys with recent graduates to confirm their willingness to participate in this type of platform. Additionally, test the platform's ability to attract small organizations by conducting outreach to a small sample of local businesses and nonprofits to gauge their interest in posting projects.

## Acceptance Criteria
- At least 100 registered users (50 graduates and 50 organizations) by launch.
- A minimum of 10 projects posted within the first month post-launch.
- Positive user feedback from both candidates and organizations regarding the platform usability.

## Risks And Failure Modes
- Low adoption by candidates or organizations due to lack of perceived value.
- Quality control issues leading to negative experiences that could damage platform reputation.
- Potential legal challenges surrounding employment practices and project classifications.

## Product Readiness
Status: LIMITED

Blocking Gaps:
- Uncertainty about the legal framework regarding short-term projects in various jurisdictions.
- Lack of detailed feedback mechanisms to ensure quality control of projects.

Required Improvements:
- Validate candidate interest through direct engagement with targeted user groups.
- Develop initial guidelines addressing legal considerations to establish a proper framework for project postings.

## Recommendation
Develop an MVP focused on connecting recent graduates with small organizations for short-term projects. First, validate the concept by testing interest with both target user segments through manual outreach before investing in a full build-out. This approach will mitigate risks associated with user adoption and legal implications while enabling iterative improvements based on user feedback.
