# Evaluation CEO - SkillBridge Version 1

Sources used: `prd.md`, `architecture.md`, `architecture.pdf`, `gtm.md`.

## Final Decision: REVISE

## Global Note: 6/10

## Executive Verdict

SkillBridge addresses a real two-sided problem: graduates need practical experience, and small organizations need affordable project help. The wedge around structured short-term projects is credible.

The project is not approved yet because legal classification, quality control, supply acquisition, and monetization are unresolved.

## Business Viability

The problem is real on both sides, but the marketplace has a cold-start issue. Graduates will not join without quality projects, and organizations will not post unless they trust candidate quality and compliance.

The proposal correctly prioritizes small organizations first. That should remain the first constraint.

## Profitability Potential

Commission on completed projects is plausible, but the economics may be thin if projects are small and require manual vetting.

The proposal lacks pricing, take rate, expected project size, dispute handling cost, support cost, and repeat usage assumptions from businesses.

## Execution Complexity

The technical system is straightforward: user profiles, project posting, applications, matching, messaging, deliverables, feedback, and admin review.

The architecture and PDF appropriately include review states, project approval, feedback, compliance monitoring, audit trails, and admin dashboards. The operational review burden is a meaningful cost.

## Go-To-Market Realism

Founder-led outreach to 50 local businesses with a target of 5 initial project posters is realistic.

The graduate side should be sourced through universities, career centers, bootcamps, and alumni communities after the first verified projects exist.

## Competitive Risk

Competition is significant: internships, apprenticeships, Upwork, Fiverr, volunteer boards, university career centers, and niche talent platforms.

SkillBridge can differentiate through structured, vetted, beginner-friendly projects, but only if quality control is strong.

## Major Risks

- Legal ambiguity around labor classification.
- Candidates are exploited through unpaid or underpaid work.
- Businesses post low-quality projects.
- Manual vetting becomes a bottleneck.
- Graduates sign up but do not get meaningful outcomes.
- Take rate is too low to cover operations.

## Final Recommendation

Revise.

Run a manually operated pilot with 5-10 vetted businesses and a small graduate cohort. Prove project completion, satisfaction, and repeat business demand before building the full platform.
