# CEO Evaluation - SkillBridge - Version 1

## 1. Business viability
The problem is real: entry-level candidates need credible proof-of-work, and startups need bounded execution support. The Paris-only, single-mission-family wedge is appropriately narrow and testable.  
However, the core viability risk is legal and ethical framing in France: if this is perceived as unpaid labor extraction, the business collapses quickly.

## 2. Profitability potential
Revenue potential exists on the startup side (posting, placement, or subscription), but not yet in the pilot phase. The proposal correctly prioritizes proof before monetization.  
Near-term profitability is weak because moderation, matching, and trust operations are labor-heavy. Unit economics are currently unproven.

## 3. Execution complexity
Execution complexity is medium-high for an MVP because trust quality depends on human moderation, legal guardrails, and consistent proof artifact quality.  
The technical architecture is reasonable (controlled workflow + admin console), but operational complexity is the real bottleneck.

## 4. Go-to-market realism
Founder-led, supply-first outreach to trusted Paris startups is realistic for an initial pilot. Candidate acquisition through universities/bootcamps is also plausible.  
The GTM remains credible only if the team tightly controls mission quality and avoids broad marketplace behavior too early.

## 5. Competitive risk
Competitive pressure is high from existing alternatives (internships, freelance platforms, referrals, project communities).  
Defensibility is currently low unless SkillBridge proves superior trust controls and creates recognized proof-of-work credibility with employers.

## Major risks
- Legal exposure around unpaid/work-like missions in France
- Abuse risk from vague or exploitative postings
- Weak proof-of-work credibility with hiring managers
- Heavy manual moderation reducing speed and margin
- Marketplace drift into generic low-trust freelancing

## 6. Final investment decision
**REVISE**

## 7. Global note
**6/10**

## Final recommendation
Do not approve full investment yet. Fund a tightly scoped concierge pilot only if three conditions are met: legal framing validated, moderation quality consistently enforced, and proof artifacts shown to influence real hiring outcomes.  
If these conditions fail, stop before scaling.
