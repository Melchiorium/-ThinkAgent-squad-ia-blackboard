## Go-To-Market Notes
- **Main Market Bottleneck**: Building trust and perceived value among both recent graduates and small businesses while preventing the perception of a low-quality freelance marketplace.
- **Side of the Market to Secure First**: Small organizations must be secured first, as their willingness to post projects directly influences graduate interest in the platform.
- **Initial Target Audience**: Narrow the audience to small local businesses (5-20 employees) seeking affordable help.
- **Positioning**: SkillBridge positions itself as a reliable bridge between organizations needing assistance with short projects and graduates needing real-world experience, leveraging the structured feedback system to enhance trust.
- **First Acquisition Motion**: Conduct a manual outreach campaign targeting local small businesses and organizations, explaining the platform’s value proposition and offering free pilot projects for initial engagement.
- **Operating Assumptions for the First Acquisition Motion**: Reach out to at least 50 local businesses, expecting a 20% response rate (10 interested), and of those, anticipate that 50% (5 businesses) will post projects within the first month.
- **Switching Trigger**: The promise of receiving quality, vetted, short-term project help without long-term commitments, positioning SkillBridge as a more reliable and less risky alternative to traditional internship and freelancing platforms.
- **First Activation Loop**: Implement a project completion and feedback submission process to encourage repeat usage. Graduates should be incentivized to apply for multiple projects within a 2-month timeframe, with targeted emails reminding them of new postings.
- **What Must Exist Before Public Launch**: At least 5 small businesses actively posting projects, along with initial trust-building measures established (user feedback system in place).

## Review Summary
The main challenge for SkillBridge's launch is building trust among both recent graduates and small organizations while demonstrating clear value. The recommended GTM direction focuses on securing small local businesses first through targeted outreach and free pilot projects, gradually emerging as a reliable platform for bridging experience gaps.

## Critical Assumptions
1. Small businesses are willing to engage with a platform that charges minimal fees for posting short projects.
2. Graduates are actively seeking structured experiences that align with their job market needs.
3. Successful project completion leads to positive feedback, reinforcing trust in the system.
4. Legal frameworks regarding short-term projects are navigable with clear guidelines.
5. An initial cohort of small businesses will produce high-quality projects, encouraging repeat participation.

## Requested Changes
1. Develop guidelines around legal considerations for using the platform to ensure compliance.
2. Design a simple but effective feedback system to track candidate and project quality.
3. Implement features that allow easy visibility of user feedback to build trust.
4. Formulate an onboarding process for businesses that includes project quality checks and clarifications.
5. Craft a clear communication strategy for upcoming outreach efforts to businesses.

## Risks
1. Low initial engagement rates from small businesses might create a lack of projects.
2. Poor quality of projects could lead to negative user experiences and reputational harm.
3. Gradual realization of complex legal implications post-launch, affecting business operations.
4. Insufficient differentiation from existing freelance platforms may hinder user acquisition.
5. Competition from other internship platforms or freelance marketplaces could stifle growth.

## Open Questions
1. What specific legal guidelines need to be established for project postings across different jurisdictions?
2. How can we best ensure that the feedback system effectively builds trust and is adhered to by all users?
3. What metrics will we use to validate that businesses value short-term projects enough to invest in posting them?
4. How do we plan to recruit early candidates if the businesses have not posted projects yet?
5. What resources will be allocated for initial outreach efforts, and what metrics will determine success?

## Why This Could Fail Even With Good Execution
Even with competent execution, the market may reject the product if the fundamental assumption that small businesses value short-term projects over traditional hires does not hold true, resulting in low engagement from both sides.

## GTM Readiness
Status: LIMITED

Blocking Gaps:
- Uncertainty regarding legal frameworks and compliance associated with short-term project engagement.

Required Improvements:
- Conduct thorough validation with potential users (both businesses and graduates) for receptiveness.
- Establish basic guidelines related to legal and operational frameworks before pursuing broader outreach initiatives.
