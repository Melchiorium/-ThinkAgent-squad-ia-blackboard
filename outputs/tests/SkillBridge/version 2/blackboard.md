## Project Brief

Project Name: SkillBridge

Pitch:
A digital platform designed to help young graduates and career switchers gain practical experience through short-term real-world projects proposed by small companies, startups, and non-profits.

Context:
Many graduates struggle to get hired because they lack professional experience.
At the same time, small organizations often need help on short projects but cannot afford full-time hires or expensive agencies.

The platform aims to connect both sides by allowing organizations to publish short missions (2 to 8 weeks) that candidates can complete remotely or on-site.

Target Users:
- Recent graduates
- Career changers
- Small businesses
- Startups
- Associations / NGOs

Potential Use Cases:
- UX audit for a small startup
- Social media campaign for a local business
- Data cleanup project
- Website redesign
- Market research mission
- Administrative process optimization

Platform Capabilities:
- User profiles
- Project posting
- Matching system
- Messaging
- Deliverable submission
- Feedback and reputation system

Constraints:
- Limited initial budget
- Need to build trust between both sides
- Must avoid becoming a low-quality freelance marketplace
- Legal/employment boundaries may vary depending on country

Challenges:
- Attract enough companies early on
- Ensure project quality
- Prevent abuse from companies looking for free labor
- Create enough value for candidates to stay engaged

Long-term Vision:
Become an alternative path to traditional internships and junior hiring by helping people build credible experience faster.

# SkillBridge Product Proposal

## Product Problem
Young graduates and career switchers often face a significant barrier to employment due to a lack of practical experience. Simultaneously, small organizations, startups, and non-profits seek assistance on short-term projects but lack the financial resources to hire full-time staff or engage expensive agencies.

## Initial Wedge
The narrowest credible MVP wedge is creating a platform that connects young graduates and career switchers with small organizations looking for assistance on short-term projects, thus facilitating practical experience without a significant financial burden on the organizations.

## First Target User
Recent graduates will be the initial target user for the MVP as they constitute the primary group facing the problem of lack of experience.

## Existing Alternatives And Switching Trigger
Existing alternatives include unpaid internships and traditional job hunting methods. Users might switch to SkillBridge if it provides a streamlined way to gain practical experience in shorter time frames with verified projects, highlighting both the learning opportunity and a structured, reliable user experience.

## Core MVP Workflow
1. **User Registration**: Recent graduates create profiles.
2. **Project Posting**: Small businesses and nonprofits post projects needing assistance.
3. **Matching System**: The platform matches graduates with suitable projects based on skills and interests.
4. **Project Engagement**: Graduates take on projects, submit deliverables, and receive feedback.
5. **Reputation Building**: Completion of projects leads to feedback and ratings, establishing credibility.

## In Scope
- User profiles for graduates
- Project posting for organizations
- Basic matching system based on skills and project needs
- Messaging between graduates and organizations
- Deliverable submission mechanism
- Feedback system to build reputation

## Out of Scope
- Advanced matching algorithms
- In-depth vetting processes for users and projects
- Extensive legal compliance management for global employment laws
- Comprehensive marketing features (e.g., social media integration)
- Full-fledged freelance marketplace functionalities

## Business Model Hypothesis
The initial hypothesis is to operate a freemium model where organizations can post a limited number of projects for free, and subscriptions are available for premium features (e.g., highlighted project postings or enhanced visibility).

## Critical Assumptions
1. There is sufficient interest among recent graduates to use the platform for gaining experience.
2. Small organizations value access to short-term projects and view it as a viable solution rather than seeking free labor.
3. The matching system will be effective in producing relevant project placements.

## How To Test Quickly
- Conduct surveys and interviews with recent graduates to validate interest in the platform.
- Develop a landing page to gauge sign-up interest and collect emails.
- Launch a small pilot with a limited number of organizations posting projects to assess engagement.

## Acceptance Criteria
1. At least 100 graduate sign-ups within the first month.
2. Minimum of 10 organizations posting projects within the first month.
3. Successful matches with at least 3 projects completed within the pilot timeframe.
4. Maintain a satisfaction rate of over 70% from both graduates and organizations based on feedback collected.

## Risks And Failure Modes
- Insufficient graduate engagement leading to a lack of project completions.
- Small organizations not realizing the value of the platform and opting out.
- Potential exploitation of graduates through poorly defined project scopes.
- Compliance risks related to labor laws in varied countries.

Status: LIMITED

Blocking Gaps:
- Insufficient validation of user interest and engagement levels.
- Lack of established trust mechanisms to prevent exploitation.
- Undefined metrics for measuring project success and user satisfaction.

Required Improvements:
- Conduct targeted research to validate user interest among recent graduates.
- Develop a preliminary reputation system to foster trust and mitigate risks of abuse by companies.
- Define key metrics and feedback mechanisms to assess project quality and user satisfaction.

## Recommendation
Proceed with a concierge pilot to validate critical assumptions, gather user feedback, and refine the platform based on real user interactions. This approach minimizes risk while providing valuable insights for future development.
