# Evaluation CEO - SkillBridge Version 2

Sources used: `prd.md`, `architecture.md`, `architecture.pdf`, `gtm.md`.

## Final Decision: REVISE

## Global Note: 5/10

## Executive Verdict

SkillBridge v2 has a real thesis: graduates need practical experience and small organizations need short-term help. It correctly recommends a concierge pilot and project guidelines.

It is still not investable as a full MVP build. Legal risk, exploitation risk, quality control, and willingness to pay remain unresolved. The architecture PDF is also weaker than the Markdown, showing no explicit flow and no admin/ops control points despite those being essential.

## Business Viability

Candidate pain is obvious. Business-side demand is less proven. Small organizations may want help, but may not have time to supervise inexperienced talent or pay for it.

The target remains too broad: graduates/career switchers and small organizations/nonprofits. The launch needs one project category and one business segment.

## Profitability Potential

The freemium model for organizations is weak. Premium visibility or highlighted postings do not matter until there is meaningful candidate volume and project quality.

Missing proof: price, paid conversion, project value, repeat posting intent, vetting cost, support cost, legal cost.

## Execution Complexity

The Markdown architecture recommends a monolith, manual project review, admin dashboard, matching, messaging, submissions, feedback. That is reasonable but still too much before validation.

Manual matching should replace the matching engine in the pilot. The PDF's lack of flows/control points is a red flag because this product depends on project approval, compliance, and abuse prevention.

## Go-To-Market Realism

The GTM is supply-first and more concrete: 20 organizations posting, less than 2 hours onboarding, 10 organizations and 30 graduates before public launch.

Still, it lacks a selected industry, geography, compensation rule, and quality criteria. "Little to no cost" risks reinforcing the free-labor perception.

## Competitive Risk

High. Alternatives include internships, freelance platforms, university projects, volunteer platforms, alumni networks, and job boards.

SkillBridge can only differentiate through verified, scoped, outcome-producing projects.

## Major Risks

- Legal ambiguity around unpaid/low-paid work.
- Businesses seek free labor.
- Graduates see low employability value.
- Manual vetting becomes expensive.
- Matching system is premature.
- Freemium monetization is weak.

## Final Recommendation

Revise. Proceed only with a concierge pilot:

- one region
- one project category
- 10-15 manually recruited organizations
- clear compensation/legal framing
- manual project vetting
- manual matching
- measure completion, satisfaction, project quality, employability value, repeat posting intent, and willingness to pay

No full MVP until business-side value and legal structure are proven.
