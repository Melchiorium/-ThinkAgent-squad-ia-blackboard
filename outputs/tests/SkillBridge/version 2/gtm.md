## Go-To-Market Notes
- **Main Market Bottleneck**: Trust and perceived value in using SkillBridge, particularly from small organizations wary of exploitation and graduates skeptical about project quality.
- **Side of the Market to Secure First**: Focus on securing small businesses and nonprofits to post projects to ensure there is value for graduates before recruitment efforts ramp up.
- **Initial Target Audience**: Recent graduates, specifically those in fields where projects can easily translate skills (e.g., marketing, design, tech).
- **Positioning**: SkillBridge as a credible, efficient path for recent graduates to acquire practical experience while providing small organizations with essential project support at little to no cost.
- **First Acquisition Motion**: A targeted outreach campaign to small businesses and nonprofits in specific industries (e.g., local startups, community organizations) offering easy onboarding to post projects and ensure initial engagement.
- **Operating Assumptions for the First Acquisition Motion**: Aim for 20 organizations to post projects in the first month; streamline the onboarding process so it requires less than 2 hours of effort from each organization.
- **Switching Trigger**: Clear incentive defined as "work experience plus reviews" vs. traditional unpaid internships lacking structured support; emphasize completion of tangible projects that enhance a graduate’s employability.
- **First Activation Loop**: Graduates should complete projects and provide feedback which, in return, allows organizations to rate the graduates, creating a cycle of engagement and reputation building.
- **What Must Exist Before Public Launch**: At least 10 organizations with successful project posts and 30 graduate sign-ups with three successfully completed projects, along with feedback indicating a satisfaction rate above 70%.

## Review Summary
The primary challenge for SkillBridge is establishing trust and value from both organizations and recent graduates before launching at scale. Focus on securing small organizations first and validating project postings will create necessary credibility for the platform’s operations.

## Critical Assumptions
1. Small organizations will view the project model as a viable and attractive alternative to hiring.
2. Recent graduates will find value in participating in a structured project-based experience.
3. The onboarding process for organizations is minimal and smooth enough to attract sufficient project postings.
4. The first set of projects will be perceived as meaningful and effective for enhancing graduate employability.
5. Graduate participation rates will be high enough to ensure project completion and continuous feedback.

## Requested Changes
1. Develop clear onboarding guidelines and templates for organizations to make project posting easy and efficient.
2. Implement a feedback mechanism for both graduates and organizations after each project that is simple and encourages honest sharing.
3. Construct a straightforward educational component within the platform presenting how to gain maximum value from the experience.
4. Include a vetting process for organizations to protect graduates from project exploitation—simple requirements could include reviewing the organization's background.
5. Enhance matched project recommendations for graduates based on their expressed interests and previous feedback.

## Risks
1. Low engagement from small organizations leads to insufficient projects for graduates to apply for.
2. Graduates perceiving the projects posted as low quality or irrelevant, leading to low participation and satisfaction rates.
3. Potential legal issues arising from the lack of clarity in the student-employer relationship concerning responsibilities and deliverables.
4. Difficulty in creating a reliable matching system without sufficient data from the initial user base to fine-tune recommendations.
5. High drop-off rates after initial project engagement due to poor user experience or unclear value propositions.

## Open Questions
1. What specific industries should be prioritized for project posting to maximize the initial success of the platform?
2. How will the platform ensure that the projects offered are high quality and provide real value to graduates?
3. What metrics will be most effective to measure project success and user satisfaction accurately?
4. Are there influential partners or local organizations that could help amplify initial outreach to small businesses?
5. What specific legal frameworks must be evaluated to ensure compliance and safety for graduates and organizations across different markets?

## Why This Could Fail Even With Good Execution
Even with effective execution, if the initial project postings do not resonate well with the recent graduate audience, leading to low engagement and negative feedback, it could poison the perception of the platform and inhibit future growth and scaling efforts.

## GTM Readiness
Status: LIMITED

- Unclear metrics for measuring project success and user satisfaction.

Required Improvements:
