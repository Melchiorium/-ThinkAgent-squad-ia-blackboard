# SkillBridge Product Proposal

## Product Problem
Young graduates and career switchers often face a significant barrier to employment due to a lack of practical experience. Simultaneously, small organizations, startups, and non-profits seek assistance on short-term projects but lack the financial resources to hire full-time staff or engage expensive agencies.

## Initial Wedge
The narrowest credible MVP wedge is creating a platform that connects young graduates and career switchers with small organizations looking for assistance on short-term projects, thus facilitating practical experience without a significant financial burden on the organizations.

## First Target User
Recent graduates will be the initial target user for the MVP as they constitute the primary group facing the problem of lack of experience.

## Existing Alternatives And Switching Trigger
Existing alternatives include unpaid internships and traditional job hunting methods. Users might switch to SkillBridge if it provides a streamlined way to gain practical experience in shorter time frames with verified projects, highlighting a structured, reliable user experience.

## Core MVP Workflow
1. **User Registration**: Recent graduates create profiles that include skills and preferences.
2. **Project Posting**: Small businesses and nonprofits post projects needing assistance, guided by established project guidelines to prevent exploitation.
3. **Matching System**: The platform matches graduates with suitable projects based on skills and interests.
4. **Project Engagement**: Graduates take on projects, submit deliverables, and receive feedback.
5. **Reputation Building**: Completion of projects leads to the generation of feedback and ratings, establishing credibility.

## In Scope
- User profiles for graduates, prominently displaying skills and preferences.
- Project posting for organizations, guided by clear and explicit initial guidelines to prevent exploitation.
- Basic matching system based on skills and project needs, emphasizing user authentication requirements.
- Messaging between graduates and organizations.
- Deliverable submission mechanism.
- Initial version of a reputation system to foster trust and user interactions.
- Simple onboarding guidelines for organizations to facilitate project posting.

## Out of Scope
- Advanced matching algorithms.
- In-depth vetting processes for users and projects beyond initial guidelines.
- Comprehensive legal compliance management for global employment laws.
- Extensive marketing features (e.g., social media integration).
- Full-fledged freelance marketplace functionalities.
- Detailed feedback mechanisms beyond initial ratings and comments.

## Business Model Hypothesis
The initial hypothesis is to operate a freemium model where organizations can post a limited number of projects for free, and subscriptions are available for premium features (e.g., highlighted project postings or enhanced visibility).

## Critical Assumptions
1. There is sufficient interest among recent graduates to use the platform for gaining experience.
2. Small organizations value access to short-term projects and view it as a viable solution rather than seeking free labor.
3. The matching system will effectively produce relevant project placements.
4. Initial project guidelines will prevent exploitation and clarify expectations.

## How To Test Quickly
- Conduct surveys and interviews with recent graduates to validate interest in the platform.
- Develop a landing page to gauge sign-up interest and collect emails.
- Launch a small pilot with a limited number of organizations posting projects to assess engagement.
- Implement a basic mechanism for collecting user feedback post-project.

## Acceptance Criteria
1. At least 100 graduate sign-ups within the first month.
2. A minimum of 10 organizations posting projects within the first month, adhering to established guidelines.
3. Successful matches with at least 3 projects completed within the pilot timeframe.
4. Maintain a satisfaction rate of over 70% from both graduates and organizations based on feedback collected.

## Risks And Failure Modes
- Insufficient graduate engagement leading to a lack of project completions.
- Small organizations not realizing the value of the platform and opting out.
- Potential exploitation of graduates through poorly defined project scopes.
- Compliance risks related to labor laws in varied countries.

## Product Readiness
Status: LIMITED

Blocking Gaps:
- Insufficient validation of user interest and engagement levels.
- Lack of detailed metrics for measuring project success and user satisfaction.

Required Improvements:
- Conduct targeted research to validate user interest among recent graduates.
- Define straightforward metrics that assess project quality and facilitate reliable user satisfaction tracking.

## Recommendation
Proceed with a concierge pilot to validate critical assumptions, gather user feedback, and refine the platform based on real user interactions. This approach minimizes risk while providing valuable insights for future development.
