# Evaluation CEO - SkillBridge Version 3

Sources used: `prd.md`, `architecture.md`, `architecture.pdf`, `gtm.md`.

## Final Decision: REVISE

## Global Note: 6/10

## Executive Verdict

SkillBridge version 3 has a credible market problem and a pragmatic MVP: profiles, project postings, manual review, keyword matching, messaging, deliverables, and feedback.

The core opportunity is investable only after legal and supply-side validation. The current proposal is not ready for approval because the risk of low-quality or legally ambiguous work remains high.

## Business Viability

Recent graduates and career changers need portfolio-building opportunities. Small businesses and nonprofits may need affordable project support.

The challenge is trust. Graduates need proof that projects are legitimate and valuable; businesses need confidence that candidates can deliver.

## Profitability Potential

Fees per project or per successful match are plausible. A company subscription may work later for recurring project posters.

The proposal lacks unit economics: average project value, fee level, support cost, review cost, payment handling, dispute resolution, and repeat posting rate.

## Execution Complexity

The software build is moderate and feasible. Manual review is the right first operating model.

The PDF is weak relative to the markdown. It shows generic blocks and no explicit flows or admin controls, while the markdown depends heavily on manual review dashboards, compliance tooling, and feedback loops. This needs to be reconciled.

## Go-To-Market Realism

The GTM correctly secures businesses first and sets a public-launch threshold of 10 verified projects and 30 engaged graduates.

The 30% business conversion assumption is optimistic. Outreach must be tested with real business conversations and a concrete project template.

## Competitive Risk

Competition is high. Freelance platforms, internships, job boards, university programs, and volunteer opportunities already exist.

SkillBridge must own “safe, scoped, beginner-friendly, portfolio-worthy projects” as a specific trust promise.

## Major Risks

- Legal classification remains unclear.
- Projects become unpaid labor or exploitative work.
- Businesses do not provide enough quality opportunities.
- Graduates churn if they are not selected quickly.
- Manual review does not scale.
- Weak matching harms trust on both sides.

## Final Recommendation

Revise.

Run the first version almost manually: verified businesses, standard project templates, explicit legal/quality criteria, and direct matching. Build the platform only after repeated successful completions and evidence of business willingness to pay.
