## Go-To-Market Notes
- **Main market bottleneck:** Trust-building between graduates and organizations to ensure a high-quality experience.
- **Side of the market to secure first:** Small businesses and startups, as they are the ones posting projects, thus driving initial demand and content for the platform.
- **2 to 3 structural GTM decisions that shape the launch:**
  1. Prioritize onboarding a select group of small businesses with a defined outreach strategy to establish a quality initial project base.
  2. Implement a manual matching process initially to ensure quality matches between candidates and projects while the technology is refined.
  3. Introduce a "trusted partner" onboarding program for organizations to build credibility and establish quality standards.
- **Initial target audience:** Recent graduates seeking practical experience in relevant fields.
- **Positioning:** A platform for meaningful, project-based experiences that stand out against low-quality freelance marketplaces by ensuring structure and quality.
- **First acquisition motion:** Conduct targeted outreach to a limited number of local small businesses and startups for project postings through founder-led networking and direct outreach.
- **Operating assumptions for the first acquisition motion:** 
  - Convert at least 30% of contacted businesses to post projects.
  - Engage at least 50 graduates to ensure they are actively seeking credible opportunities.
- **Switching trigger:** Graduates will choose SkillBridge over existing alternatives if they perceive the opportunities as structured and clearly defined with potential long-term benefits.
- **First activation loop:** Enable graduates to create profiles, receive project notifications tailored to their skills, and submit applications for projects they find appealing, fostering engagement further through feedback on submissions.
- **What must exist before public launch:** A minimum of 10 verified project postings from small businesses and at least 30 engaged graduates who have completed profile setup.

## Review Summary
The main launch challenge lies in establishing trust between recent graduates and small businesses while ensuring project quality. The recommended GTM direction focuses on building a small base of trusted organizations and refining the matching process to create early traction within a niche audience.

## Critical Assumptions
1. Graduates are willing to engage with the platform for tangible project experience rather than traditional internships.
2. Small businesses see distinct value in using SkillBridge for low-cost project solutions.
3. The manual matching process adequately supports effective connections between candidates and projects.
4. Clear onboarding guidelines for businesses create initial credibility and quality assurance.
5. Graduates find the platform valuable enough to engage in multiple projects over time.

## Requested Changes
1. Develop an onboarding process for small businesses that includes guidelines for quality project creation and clear communication of expectations.
2. Create a manual review system of posted projects before they go live to filter out low-quality or illegitimate offerings.
3. Implement a structured feedback mechanism that provides immediate insights to businesses after project completions to ensure improvement.
4. Establish partnerships with local career centers or universities for direct access to the target graduate audience.
5. Add a feature allowing graduates to showcase completed projects to facilitate trust building with potential employers.

## Risks
1. Inadequate supply of quality projects from businesses leading to a poor graduate experience.
2. Potential legal misunderstandings regarding project-based work and labor laws in varying locations.
3. Graduates’ initial reception of the platform may be lukewarm if it lacks sufficient project variety.
4. Difficulty in maintaining high-quality matches as the platform scales without robust algorithmic support.
5. Trust issues may hinder user engagement if any negative experiences are reported.

## Open Questions
1. What specific outreach methods resonate most with targeted small businesses to encourage project postings?
2. How will the platform verify the legitimacy of the businesses posting projects?
3. What minimum legal protections will be established for both workers and businesses involved with SkillBridge?
4. How do we measure and quantify project quality and candidate success to maintain user trust?
5. What incentives can be introduced to encourage repeat participation from graduates?

## Why This Could Fail Even With Good Execution
Despite effective execution, if the underlying assumptions about the value proposition and user trust are incorrect, SkillBridge may struggle to maintain user engagement and project quality, leading to rapid attrition from both sides of the platform.
