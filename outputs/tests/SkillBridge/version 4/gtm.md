## Go-To-Market Notes

- **Main Market Bottleneck**: The primary bottleneck is attracting enough small businesses to post projects while also ensuring that the projects are valuable and high-quality, as initial credibility is crucial for user trust.

- **Side of the Market to Secure First**: Focus on securing small businesses and organizations first, as they are the supply side of the market. Without enough credible projects, it will be difficult to attract and retain graduates.

- **Structural GTM Decisions**:
  1. Prioritize pilot outreach efforts to a defined set of local small businesses with established professional networks.
  2. Limit recruitment of graduates initially to a focused geographic area or educational institutions to build localized trust.
  3. Create a curated project approval process to ensure quality and value for both sides of the platform.

- **Initial Target Audience**: Recent graduates interested in gaining practical experience, with an immediate focus on recent college graduates in nearby cities.

- **Positioning**: "SkillBridge connects motivated young professionals with small organizations for short-term projects, providing valuable hands-on experience that helps build careers."

- **First Acquisition Motion**: Launch a targeted outreach campaign to small businesses within a defined geographic area, leveraging local networks (e.g., universities, industry associations) to promote the platform.

- **Operating Assumptions for the First Acquisition Motion**:
  - Small businesses value affordable, realistic support for their short-term projects.
  - Graduate users are willing to engage and apply for projects if they perceive real value in the experience offered.

- **Switching Trigger**: The key motivation for both sides to switch to SkillBridge will be the promise of quality, curated short-term projects that lead to tangible skill development rather than low-quality freelance gigs.

- **First Activation Loop**: After a graduate applies and completes a project, they receive feedback, which reinforces their profile’s reputation. This positive experience encourages them to seek more projects and helps maintain engagement.

- **What Must Exist Before Public Launch**: 
  - A basic functioning platform with user profiles, project posting, a matching system, and messaging capabilities.
  - Clear guidelines for project quality standards and a feedback mechanism to foster trust.

- **Clear Distinction**:
  - **Must Be Productized Now**: Core user profiles, project posting functionality, basic matching algorithm, messaging system, deliverable submission, and initial feedback system.
  - **Can Stay Manual or Operational During Pilot**: Manual validation of project quality, outreach efforts to small businesses for initial engagement, and basic support for answering user questions.
  - **Deferred Until After Proof**: Enhanced matching features, in-depth analytics, mature community features, and advanced feedback mechanisms.

## Review Summary
The main challenge for SkillBridge's launch is securing small businesses while establishing trust and maintaining project quality. A focused, manual outreach strategy targeting local businesses should be prioritized, alongside a well-defined pilot program.

## Build Vs Pilot Operations

### Must Be Productized Now
- User profiles for graduates and organizations
- Project posting feature
- Basic matching algorithm
- Messaging system
- Deliverable submission
- Initial feedback and reputation system

### Can Stay Manual Or Operational During Pilot
- Basic support for project quality assurance
- Manual validation of projects and users to prevent abuse
- Outreach efforts to attract initial organizations

### Deferred Until After Proof
- Advanced matching features (e.g., skill-based recommendations)
- In-depth analytics or reporting tools
- Comprehensive feedback mechanisms
- Community-building features (forums, groups)

## Critical Assumptions
- Graduates will value short-term projects as viable experience.
- Small businesses will find the offering attractive and be willing to pay for project postings.
- The platform will effectively prevent abuse and maintain quality through a feedback system.

## Requested Changes
- Integrate a simple project quality assurance mechanism before launch, allowing manual checks.
- Develop a messaging template or onboarding guide for small businesses to improve engagement.
- Create a user-friendly FAQ section to address common concerns for graduates and organizations.
- Implement basic features for feedback collection to encourage interaction from both users.
- Establish a preliminary marketing plan focused on local networking and event participation to enhance credibility.

## Risks
- Lack of engagement from small businesses leading to insufficient projects for graduates.
- Difficulty attracting and retaining a steady pool of candidate users.
- Risk of organizations abusing the platform to solicit free labor.
- High competition for graduates from established freelance platforms.
- Initial lack of trust in the platform may deter users.

## Open Questions
- What specific incentives can be provided to attract small businesses during the initial launch phase?
- How will the platform manage legal boundaries related to employment in various regions?
- What steps will be taken to ensure high project quality and prevent abuse?
- Can partnerships with universities or organizations enhance credibility and trust?
- How will the platform handle disputes or feedback challenges between users?

## Why This Could Fail Even With Good Execution
SkillBridge may fail to achieve traction if the fundamental assumption that small businesses find value in posting short-term projects and trust the platform doesn't materialize, regardless of effective execution.
