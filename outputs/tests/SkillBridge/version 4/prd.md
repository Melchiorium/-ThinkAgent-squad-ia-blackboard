# SkillBridge Product Proposal

## Product Problem
Recent graduates and career switchers struggle to gain professional experience, making it difficult for them to secure employment. Simultaneously, small businesses and organizations need temporary support for short projects but lack the budget to hire full-time staff or expensive agencies.

## Initial Wedge
Connecting recent graduates with small businesses and startups through a platform that allows organizations to post short-term projects. The MVP will focus on the matching system and project posting functionalities.

## First Target User
Recent graduates seeking practical experience and skill development.

## Existing Alternatives And Switching Trigger
- Existing job boards (e.g., LinkedIn, Indeed) lacking focus on short-term projects.
- Freelance platforms (e.g., Upwork) that may not attract recent graduates due to competition with experienced freelancers.
- The switching trigger will be the unique offering of curated short-term projects directly aimed at skill development, alongside a structured feedback and reputation system.

## Core MVP Workflow
1. Organizations create profiles and post short-term projects.
2. Graduates create profiles highlighting their skills and preferences.
3. The platform matches candidates to relevant projects based on their profiles.
4. Graduates apply for projects and organizations choose candidates.
5. Communication and project deliverables are managed through the platform.
6. Post-project feedback is collected to build reputation.

## In Scope
- User profiles for graduates and organizations
- Project posting feature for organizations
- Basic matching algorithm
- Messaging capability for communication between users
- Deliverable submission feature
- Initial feedback and reputation system
- Simple onboarding flow for both graduates and organizations

## Out of Scope
- Advanced features such as payment processing or contract management
- Detailed analytics for organizations
- Enhanced reporting for graduates
- Customization of user profiles beyond the MVP requirements
- Community-building features (forums, groups)
- Complex onboarding processes that extend beyond the initial setup

## MVP Build Vs Pilot Operations
### Must Build Now
- User profiles for graduates and organizations
- Project posting feature
- Basic matching algorithm
- Messaging system
- Deliverable submission
- Initial feedback and reputation system
- Simple onboarding flow for effective initial user engagement

### Manual Or Operational During Pilot
- Basic support for project quality assurance
- Manual validation of projects and users to prevent abuse
- Outreach efforts to attract initial organizations

### Deferred Until After Proof
- Advanced matching features (e.g., skill-based recommendations)
- In-depth analytics or reporting tools
- Comprehensive feedback mechanisms
- User-friendly FAQ section

## Business Model Hypothesis
A commission-based model where organizations pay a fee to post projects, which can be scaled as the platform grows. Additionally, consider offering premium features for organizations seeking enhanced visibility.

## Critical Assumptions
- Graduates will value short-term projects as viable experience.
- Small businesses will find the offering attractive and be willing to pay for project postings.
- The platform will effectively prevent abuse and maintain quality through a feedback system.

## How To Test Quickly
- Conduct surveys with recent graduates to gauge interest in the concept.
- Pilot with a limited group of small businesses to validate project posting and the graduate engagement process.
- Measure the number of candidates applying to posted projects and gather qualitative feedback.

## Acceptance Criteria
- At least 10 organizations must successfully post projects.
- A minimum of 50 graduates must create profiles and engage with posted projects.
- Successful completion of at least 5 projects with feedback collected from both candidates and organizations.

## Risks And Failure Modes
- Lack of engagement from small businesses leading to insufficient projects for graduates.
- Difficulty attracting and retaining a steady pool of candidate users.
- Risk of organizations abusing the platform to solicit free labor.

## Product Readiness
Status: LIMITED

Blocking Gaps:
- Validating the demand for the project posting feature among small organizations.
- Establishing trust and quality assurance measures to prevent abuse.

Required Improvements:
- Simplified user onboarding processes to guide both graduates and organizations through initial setup and engagement. [onboarding]
