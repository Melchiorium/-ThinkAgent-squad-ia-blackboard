## Go-To-Market Notes

- **Main Market Bottleneck:** The need to validate that singles prioritize music compatibility as a crucial factor for dating.
  
- **Side of the Market to Secure First:** Focus on single music enthusiasts who are actively seeking meaningful connections based on shared musical tastes.

- **Initial Target Audience:** Singles aged 20-35 who identify deeply with their music preferences and are engaged in local music scenes.

- **Positioning:** Melody is a dating platform that leverages music compatibility to foster deeper personal connections, positioning itself as a companion to traditional dating apps but focusing on shared lifestyle preferences around music.

- **First Acquisition Motion:** Leverage social media and music communities (like Facebook groups or local music forums) to attract existing music lovers by highlighting music compatibility features and the uniqueness of the platform.

- **Operating Assumptions for the First Acquisition Motion:** Aim for a minimum of 300 sign-ups in the first month with a targeted conversion rate of 5-10% from outreach efforts; expect about 30% of these users to complete the onboarding process.

- **Switching Trigger:** Users will choose Melody over existing platforms if they perceive it as providing a significantly deeper connection through shared music tastes, coupled with concert discovery that traditional dating apps lack.

- **First Activation Loop:** Engaging users through the onboarding process followed by personalized concert recommendations that lead to messaging matches; aim for users to message at least one match within their first week of signing up.

- **What Must Exist Before Public Launch:** A tested and refined onboarding process that successfully gathers music preferences, a functioning compatibility scoring algorithm, and a basic messaging feature that implicitly encourages interaction.

## Review Summary
Melody's main launch challenge lies in validating the importance of music compatibility for singles seeking relationships. The recommended GTM direction focuses on attracting single music enthusiasts via targeted social outreach while ensuring the viability of the core features within the user onboarding process.

## Critical Assumptions
1. Singles value music compatibility as a significant factor in dating.
2. The market has enough demand for a dedicated music-focused dating platform.
3. Users will find concert discovery and musical compatibility features engaging.
4. Social media and music communities provide an effective acquisition channel.
5. Basic messaging functionality will encourage user interaction within the app.

## Requested Changes
1. Implement a simple survey during onboarding to directly ask users how much they value music compatibility in dating, along with potential interest in other features.
2. Develop and test a basic compatibility scoring algorithm before launch to ensure it delivers meaningful matches.
3. Integrate concert discovery functionality immediately after onboarding to improve the activation loop.
4. Create a landing page to track sign-up interest and gather initial music preferences for early user insights.
5. Simplify the messaging feature to encourage engagement by implementing prompts to initiate conversations.

## Risks
1. Low user engagement if the platform fails to deliver valuable matches based on music compatibility.
2. Technical issues in developing an accurate compatibility scoring system.
3. Strong competition from established dating platforms might overshadow Melody's unique value proposition.
4. Challenges in effectively sourcing and validating music preference data.
5. User retention risks if the app lacks compelling ongoing engagement features beyond initial matches.

## Open Questions
1. What specific data services can be leveraged to source reliable music preference data?
2. How will we measure users’ satisfaction with the compatibility scores?
3. What community partners can we collaborate with to boost early adoption?
4. What metrics will most closely indicate user engagement and retention post-launch?
5. Are there any regions or demographics within the target audience we should prioritize?

## Why This Could Fail Even With Good Execution
If Melody effectively executes its launch strategy but finds that the target audience does not prioritize music compatibility as anticipated, the app may fail to capture it’s initial user base, leading to limited engagement and ultimately, poor retention.
