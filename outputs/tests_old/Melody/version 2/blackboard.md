## Project Brief

Project Name: Melody

Pitch:
A social and dating platform built around music compatibility.

The app helps music lovers connect through shared musical tastes, discover new artists, attend concerts together, find band members, and participate in local music communities.

Context:
Music plays a central role in many people's identity, relationships, and social lives. Traditional dating apps rarely account for deep lifestyle compatibility, and music communities are fragmented across multiple platforms.

Melody aims to combine relationship matching, music discovery, and community interaction into one platform.

Target Users:
- Music enthusiasts
- Amateur musicians
- Concert-goers
- Singles looking for deeper compatibility
- People looking for band members
- Music communities

Potential Use Cases:
- Dating based on music compatibility
- Finding concert partners
- Discovering new artists
- Finding musicians to jam with
- Buying/selling music equipment
- Joining local music communities

Platform Capabilities:
- Music taste onboarding
- Compatibility scoring
- Messaging
- Concert discovery
- Community groups
- Music marketplace
- Artist recommendations

Constraints:
- Need access to music preference data
- Must avoid becoming "just another dating app"
- Need strong early retention loop
- Limited initial funding

Challenges:
- Building enough user density
- Prioritizing features for MVP
- Competing with existing dating/social platforms
- Defining a sustainable business model

Long-term Vision:
Become the leading social ecosystem for people whose identity is deeply tied to music.

## Initial PRD

# Melody MVP Proposal

## Product Problem
Traditional dating apps fail to address deep lifestyle compatibility by focusing primarily on superficial attributes. This results in missed connections among music enthusiasts who desire meaningful interactions based on shared musical interests and values. Furthermore, music communities are fragmented across various platforms, making it difficult for users to engage effectively.

## Initial Wedge
The initial wedge for the MVP will be the dating functionality based on music compatibility, connecting singles who share similar music tastes. This focuses on building a core user base that values deep compatibility through music.

## First Target User
The first target user will be singles aged 18-35 who are passionate about music and are looking for meaningful relationships based on mutual musical tastes.

## Existing Alternatives And Switching Trigger
Current alternatives include traditional dating apps (e.g., Tinder, Bumble) and social music platforms (e.g., Spotify, Last.fm), which do not provide deep compatibility matching. The main switching trigger is the ability to find romantic matches based on shared music preferences, enabling users to engage with potential partners through their love for music.

## Core MVP Workflow
1. **Music Taste Onboarding**: Users input their top artists and genres.
2. **Compatibility Scoring**: The app generates compatibility scores based on shared music interests.
3. **User Profiles**: Users can view potential matches based on music compatibility and shared interests.
4. **Messaging**: Basic chat functionality to allow users to connect.
5. **Concert Discovery**: Suggest local concerts based on user preferences and compatibility.

## In Scope
- Music taste onboarding
- Compatibility scoring
- User profiles
- Basic messaging
- Concert discovery

## Out of Scope
- Music marketplace features
- Community groups
- Finding band members
- Buying/selling music equipment
- Artist recommendations

## Business Model Hypothesis
The primary revenue model will be freemium, with basic functionality available for free and premium features (e.g., advanced matchmaking algorithms, concert perks) offered via a subscription service. Initially, revenue could also come from partnerships with local concert venues for advertising.

## Critical Assumptions
- Users will find a matching dating experience based on music preferences appealing enough to switch from traditional dating apps.
- Users will engage consistently with the platform due to music-related events and interactions.
- Access to music preference data will be feasible and legal.

## How To Test Quickly
- Conduct surveys among target users to validate interest in music-based dating.
- Test the music onboarding and compatibility scoring mechanisms with a small group through a concierge pilot.
- Launch a landing page that explains the value proposition and collects emails for interested users.

## Acceptance Criteria
- 1000 users onboarded in the first month.
- At least 500 active matches (conversations initiated) within the first month of launching.
- A minimum of 25% retention of users after 1 month.

## Risks And Failure Modes
- Low user adoption due to competition with established dating platforms.
- Difficulty in maintaining a sufficient user base to ensure effective matching.
- Potential legal issues around accessing music preference data.
- Underwhelming user engagement leading to high churn rates.
