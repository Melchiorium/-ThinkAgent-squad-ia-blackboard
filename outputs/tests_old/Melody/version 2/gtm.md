## Go-To-Market Notes

- **Main Market Bottleneck:** Establishing deep music-based compatibility as a primary differentiator from traditional dating apps, which focus on superficial attributes.
  
- **Side of the Market to Secure First:** Singles aged 18-35 who are passionate about music and are seeking deeper compatibility through shared music interests.

- **Initial Target Audience:** A narrow audience segment of 200-500 music enthusiasts in local metropolitan areas who are currently actively seeking romantic connections.

- **Positioning:** Position Melody as the go-to dating platform that unites music lovers by fostering meaningful connections based on music preferences and lifestyle compatibility rather than surface-level attributes.

- **First Acquisition Motion:** A manual, founder-led outreach targeting local music communities, colleges, and social media groups, combined with partnerships with local music venues to host meet-ups.

- **Operating Assumptions for the First Acquisition Motion:** 
  - Convert 10% of interested users from outreach, aiming for 20-50 users engaged in the pilot phase.
  - Host 1-2 community meet-up events with 100-200 attendees to generate buzz and gather more users for meaningful engagement.

- **Switching Trigger:** The primary reason for users to switch would be the ability to discover and connect with potential partners through shared music tastes and experiences, which traditional dating apps do not offer.

- **First Activation Loop:** Users engage with the app by completing their music taste onboarding, receiving matches, and initiating conversations based on shared music interests, with retention driven by concert discovery notifications and messaging interactions.

- **What Must Exist Before Public Launch:** A confirmed legal framework for accessing music preference data, a working MVP featuring basic functionality (onboarding, compatibility scoring, messaging, concert suggestions), and at least 100 active users from a successful concierge pilot phase.

## Review Summary
The main launch challenge for Melody lies in differentiating itself from traditional dating apps while building a critical mass of engaged users around music compatibility. A targeted, manual acquisition approach focused on local music enthusiasts is recommended to validate the concept and gain traction.

## Critical Assumptions
- Users prefer a music-centered matching experience over traditional dating apps.
- Engagement will remain high as users share experiences related to music events and dating.
- Legal access to music preference data is achievable and straightforward.
- Community-driven events will effectively stimulate user acquisition and retention.
- The MVP functionalities will effectively address user needs and drive engagement.

## Requested Changes
- Integrate legal consultations to ensure compliance in accessing music preference data.
- Refine onboarding to reduce friction and enhance user experience, possibly through gamification of the music preference selection process.
- Include basic analytics to track user engagement metrics from the start.
- Develop an initial set of community-building events as a foundational strategy for user acquisition.
- Add clear user safety protocols in messaging to build trust in the platform from day one.

## Risks
- Competition from established dating platforms may stifle user adoption.
- Inability to maintain sufficient user density for effectiveness in matching.
- Legal complications could arise from accessing user music preference data.
- Low engagement rates post-launch could drive higher churn.
- The needs of target users may shift or become unclear during the pilot phase.

## Open Questions
- What types of local music venues or communities would be most effective to partner with for the initial outreach?
- What specific feedback mechanisms will be set up during the concierge pilot to ensure actionable insights?
- How will the initial messaging features ensure a safe and engaging user experience while scaling?
- What specific legal hurdles must be navigated regarding music data access?
- How can we incentivize early pilot users to provide ongoing feedback and contribute to community building?

## Why This Could Fail Even With Good Execution
Even with solid execution, Melody may fail if the assumption that users prioritize music compatibility when seeking meaningful relationships does not hold true, leading to limited engagement and willingness to switch from established platforms.
