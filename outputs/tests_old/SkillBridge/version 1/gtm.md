## Go-To-Market Notes
- **Main Market Bottleneck:** Establishing trust and quality assurance to attract both candidates and businesses to the platform.
- **Side of the Market to Secure First:** Small businesses that are willing to post short-term projects.
- **Initial Target Audience:** Recent graduates looking for practical experience in specific fields (e.g., marketing, UX, research).
- **Positioning:** A structured opportunity for graduates to gain professional experience through defined, short-term projects with small businesses, unlike traditional internships which may require long-term commitments.
- **First Acquisition Motion:** Manual outreach to local small businesses and business networks to gather a pilot batch of projects while simultaneously marketing to recent graduate communities (universities, career centers).
- **Operating Assumptions for the First Acquisition Motion:** Secure 10 small businesses to list projects before launch, with at least 50 graduates expressing interest in these projects—assuming a conversion rate of 20% interest to signups.
- **Switching Trigger:** The structured nature of projects and the appeal of demonstrable experience that enhances resumes and LinkedIn profiles can incentivize users to choose SkillBridge over existing alternatives.
- **First Activation Loop:** Upon project acceptance, a feedback system activated by both graduates and businesses to exchange reviews can enhance trust and encourage further user engagement, ideally driving repeat usage for project listing and application.
- **What Must Exist Before Public Launch:** A minimum viable number of 10 project listings from small businesses and 50 candidate registrations with verified skills/interests.

## Review Summary
The main launch challenge is establishing trust and quality offerings within a limited budget, highlighting the necessity to secure small business participation before engaging candidates. A manual outreach strategy focused initially on local markets is recommended to test the viability of the concept before a broader launch.

## Critical Assumptions
1. Small businesses perceive value in offering short-term projects and are willing to participate without upfront costs.
2. Recent graduates see value in unpaid experiences tied to resume enhancement and skill acquisition.
3. A mutual trust can be built through a feedback system that deters misuse of the platform.
4. There are legal frameworks in place that permit unpaid projects in the relevant regions.
5. Sufficient engagement from both sides will lead to positive qualitative user metrics.

## Requested Changes
1. Develop clear guidelines and resources on the legal implications of unpaid projects to ensure compliance across various countries before launch.
2. Create a manual vetting process for project submissions to maintain quality and build trust.
3. Develop targeted outreach materials (e.g., presentations, flyers) for businesses outlining the win-win of posting projects on SkillBridge.
4. Implement a defined pilot testing phase to fine-tune the matching process between graduates and businesses before full-scale launch.
5. Add a simple onboarding guide for businesses on how to create project listings effectively.

## Risks
1. The absence of early engagement from small businesses may stall platform traction.
2. Poor project quality control leading to negative experiences from graduates could harm reputation early on.
3. Legal ambiguities regarding unpaid project work could expose the platform to vulnerabilities.
4. High turnover with initial users resulting in insufficient data to build a reliable reputation system.
5. Misalignment between candidate expectations and what small businesses require in terms of deliverables.

## Open Questions
1. What specific legal considerations need to be addressed for unpaid projects in our target regions?
2. How can we effectively measure candidate engagement and satisfaction to iterate on our offerings?
3. What approach will we take to maintain the quality of projects while addressing potential concerns about companies seeking free help?
4. How do we ensure a robust onboarding process for both graduates and businesses to maximize initial user experience?
5. What resources can we leverage (partners, incubators, etc.) to mitigate trust issues and expedite user engagement?

## Why This Could Fail Even With Good Execution
Even with competent execution, if there is an inherent mismatch between the value graduates perceive in using SkillBridge and the offerings from businesses, there might be insufficient traction, leading to low engagement rates that hinder overall platform viability.
