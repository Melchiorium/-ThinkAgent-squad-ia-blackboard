# SkillBridge Product Proposal

## Product Problem
Many recent graduates and career switchers struggle to find employment due to a lack of practical experience. Simultaneously, small businesses, startups, and non-profits need assistance with short-term projects but lack the resources to hire full-time employees or expensive agencies. SkillBridge intends to bridge this gap by connecting these two groups through a streamlined digital platform.

## Initial Wedge
The narrowest credible MVP wedge will focus on connecting recent graduates seeking short-term work experience with small businesses that require project-based assistance.

## First Target User
The first target user segment will be recent graduates looking to gain practical experience in their field.

## Existing Alternatives And Switching Trigger
Current alternatives for candidates include internships, volunteering, and low-paying freelance gigs. The switching trigger will be the appeal of structured, time-limited projects clearly associated with skill development and professional experience that can enhance their resumes.

## Core MVP Workflow
1. **User Registration:** Graduates create profiles showcasing their skills, interests, and availability.
2. **Project Posting:** Small businesses submit brief descriptions of their short-term projects.
3. **Matching:** The platform uses a basic algorithm to match candidates with suitable projects based on their profiles.
4. **Communication:** A messaging system allows businesses and candidates to discuss project scope and expectations.
5. **Project Execution:** Candidates complete projects, submit deliverables, and receive feedback.
6. **Reputation System:** A feedback mechanism collects reviews from both candidates and businesses to build trust and quality assurance.

## In Scope
- User profiles for candidates
- Project posting functionality for small businesses
- Basic matching system based on skills and availability
- Messaging feature for communication
- Deliverable submission mechanism
- Initial feedback and reputation system
- Clear legal guidelines for unpaid project work across target markets
- Manual vetting process for project submissions to ensure quality

## Out of Scope
- Advanced algorithms for matching (beyond basic skill matching)
- Payment transaction systems (initial focus on volunteer, not employment)
- Mobile application at launch
- Extensive legal agreements beyond basic terms of service
- Onboarding materials and guides (to be developed later)

## Business Model Hypothesis
SkillBridge will operate under a commission-based model, charging small businesses a fee for project postings once a matching candidate is accepted, while remaining free for candidates.

## Critical Assumptions
1. There is a sufficient demand among small businesses for short-term project assistance.
2. Recent graduates are willing to invest their time in unpaid or low-paid projects to gain experience.
3. Trust can be established via a feedback system and a vetted submission process to prevent abuse on both sides.
4. The business segment can be attracted to post projects without high costs.

## How To Test Quickly
- Conduct interviews and surveys with recent graduates to gauge interest in the platform.
- Outreach to small businesses to validate the demand for short-term project support.
- Prototype the key functionalities (posting projects, matching candidates) and gather early feedback.

## Acceptance Criteria
- Minimum of 50 project submissions from businesses within the first 3 months of launch.
- At least 100 candidate registrations within the same timeframe.
- Average project rating must be above 4 out of 5 within the pilot period.
- User engagement metrics indicating satisfactory communication (e.g., message response rate).

## Risks And Failure Modes
- Low engagement from either side (graduates or businesses), leading to a lack of project offerings.
- Quality control issues resulting in negative user experiences.
- Abuse of the system with businesses seeking free labor without fair compensation.

## Product Readiness
Status: LIMITED

Blocking Gaps:
- Uncertainty surrounding the legal and employment implications across different markets.
- Lack of verified success metrics for both candidates and businesses.

Required Improvements:
- Conduct thorough research regarding legal constraints and necessary agreements, focusing on core compliance requirements.
- Establish a manual quality assurance process for project submissions to maintain trust and quality.
- Development of clear metrics for assessing project success and candidate engagement.

## Recommendation
Proceed with validation efforts through manual testing and interviews (concierge pilot) to confirm demand and willingness to engage from both recent graduates and small businesses. Validate critical assumptions before moving toward MVP development. Additionally, implement a manual quality assurance process for project submissions and finalize user verification processes to build trust prior to platform launch.
