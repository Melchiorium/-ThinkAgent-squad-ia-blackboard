# Evaluation Report - SkillBridge Version 2

## Final Decision: REVISE

## Global Note: 5/10

## Executive Verdict

SkillBridge Version 2 is a coherent early-stage proposal, but it is still not investable as a full MVP build. It correctly identifies the need for a concierge pilot, manual vetting, and supply-side validation with small organizations.

The core issue remains unresolved: the platform is built around unpaid or low-paid project work, which creates legal, ethical, quality, and reputational risk. The business model is also weakly supported. Small organizations are expected to pay for postings or premium features, but the proposal does not prove they have enough willingness to pay.

The right next step is a tightly scoped concierge pilot, not product build.

## Business Viability

The candidate-side problem is real. Graduates and career switchers need practical experience, portfolio material, feedback, and proof of ability.

The organization-side problem is plausible, but less proven. Small businesses and non-profits may need short-term help, but they may also lack time to supervise inexperienced candidates, define good projects, and provide meaningful feedback.

The wedge is still too broad. "Recent graduates/career switchers" and "small businesses/non-profits" cover too many needs and behaviors. The proposal mentions structured projects, but does not choose a specific project category such as marketing audits, UX research, data cleanup, or website improvements.

Without a sharper vertical, project quality will be hard to control.

## Profitability Potential

The freemium business model is not convincing yet.

The proposal says companies may pay a nominal fee after a posting limit or for premium analytics. This is weak for an early marketplace. Small organizations are described as budget-constrained, and premium analytics are unlikely to be compelling before there is significant candidate volume and hiring intent.

Missing profitability inputs:

- expected posting fee
- expected paid conversion rate
- repeat posting rate
- cost to acquire each organization
- cost to vet each project
- support/dispute cost
- legal/compliance cost
- value of a completed project to the organization

The platform may generate interest, but interest is not revenue. Profitability requires proof that organizations will repeatedly pay for structured access to junior talent.

## Execution Complexity

The technical build is moderate, but still too much for the risk profile. Profiles, project posting, matching, messaging, deliverables, feedback, reputation, admin review, support, and compliance processes are a broad MVP.

The architecture recommends a matching algorithm, but matching should be manual during the concierge pilot. The riskiest assumptions are market, legal, and quality-related, not algorithmic.

Manual vetting is correctly included, but the proposal does not define concrete quality criteria. That is a serious gap. If projects are poorly scoped, candidates will churn and the platform will be perceived as low-quality freelance or free labor.

The architecture is reasonable only if the team resists building too much before pilot validation.

## Go-To-Market Realism

The GTM is one of the stronger parts of the proposal. It correctly says the organization side must be secured first and proposes outreach to 50 target organizations with a goal of 10-15 pilot participants.

That is a concrete starting motion.

But it still needs more precision:

- which geography
- which organization type
- which project category
- what incentive for organizations
- what qualifies as a good pilot project
- who approves projects
- what happens if projects are unpaid
- what proves organizations will come back

The target of a 20% response rate may be optimistic unless the outreach list is warm or highly targeted. Universities and candidate communities can supply graduates, but candidates are not the scarce side.

## Competitive Risk

Competitive risk is high. SkillBridge competes with:

- internships
- freelance platforms
- volunteer platforms
- university project programs
- LinkedIn and alumni networks
- junior agencies
- job boards
- community-based matching

The product features are easily copied. Defensibility would need to come from trusted project quality, vetted organizations, strong candidate outcomes, and repeat organization participation.

The proposal has not yet shown that moat.

## Major Risks

- Legal ambiguity around unpaid or low-paid project work.
- Businesses use the platform for free labor rather than meaningful projects.
- Organizations do not pay for postings or premium analytics.
- Candidate supply outpaces quality project supply.
- Project quality is inconsistent without strict vetting criteria.
- Matching and messaging are built before manual process validation.
- Feedback ratings become low-signal or performative.
- Graduates do not gain employability value from completed projects.

## What Is Strong

- The proposal identifies supply-side acquisition as the first priority.
- Concierge pilot is the right next motion.
- Manual project vetting is included.
- Payment processing is excluded from MVP, reducing build complexity.
- Product, Tech, and GTM readiness are marked `LIMITED`, which is honest.
- GTM includes directional outreach numbers.

## What Must Improve

- Select one launch geography.
- Select one project category.
- Define compensation rules: paid, stipend-based, unpaid, or mixed.
- Define legal boundaries for the target region.
- Replace algorithmic matching with manual matching during the pilot.
- Define project quality criteria and rejection rules.
- Test willingness to pay directly with organizations.
- Add outcome metrics tied to employability and repeat organization usage.

## Final Recommendation

Revise before building a full MVP.

Proceed only with a concierge pilot under strict conditions:

- 10-15 organizations recruited from one narrow segment
- one category of project only
- all projects manually vetted before candidate access
- legal framing clarified for the pilot region
- manual matching and manual feedback collection
- explicit test of organization willingness to pay or repeat
- candidate outcomes measured beyond satisfaction
- completion rate, repeat posting intent, project quality, and referral intent tracked

SkillBridge Version 2 is heading in the right direction, but the business remains unproven and legally sensitive. The correct decision is `REVISE`.
