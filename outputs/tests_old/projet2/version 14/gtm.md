## Go-To-Market Notes
- **Primary Audience**: Recent graduates looking for practical experience.
- **Positioning**: SkillBridge is a trusted platform designed specifically for young graduates and career switchers to gain practical experience through short-term projects, making it a viable alternative to traditional internships.
- **First Acquisition Channel**: Leverage partnerships with universities and career services to promote the platform directly to recent graduates.
- **First Activation Idea**: Create a guided onboarding process that helps users complete their profiles and match with a project within their first week of signing up.
- **Early Success Indicators**: Achieving the first 100 project postings and 200 active candidates in the first month post-launch, with at least 75% profile completion rate among new users.

## Review Summary
The main growth challenge is building trust and attracting an initial base of both candidates and small organizations. The recommended GTM direction focuses on targeting recent graduates through university partnerships, while ensuring a streamlined onboarding process to enhance activation and engagement.

## Requested Changes
1. Implement a simplified onboarding checklist for candidates to complete their profiles and apply for projects.
2. Establish a verification process for organizations posting projects to build trust in the platform.
3. Include a clear guideline and standard for project quality and expectations in the project posting feature.
4. Develop a feedback mechanism specifically aimed at addressing concerns of candidates about project quality and company practices.
5. Introduce a limited-time incentive for organizations that post their first project, such as a discount or feature enhancement, to encourage initial engagement.

## Risks
1. The risk of low engagement from organizations if early project quality does not meet expectations, leading to distrust from candidates.
2. Potential for candidates to view the platform as a source of underpaid work or free labor, which can hinder long-term trust and platform reputation.
3. High churn rate of candidates if they fail to secure projects within a short time after signing up.

## Open Questions
1. What specific partnerships can be established with universities or career services to enhance initial user acquisition?
2. How will the platform handle variations in legal and employment regulations across different countries?
3. What measures will be put in place to prevent abuse of the platform by organizations seeking free labor?
