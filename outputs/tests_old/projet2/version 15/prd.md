# SkillBridge Product Proposal

## Product Problem
Young graduates and career switchers often lack practical experience, making it challenging for them to secure jobs. Simultaneously, small organizations need assistance with short-term projects but cannot afford to hire full-time employees or expensive agencies. SkillBridge aims to bridge this gap by providing a platform for both parties to collaborate on short-term projects.

## Initial Wedge
The initial wedge focuses on providing a validated experience for graduates and a reliable source of project assistance for small businesses. The objective is to demonstrate that there is a significant demand for short-term projects that can enhance employability while offering valuable contributions to companies.

## First Target User
The first target user for the launch will be **recent graduates actively seeking internships or short-term work experience**.

## Core MVP Workflow
1. **User Onboarding**: Recent graduates create profiles highlighting their skills and interests.
2. **Project Posting**: Small businesses and startups post short-term projects.
3. **Matching System**: Graduates receive notifications for projects matching their skills.
4. **Application Process**: Graduates apply for relevant projects via the platform.
5. **Project Execution**: Graduates complete projects, submit deliverables via templates, and receive feedback.

## In Scope
- User profiles for graduates and organizations.
- Project posting interface for organizations.
- Basic matching system based on skills and project requirements.
- Messaging system for communication between users.
- Deliverable submission feature with standardized templates.
- Feedback and reputation system that includes a mechanism for users to report issues or abuses.

## Out of Scope
- Complex matching algorithms.
- Advanced user profile features (e.g., endorsements, dynamic portfolios).
- Extensive project management tools.
- Comprehensive legal compliance or detailed employment categorization per country.
- Marketing tools for businesses.
- Case study templates or extensive communication strategies.

## Critical Assumptions
1. Graduates are willing to engage in short-term projects for minimal or no pay to gain experience.
2. Small businesses are willing to post projects on the platform.
3. The platform can establish a trust-based environment to prevent issues of companies seeking free labor.
4. Graduates will return to the platform for multiple projects.

## How To Test Quickly
- Conduct a survey among recent graduates to gauge interest in short-term projects for experience.
- Recruit a small cohort of businesses willing to post trial projects and observe engagement levels.
- Analyze the interaction levels with project postings (views, applications) during a limited beta release.

## Acceptance Criteria
1. At least 50 recent graduate profiles created within the first month.
2. A minimum of 10 small businesses posting one or more projects by the end of the testing phase.
3. An engagement metric where at least 30% of graduates apply to projects posted.
4. Gather qualitative feedback from both graduates and businesses regarding the platform’s usability and perceived value.

## Risks And Failure Modes
- Risk of low engagement from either recent graduates or small businesses.
- Potential for abuse of the platform by companies seeking free labor.
- Difficulty in maintaining a high-quality experience, leading to poor feedback and reputation.
- Variability in legal status impacting project engagement and user trust.

## Recommendation
Proceed with the project to build the MVP focusing on the core features that facilitate user onboarding, project posting, and basic communication. Prioritize establishing trust and value with initial users to validate the concept. Further investment should be contingent on successful validation of the critical assumptions and metrics set forth in the acceptance criteria.
