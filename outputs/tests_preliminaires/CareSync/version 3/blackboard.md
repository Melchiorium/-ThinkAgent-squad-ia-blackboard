## Project Brief

Project Name: CareSync

Pitch:
A digital platform that helps families coordinate care for elderly relatives by centralizing medical appointments, medication reminders, caregiving tasks, and communication between family members and professional caregivers.

Context:
Many families struggle to manage care for aging parents or relatives, especially when multiple siblings or caregivers are involved. Information is often fragmented across phone calls, messaging apps, handwritten notes, and hospital documents.

At the same time, professional caregivers and home assistance providers often lack visibility into family decisions and day-to-day coordination.

The platform aims to simplify elderly care coordination while reducing stress for families.

Target Users:
- Adults managing care for elderly parents
- Family members living in different cities
- Professional caregivers
- Home nurses
- Elderly care agencies

Potential Use Cases:
- Scheduling medical appointments
- Medication reminders
- Shared task management
- Emergency contact coordination
- Sharing medical documents
- Tracking caregiving responsibilities

Platform Capabilities:
- Shared family dashboard
- Calendar coordination
- Notifications/reminders
- Secure document storage
- Messaging system
- Permission management for family members and caregivers

Constraints:
- High trust and privacy expectations
- Sensitive medical-related information
- Elderly users may have low digital literacy
- Potential legal/data compliance issues depending on country

Challenges:
- Convincing families to adopt a new platform
- Balancing simplicity with complex coordination needs
- Building trust around sensitive personal information
- Identifying a sustainable business model

Long-term Vision:
Become the operating system for family caregiving coordination and eventually expand into broader home healthcare services.

### Tech Summary

The main challenge to feasibility is establishing user trust concerning sensitive data management. It is recommended to implement a simplified, manual onboarding process and build core functionalities (dashboard, scheduling, reminders) to validate user needs prior to full-scale development.

## Tech Recommendations

- Clarify the privacy policies and compliance strategy regarding sensitive medical data handling.
- Explore user onboarding preferences to ensure a smooth initial experience.
- Implement visible feedback mechanisms for user actions (e.g., appointment confirmations).
- Assess the need for a concierge service during initial user testing to guide families.
- Refine user roles and permissions to accommodate different family dynamics and caregiver access.


## Tech Risks

- Users may distrust the platform's ability to secure sensitive information, hampering adoption.
- Complex onboarding may lead to high drop-off rates among users.
- Reliance on manual processes could hinder scalability and efficiency.


## Tech Open Questions

- How will we handle varying levels of digital literacy among elderly users?
- What specific features can be included in the MVP to maximize immediate user engagement?


### Growth Summary

The main challenge for CareSync lies in building trust and encouraging adoption among families managing elderly care, particularly with sensitive medical information involved. A concierge pilot targeting a small, defined user base is recommended to gather insights and validate the platform’s value proposition before scaling.

## Growth Recommendations

- Integrate a visible privacy policy and trust-building features directly within the app to show commitment to security.
- Add a "Get Started" tutorial to guide users through setting up the dashboard and utilizing the main features safely and efficiently.
- Include a feature for collecting user feedback directly in-app to iterate on user needs rapidly.
- Streamline the document sharing feature for simpler access to medical documents.
- Develop a user verification process to enhance security and privacy management.


## Growth Risks

## Growth Open Questions

- How can we effectively communicate the privacy and security measures within the platform to build user trust?
- What existing caregiving tools do families use, and why are they hesitant to switch?

## Initial PRD

# CareSync Product Proposal

## Product Problem
Families often experience fragmentation and inefficiency in coordinating care for elderly relatives. Multiple siblings or caregivers frequently share responsibilities, leading to communication gaps and stress. This difficulty is exacerbated by the need to manage medical appointments, medication schedules, and caregiving tasks without a centralized system. Professional caregivers also struggle with a lack of visibility into family dynamics and decisions, hindering effective care delivery.

## Initial Wedge
The narrowest credible MVP wedge is a shared digital family dashboard focused on scheduling medical appointments and medication reminders.

## First Target User
The first target user segment is adults managing care for elderly parents who live in different cities. They are likely to face the highest coordination challenges and have a pressing need for an organized system.

## Existing Alternatives And Switching Trigger
Existing alternatives include standalone calendar apps, messaging platforms, and physical notes. Users would switch to CareSync if they find it provides a holistic, centralized solution that reduces coordination stress and integrates multiple caregiving functions in one platform.

## Core MVP Workflow
1. Users can create and share a family dashboard.
2. Users can schedule medical appointments collaboratively.
3. Users receive medication reminders through notifications.
4. The dashboard allows for shared access to important documents.

## In Scope
- Shared family dashboard for real-time updates
- Calendar coordination for medical appointments
- Medication reminder notifications
- Basic secure document storage for medical documents

## Out of Scope
- Advanced task management features
- Messaging systems beyond notification alerts
- Integration with medical systems or electronic health records (EHR)
- Comprehensive compliance management features

## Business Model Hypothesis
CareSync could operate on a subscription model with a monthly fee charged to families or caregivers for accessing premium features such as enhanced storage or advanced reporting tools.

## Critical Assumptions
1. Families are willing to adopt a new digital platform for care coordination.
2. Users prioritize having a centralized solution versus using multiple apps.
3. Users trust the platform with sensitive medical information due to visible privacy controls.
4. The platform can demonstrate significant value in improving care coordination.

## How To Test Quickly
Conduct surveys and interviews with target users to validate willingness to adopt a new platform. Create a low-fidelity prototype to gauge user interest in core functionalities (dashboard, scheduling, reminders) through user testing sessions.

## Acceptance Criteria
- User registration and dashboard setup within 10 minutes.
- Successful scheduling of a medical appointment by at least 80% of users in a user testing setting.
- At least 70% of users report satisfaction with receiving medication reminders.
- Basic document sharing functionality confirmed as functional with no major issues.

## Risks And Failure Modes
- High user drop-off rates during onboarding if the process is not intuitive.
- Concerns regarding data privacy and security may deter adopters.
- Stakeholder engagement may be low due to the inertia of existing solutions.
- Misalignment between family needs and platform capabilities could limit user satisfaction.

## Product Readiness
Status: LIMITED

Blocking Gaps:
- User trust in managing sensitive data is not yet validated.
- There is a need for in-depth user research to fully understand family caregivers' workflows.

Required Improvements:
- Develop trust-building features (e.g., visible privacy policies, testimonials).
- Create an initial prototype to test with target users and gather feedback.

## Recommendation
Proceed with user research followed by a concierge pilot to validate core assumptions and workflows. The pilot should integrate real users to uncover detailed needs and preferences before moving forward with full product development.
