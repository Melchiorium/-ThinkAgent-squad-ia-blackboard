## Project Brief

Project Name: CareSync

Pitch:
A digital platform that helps families coordinate care for elderly relatives by centralizing medical appointments, medication reminders, caregiving tasks, and communication between family members and professional caregivers.

Context:
Many families struggle to manage care for aging parents or relatives, especially when multiple siblings or caregivers are involved. Information is often fragmented across phone calls, messaging apps, handwritten notes, and hospital documents.

At the same time, professional caregivers and home assistance providers often lack visibility into family decisions and day-to-day coordination.

The platform aims to simplify elderly care coordination while reducing stress for families.

Target Users:
- Adults managing care for elderly parents
- Family members living in different cities
- Professional caregivers
- Home nurses
- Elderly care agencies

Potential Use Cases:
- Scheduling medical appointments
- Medication reminders
- Shared task management
- Emergency contact coordination
- Sharing medical documents
- Tracking caregiving responsibilities

Platform Capabilities:
- Shared family dashboard
- Calendar coordination
- Notifications/reminders
- Secure document storage
- Messaging system
- Permission management for family members and caregivers

Constraints:
- High trust and privacy expectations
- Sensitive medical-related information
- Elderly users may have low digital literacy
- Potential legal/data compliance issues depending on country

Challenges:
- Convincing families to adopt a new platform
- Balancing simplicity with complex coordination needs
- Building trust around sensitive personal information
- Identifying a sustainable business model

Long-term Vision:
Become the operating system for family caregiving coordination and eventually expand into broader home healthcare services.

## Initial PRD

# CareSync Product Proposal

## Product Problem
Families face significant challenges in managing care for elderly relatives, resulting in fragmented communication and inefficiencies. Current solutions include phone calls and multiple apps, which complicate coordination and increase stress among family members. Furthermore, professional caregivers lack the necessary insight into family decisions and day-to-day tasks, hindering effective care.

## Initial Wedge
The initial wedge for CareSync is centered around **scheduling medical appointments and sending medication reminders**, which are essential pain points for families managing care.

## First Target User
The first target user for the launch will be **adult children managing care for their elderly parents**, particularly those living in different cities and juggling other commitments.

## Existing Alternatives And Switching Trigger
Current alternatives include managing care through:
- Phone calls
- Shared calendars (Google Calendar, iCal)
- Messaging apps (WhatsApp, text messages)
- Paper notes

The critical switching trigger is the promise of a **centralized and user-friendly platform** that reduces the complexity of coordinating elderly care, coupled with features that specifically address the pain points of fragmentation and missed appointments.

## Core MVP Workflow
1. User signs up and creates a family group.
2. Users can input and coordinate medical appointments.
3. Users set up medication reminders.
4. Notifications are sent to family members.
5. Basic messaging functionality allows for family dialogue about care.

## In Scope
- User onboarding and family group setup
- Calendar integration for medical appointments
- Medication reminder system
- Notifications for appointments and tasks
- Basic messaging feature for family communication

## Out of Scope
- Advanced features like document storage and permission management
- Customizable user interfaces
- Advanced analytics for caregivers
- Integration with third-party health apps

## Business Model Hypothesis
CareSync could generate revenue through a subscription model targeting families, and potential partnerships with healthcare agencies could lead to additional revenue streams.

## Critical Assumptions
1. Adult children are actively seeking a streamlined solution for managing their parents' care.
2. Users are willing to subscribe to a platform that simplifies care coordination.
3. User engagement will be high with effective reminders and notifications.
4. Privacy concerns can be sufficiently addressed to gain user trust.

## How To Test Quickly
- Conduct interviews with the target user segment to validate demand and willingness to pay.
- Build a simple landing page outlining core features and measure user sign-ups or subscriptions.
- Run usability tests with prototypes focusing on scheduling and reminders.

## Acceptance Criteria
1. At least 70% of testers find the app easy to navigate.
2. A minimum of 80% of users accurately input and receive notifications for appointments in testing.
3. Users express willingness to pay for the solution based on its value.
4. 75% of users report a decrease in stress levels related to managing elderly care within the trial period.

## Risks And Failure Modes
- High trust and privacy concerns may hinder adoption.
- Elderly users’ low digital literacy could limit usage.
- Families may still find existing solutions acceptable, leading to low conversion.

Status: LIMITED
