# Blackboard

## Project Brief

Project Name: LocalLoop

Pitch:
A mobile application that helps people discover local independent businesses through personalized recommendations, neighborhood deals, and loyalty rewards.

Context:
Many consumers want to support local businesses but often default to large platforms because discovering small local stores, restaurants, services, and events is fragmented and inconvenient.

At the same time, independent businesses struggle with visibility and customer retention but often lack the budget or expertise to run digital marketing campaigns.

The platform helps consumers discover relevant local businesses while giving small businesses simple tools to attract and retain nearby customers.

Target Users:
- Urban residents
- Young professionals
- Families
- Tourists
- Independent local businesses

Potential Use Cases:
- Discover nearby coffee shops
- Find local restaurants with promotions
- Discover niche stores
- Access loyalty rewards
- Local event discovery
- Neighborhood recommendations

Platform Capabilities:
- Personalized recommendations
- Geolocation
- Merchant profiles
- Promotions/offers
- Loyalty system
- Reviews
- Local event feed

Constraints:
- Limited initial budget
- Need enough local business supply in one city before expanding
- Must avoid competing directly with massive horizontal platforms

Challenges:
- Convincing local businesses to join
- Maintaining recommendation quality
- Avoiding platform saturation with irrelevant offers

Long-term Vision:
Become the default discovery platform for local commerce and neighborhood experiences.

# LocalLoop Product Proposal

## Product Problem
Consumers face challenges in discovering local independent businesses due to fragmented and inconvenient options, often leading them to larger platforms. Independent businesses struggle with visibility and customer retention due to limited marketing resources and expertise.

## Initial Wedge
The initial wedge will be a mobile app that offers personalized recommendations for local independent businesses, along with exclusive neighborhood deals and a loyalty rewards program. This will serve as a compelling reason for users to engage with LocalLoop rather than defaulting to larger platforms.

## First Target User
Urban residents aged 25-40 (young professionals and families) who are interested in supporting local businesses and seeking personalized local experiences.

## Existing Alternatives And Switching Trigger
Current alternatives include Google Maps, Yelp, and Groupon, where users often discover local businesses but are overwhelmed by choices and irrelevant information. Users are likely to switch to LocalLoop if they find highly relevant, curated recommendations and special local offers that enhance their community engagement.

## Core MVP Workflow
1. User signs up and inputs their location/preferences.
2. The app provides a list of personalized local business recommendations.
3. Users receive notifications about nearby deals and loyalty rewards.
4. Users can leave reviews and check in at businesses for loyalty points.

## In Scope
- User sign-up and location-based recommendations
- Merchant profiles with deals and rewards
- User loyalty system (e.g., points for visits)
- Reviews feature for businesses
- Basic geolocation capabilities

## Out of Scope
- Comprehensive marketing tools for businesses
- In-app purchases or payment processing
- Advanced social features (e.g., sharing)

## Business Model Hypothesis
LocalLoop may operate on a freemium model where consumers access the app for free while businesses can pay for premium listings, promotional placements, or loyalty program features.

## Critical Assumptions
- Consumers value personalized recommendations significantly enough to adopt LocalLoop.
- Local businesses will see enough value in the platform to participate and promote it.
- Geolocation and deal notifications effectively drive user engagement.

## How To Test Quickly
- Conduct user interviews to gauge interest in personalized recommendations.
- Prototype the app and seek feedback from a small group of urban residents.
- Survey local businesses regarding their interest in joining a platform that offers promotional tools.

## Acceptance Criteria
1. 100 users registered and actively using the app within the first month.
2. At least 10 local businesses onboarded and offering promotions.
3. Users report finding personalized recommendations useful (>=75% positive feedback).

## Risks And Failure Modes
- Resistance from local businesses due to perceived complexity or costs.
- Low user engagement if the app does not deliver relevant recommendations.
- Potential over-saturation of promotions leading to user fatigue.
