# Melody Product Proposal

## Product Problem
Traditional dating apps often fail to address the importance of lifestyle compatibility, particularly in the context of music. Music enthusiasts struggle to find meaningful connections based on shared tastes, while musicians face challenges in discovering collaborators. Melody aims to fill this gap by providing a platform where users can engage not only in dating but also in music discovery and community building based on their musical preferences.

## Initial Wedge
The initial wedge for Melody will focus on music compatibility for dating purposes. This will allow us to leverage the emotional connection people have with music to foster relationships, providing a deeper experience compared to existing dating apps.

## First Target User
The first target user segment will be singles looking for deeper compatibility through shared musical interests. This group is likely to be more engaged and willing to explore connections based on their passion for music.

## Existing Alternatives And Switching Trigger
Current alternatives include traditional dating apps (e.g., Tinder, Bumble) and music platforms (e.g., Spotify, Last.fm). Users might switch to Melody if they perceive it as a service that facilitates deeper connections through their love of music, enhanced by features oriented towards music discovery and community interaction.

## Core MVP Workflow
1. **User Onboarding:** Users complete a music taste profile by selecting favorite genres, artists, and songs.
2. **Compatibility Scoring:** The system generates compatibility scores based on music preferences, considering selected artists and genres.
3. **Matching:** Users can view profiles of potential matches based on the compatibility score.
4. **Messaging:** Users can message their matches to foster interaction, with safety prompts to ensure trust.
5. **Concert Discovery:** Recommendations for concerts that align with user music tastes will be integrated immediately post-onboarding.

## In Scope
- Music taste onboarding
- Compatibility scoring based on musical preferences influenced by selected genres and artists
- Basic messaging feature with safety prompts
- Concert discovery based on user profiles and preferences
- Engagement metrics to assess the success of onboarding

## Out of Scope
- Advanced features like band member or community group integrations
- Marketplace capabilities for buying/selling music equipment
- Integration with existing social media platforms
- In-depth community interaction features
- "Refer a Friend" functionality will be deferred for later versions.

## Business Model Hypothesis
Monetization will rely on a freemium model where basic features are free, with premium subscription options for enhanced features like advanced filtering, concert alerts, and exclusive community access.

## Critical Assumptions
- Users prioritize music compatibility in their dating preferences.
- There is sufficient demand for a music-focused dating app.
- Users will engage regularly with concert discovery and compatibility features.

## How To Test Quickly
- Conduct user surveys during onboarding to validate the importance of music compatibility.
- Create a landing page to gauge user sign-ups and gather initial music preferences.
- Run a pilot with a small group to test onboarding and matching functionality.

## Acceptance Criteria
- Successful onboarding of at least 100 users with completed music profiles.
- At least 30% of users engaging in messaging within the first month.
- Positive feedback on compatibility scores in a user survey, targeting an average rating of 4 out of 5.

## Risks And Failure Modes
- Low user engagement if the platform does not deliver on the promise of deep music compatibility.
- Technical challenges in developing effective compatibility algorithms.
- Competition from established dating platforms that may launch similar features.

## Product Readiness
Status: LIMITED

Blocking Gaps:
- Need to validate the concept of music compatibility as a priority for potential users.
- Insufficient clarity on how to source music preference data effectively.

Required Improvements:
- Conduct user research to confirm the demand for music-based compatibility.
- Develop and test initial algorithms for generating compatibility scores based on user profiles, using reliable music data services.

## Recommendation
Proceed with a focused MVP build that emphasizes music compatibility for dating, addressing essential workflows while conducting further research to verify assumptions and reduce risks. Limit initial features to ensure a viable and launch-ready product. Focus on user onboarding, scoring, basic messaging, and concert discovery. Deploy manual workflows where appropriate until core technological solutions are credible.
