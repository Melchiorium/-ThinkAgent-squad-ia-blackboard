# Melody MVP Proposal

## Product Problem
Traditional dating apps fail to address deep lifestyle compatibility by focusing primarily on superficial attributes. This results in missed connections among music enthusiasts who desire meaningful interactions based on shared musical interests and values. Furthermore, music communities are fragmented across various platforms, making it difficult for users to engage effectively.

## Initial Wedge
The initial wedge for the MVP will be the dating functionality based on music compatibility, specifically connecting singles who share similar music tastes. This focus aims to build a core user base that values deep compatibility through music.

## First Target User
The first target user will be singles aged 18-35 who are passionate about music and looking for meaningful relationships founded on mutual musical tastes.

## Existing Alternatives And Switching Trigger
Current alternatives include traditional dating apps (e.g., Tinder, Bumble) and social music platforms (e.g., Spotify, Last.fm), which do not provide deep compatibility matching. The main switching trigger is the ability to find romantic matches based on shared music preferences, facilitating meaningful interactions.

## Core MVP Workflow
1. **Music Taste Onboarding**: Users input their top artists and genres in a gamified format to enhance engagement.
2. **Compatibility Scoring**: The app generates compatibility scores based on shared music interests.
3. **User Profiles**: Users can view potential matches based on music compatibility and shared interests.
4. **Messaging**: Basic chat functionality includes a preliminary moderation system for user safety, enabling users to connect confidently.
5. **Concert Discovery**: Suggest local concerts based on user preferences and compatibility, integrated through partnerships with local venues.

## In Scope
- Music taste onboarding (gamified).
- Compatibility scoring.
- User profiles.
- Basic messaging with moderation.
- Concert discovery through local partnerships.

## Out of Scope
- Music marketplace features.
- Community groups.
- Finding band members.
- Buying/selling music equipment.
- Artist recommendations.

## Business Model Hypothesis
The primary revenue model will be freemium, with essential functionality available for free and premium features (e.g., advanced matchmaking algorithms, concert perks) offered as a subscription. Revenue could also come from partnerships with local concert venues for advertising.

## Critical Assumptions
- Users will find a matching dating experience based on music preferences appealing enough to switch from traditional dating apps.
- Users will engage consistently with the platform due to music-related events and interactions.
- Access to music preference data will be achievable and compliant with legal standards.

## How To Test Quickly
- Conduct surveys among target users to validate interest in music-based dating.
- Test the music onboarding and compatibility scoring mechanisms through a concierge pilot with a small group to gather actionable feedback on user experience.
- Launch a landing page explaining the value proposition and collect emails from interested users.

## Acceptance Criteria
- 1000 users onboarded in the first month.
- At least 500 active matches (conversations initiated) within the first month of launching.
- A minimum of 25% retention of users after 1 month.

## Risks And Failure Modes
- Low user adoption due to competition with established dating platforms.
- Difficulty in maintaining a sufficient user base to ensure effective matching.
- Potential legal issues surrounding access to music preference data.
- Underwhelming user engagement leading to high churn rates.

## Product Readiness
Status: LIMITED

Blocking Gaps:
- Uncertainty around legal access to music preference data.

Required Improvements:
- Conduct feasibility studies on music data access and partnerships.
- Refine the onboarding process to ensure a seamless and engaging user experience.

## Recommendation
Proceed with a concierge pilot to validate assumptions about user interest and preferences, followed by iterating on the MVP based on feedback. Confirm legal access to music preference data before transition to a full MVP. Focus on precise user onboarding steps and establishing local venue partnerships for concert discovery.
