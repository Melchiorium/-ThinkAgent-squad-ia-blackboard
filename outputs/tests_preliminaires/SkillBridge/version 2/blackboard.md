## Project Brief

Project Name: SkillBridge

Pitch:
A digital platform designed to help young graduates and career switchers gain practical experience through short-term real-world projects proposed by small companies, startups, and non-profits.

Context:
Many graduates struggle to get hired because they lack professional experience.
At the same time, small organizations often need help on short projects but cannot afford full-time hires or expensive agencies.

The platform aims to connect both sides by allowing organizations to publish short missions (2 to 8 weeks) that candidates can complete remotely or on-site.

Target Users:
- Recent graduates
- Career changers
- Small businesses
- Startups
- Associations / NGOs

Potential Use Cases:
- UX audit for a small startup
- Social media campaign for a local business
- Data cleanup project
- Website redesign
- Market research mission
- Administrative process optimization

Platform Capabilities:
- User profiles
- Project posting
- Matching system
- Messaging
- Deliverable submission
- Feedback and reputation system

Constraints:
- Limited initial budget
- Need to build trust between both sides
- Must avoid becoming a low-quality freelance marketplace
- Legal/employment boundaries may vary depending on country

Challenges:
- Attract enough companies early on
- Ensure project quality
- Prevent abuse from companies looking for free labor
- Create enough value for candidates to stay engaged

Long-term Vision:
Become an alternative path to traditional internships and junior hiring by helping people build credible experience faster.

# SkillBridge Product Proposal

## Product Problem
Recent graduates and career switchers often struggle to gain the necessary practical experience to become employable. Simultaneously, small businesses and non-profits require assistance on short-term projects but lack the resources for full-time hires. This disconnect creates a gap that SkillBridge aims to fill by connecting these two groups.

## Initial Wedge
Facilitate engagement between recent graduates/career switchers and small businesses/non-profits for short-term project collaborations. The MVP will focus on a matchmaking platform that allows organizations to post short missions that candidates can apply for, enabling experiential learning while avoiding a low-quality freelance marketplace.

## First Target User
Recent graduates looking to gain hands-on experience in their respective fields. This segment has a pressing need for practical experience to enhance their employability.

## Existing Alternatives And Switching Trigger
Currently, graduates rely on traditional internships, volunteer opportunities, and low-quality freelance platforms to gain experience. The switching trigger will be SkillBridge's emphasis on structured, meaningful projects from reputable organizations with a built-in mechanism for feedback and reputation that elevates the quality of the experiences compared to existing alternatives.

## Core MVP Workflow
1. Organizations create profiles and post short missions.
2. Graduates create profiles showcasing their skills and interests.
3. A matching system connects graduates to suitable missions based on skill and interest.
4. Graduates apply for missions and complete tasks with deliverables submitted.
5. Organizations provide feedback and rate candidates, enhancing their profiles.

## In Scope
- User profiles (for both graduates and organizations)
- Basic project posting and application system
- Matching system based on skills and interests
- Messaging system between graduates and organizations
- Delivery of completed missions and feedback mechanism

## Out of Scope
- Payment processing or financial transactions
- Extensive customization of profiles or projects
- In-depth training or mentoring features
- Legal consultancy or compliance checks across countries

## Business Model Hypothesis
SkillBridge will operate on a freemium model where companies pay a nominal fee to post projects beyond a certain limit, or access premium features such as analytics on candidate applications. Graduates will use the platform for free to encourage wide adoption.

## Critical Assumptions
- Graduates seek short-term, meaningful projects to gain experience.
- Small organizations are willing and able to post missions on the platform.
- Both sides can be effectively matched through a simple algorithm.
- Companies are interested in contributing feedback to improve the platform.

## How To Test Quickly
- Conduct surveys with prospective users (both graduates and organizations) to validate their willingness to use the platform for finding projects or candidates.
- Create a landing page to gauge interest with options to sign up for more information, helping to measure demand.

## Acceptance Criteria
- At least 50 organizations willing to post a project within the first month.
- Minimum of 200 graduates signing up and completing profiles within the same timeframe.
- Functioning matching system that effectively connects at least 30% of applicants to posted missions.
- Positive user feedback (target: 70% satisfaction with the matching process).

## Risks And Failure Modes
- Low engagement from target organizations leading to inadequate project postings.
- Graduates may perceive the value of the projects as insufficient.
- Potential legal challenges related to employment practices across different regions.
- Quality control issues where organizations may misuse the platform to source free labor.

## Core MVP Workflow
1. Organizations create profiles and post short missions.
2. Graduates create profiles showcasing their skills and interests.
3. A matching system connects graduates to suitable missions based on skill and interest.
4. Graduates apply for missions and submit deliverables.
5. Organizations provide feedback and rate candidates, enhancing their profiles.

## In Scope
- User profiles (for both graduates and organizations)
- Basic project posting and application system
- Matching system based on skills and interests
- Messaging system between graduates and organizations
- Delivery of completed missions and feedback mechanism
- Quality assurance process to evaluate project postings for standard compliance

## Out of Scope
- Payment processing or financial transactions
- Extensive customization of profiles or projects
- In-depth training or mentoring features
- Legal consultancy or compliance checks beyond initial framework validation

## Business Model Hypothesis
SkillBridge will operate on a freemium model where companies pay a nominal fee to post projects beyond a certain limit or access premium features with enhanced analytics on candidate applications. Graduates will continue to use the platform for free to foster broad usage.

## Critical Assumptions
- Graduates actively seek short-term, meaningful projects to gain experience.
- Small organizations are willing and able to post missions on the platform.
- Effective matching can be achieved through a simple algorithm.
- Companies are motivated to provide actionable feedback.

## How To Test Quickly
- Conduct surveys with prospective user groups to validate their willingness to utilize the platform for finding projects or candidates.
- Create an initial landing page to gauge interest, allowing sign-ups for further information to measure demand.
- Execute a concierge pilot with select organizations to evaluate engagement and project quality effectively.

## Acceptance Criteria
- At least 50 organizations willing to post a project within the first month.
- Minimum of 200 graduates signing up and completing profiles within the same timeframe.
- Functioning matching system that connects at least 30% of applicants to posted missions.
- Positive user feedback (target: 70% satisfaction with the matching process).

## Risks And Failure Modes
- Insufficient engagement from target organizations leading to inadequate project postings.
- Graduates may perceive projects as lacking value.
- Potential legal challenges related to employment practices across different regions.
- Quality control issues, where organizations may misuse the platform to obtain free labor.

## Product Readiness
Status: LIMITED

Blocking Gaps:
- Uncertainty around the legal implications of temporary project work in varying regions.
- Need for thorough validation of demand from both graduates and organizations.
- Establishment of quality assurance measures to prevent low-quality postings.

Required Improvements:
- Conduct focused research on legal ramifications to ensure compliance.
- Set up pilot tests to validate core assumptions and refine the matching process.
- Implement a feedback mechanism for both graduates and companies upon project completion to enhance trust and quality assurance.

## Recommendation
Proceed with a concierge pilot to validate critical assumptions, refine workflows, and establish trust before progressing to a full MVP build. This approach will facilitate a better understanding of user needs and mitigate risks of low engagement from both graduates and organizations.
