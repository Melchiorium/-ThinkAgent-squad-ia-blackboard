## Go-To-Market Notes

- **Main Market Bottleneck**: Building initial trust and engagement between graduates and organizations, essential to prevent exploitation and establish a high-quality project marketplace.

- **Side of the Market to Secure First**: Small organizations (startups and non-profits) that are willing to post Projects and offer meaningful experiences to graduates.

- **Initial Target Audience**: Small startups and non-profits looking for affordable project assistance. These organizations are likely more flexible and willing to participate than larger corporations.

- **Positioning**: SkillBridge positions itself as a trustworthy platform that facilitates real-world project experience through structured, reputable missions that offer clear value to graduates while ensuring organizations receive quality support for their projects.

- **First Acquisition Motion**: Conduct concierge pilots where a small group of startups and non-profits are engaged directly through outreach, followed by personalized support in posting projects.

- **Operating Assumptions for the First Acquisition Motion**:
  - Outreach to 50 target organizations with a goal of securing 10-15 willing to participate in the pilot.
  - 50% of participating organizations agree to provide feedback post-project completion.
  - Target a response rate of at least 20% from initial outreach efforts to gauge interest and finalize participants.

- **Switching Trigger**: The structured nature of projects, combined with the reputation scoring for graduates, differentiates SkillBridge by filtering out low-quality experiences, a key concern for both parties comparing alternatives.

- **First Activation Loop**: Graduates create profiles, are matched with projects, complete deliverables, and receive feedback. This loop should ensure repeated engagement as graduates complete projects and organizations provide ongoing missions.

- **What Must Exist Before Public Launch**: 
  - A minimum of 10 participating organizations in the concierge pilot.
  - Five positive feedback responses from graduates who completed projects.
  - A functioning matching system that successfully connects 30% of applicants to posted missions.

## Review Summary
The primary challenge for SkillBridge lies in establishing trust and engagement between graduates and small organizations before scaling. The recommended GTM direction is a targeted concierge pilot to validate critical assumptions and refine interactions, ensuring quality and adherence to expectations.

## Critical Assumptions
- Graduates prefer real-world project experiences over traditional internships or low-quality freelance work.
- Small organizations can effectively utilize the platform for short-term project needs.
- Positive volunteer experiences will lead to organic referrals and engagement.
- The quality assurance and feedback mechanisms will be trusted by both graduates and organizations.
- Organizations will perceive value in offering feedback, enhancing the platform’s credibility.

## Requested Changes
- Implement a robust vetting process for posted projects to ensure quality and prevent exploitation.
- Establish a feedback loop mechanism that encourages immediate posts from graduates and companies upon project completion.
- Design clear, simple onboarding and matching instructions for both graduates and organizations to ensure seamless initial experiences.
- Introduce an onboarding tutorial for both parties to guide them through using the platform effectively.
- Clarify the pilot structure for organizations to understand their commitments and expected involvement.

## Risks
- Insufficient engagement from target organizations could lead to limited project postings.
- Graduates may find the platform's offerings unworthy, causing churn and low repeat usage.
- Legal ambiguities regarding project job classifications in various countries may deter participation from organizations.
- Potential misuse of the platform as companies may try to gain free labor instead of legitimate project assistance.
- Inconsistent project quality leading to negative experiences and poor adoption rates.

## Open Questions
- What legal frameworks need to be considered to ensure compliance across different regions?
- How will organizations be incentivized to provide consistent, quality feedback?
- What specific metrics will be used to assess the quality of project postings from organizations?
- How can we design the initial onboarding process to minimize abandonment rates from both users?
- What strategies will ensure long-term retention for graduates beyond their initial project experience?

## Why This Could Fail Even With Good Execution
If the team successfully executes the pilot but fails to verify the core market assumptions regarding demand and quality assurance, they may inadvertently attract organizations or graduates unwilling to use the platform in the long run, ultimately hampering growth efforts.
