## Go-To-Market Notes
**Primary audience:**  
Start with **daily urban commuters who already pay for parking 3–5 days per week** in one dense city zone (near offices, transit hubs, hospitals, campuses). They have the clearest pain, repeat behavior, and highest willingness to reserve ahead of time.

**Positioning:**  
Position ParkEasy as **“reserve a guaranteed private parking spot before you leave”** — not a broad parking app, but a **reliable alternative to circling for street parking** in a specific high-demand area. For owners, the message is **“earn from unused parking spots with zero hassle.”**

**First acquisition channel:**  
Use a **hyperlocal supply-led launch**:
1. Sign up 20–50 verified parking spot owners in one tight geography.
2. Then acquire drivers through **local commuter-adjacent channels**: office building mailing lists, neighborhood/community groups, transit comms, and direct outreach around specific destinations.
This is the most realistic first channel because marketplace liquidity matters more than broad awareness.

**First activation idea:**  
Make the first-user activation loop **“search a destination, see 3–5 reservable spots, and complete a booking in under 2 minutes.”**  
Critical launch flow: destination-based search → clearly reserved spot with exact access instructions → instant confirmation → GPS navigation.  
For owners, activation is complete when they **create a listing and get the first booking within the first week**.

**Early success indicators:**  
- At least **20–50 active, verified listings** in one launch zone
- **Search-to-book conversion** above a meaningful early baseline
- **% of listings booked at least once per week**
- **Time to first booking** for new owners
- **Reservation completion rate** and low cancellation/double-booking rate
- **Repeat booking rate** among early commuter users

## Review Summary
The main growth challenge is not broad demand generation; it is **creating enough trusted supply in one dense area to make the app useful on day one**. The recommended GTM path is a **tight, supply-led city launch** focused on repeat commuters and a small set of verified parking owners, with the product optimized around fast reservation and confidence that the spot is real.

## Requested Changes
- Add a **launch geography definition**: one city plus 1–3 specific parking clusters/destinations to avoid thin marketplace coverage.
- Require **verified supply onboarding** for initial listings, including proof of ownership/authorization and exact access instructions.
- Add a **destination-first search flow** in MVP, not just “near me,” so drivers can search around the places they actually need to park.
- Define a **minimum reservation trust package**: exact spot location, entry instructions, booking confirmation, and support contact visible before payment.
- Add an **owner onboarding shortcut** for launch: manual-assisted listing creation for the first cohort, so supply can be seeded quickly and accurately.

## Risks
- If the launch zone does not reach sufficient density, drivers will churn after seeing too few usable options.
- Trust may break quickly if spot accuracy or access instructions are incomplete, because the product is tied to a physical asset and immediate arrival.
- Owners may not stay engaged if bookings are too infrequent, making supply retention a bigger issue than initial sign-up.
