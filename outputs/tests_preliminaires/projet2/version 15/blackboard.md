## Project Brief

Project Name: SkillBridge

Pitch:
A digital platform designed to help young graduates and career switchers gain practical experience through short-term real-world projects proposed by small companies, startups, and non-profits.

Context:
Many graduates struggle to get hired because they lack professional experience.
At the same time, small organizations often need help on short projects but cannot afford full-time hires or expensive agencies.

The platform aims to connect both sides by allowing organizations to publish short missions (2 to 8 weeks) that candidates can complete remotely or on-site.

Target Users:
- Recent graduates
- Career changers
- Small businesses
- Startups
- Associations / NGOs

Potential Use Cases:
- UX audit for a small startup
- Social media campaign for a local business
- Data cleanup project
- Website redesign
- Market research mission
- Administrative process optimization

Platform Capabilities:
- User profiles
- Project posting
- Matching system
- Messaging
- Deliverable submission
- Feedback and reputation system

Constraints:
- Limited initial budget
- Need to build trust between both sides
- Must avoid becoming a low-quality freelance marketplace
- Legal/employment boundaries may vary depending on country

Challenges:
- Attract enough companies early on
- Ensure project quality
- Prevent abuse from companies looking for free labor
- Create enough value for candidates to stay engaged

Long-term Vision:
Become an alternative path to traditional internships and junior hiring by helping people build credible experience faster.

## Expert Contributions

### Tech Summary

The primary feasibility challenge lies in establishing trust between users which is vital for engagement and preventing abuse of the platform. The recommended direction is to implement a straightforward MVP focused on user profiles and project postings, with manual feedback processes to validate user interaction and experience.

## Tech Recommendations

- Incorporate a clear process for users to report issues or abuses to build trust and safety.
- Define the criteria for project quality assurance to avoid low-quality projects.
- Allow businesses to provide clear project descriptions and expectations to help graduates make informed decisions.
- Establish output templates for deliverables to standardize submissions.
- Add a structured onboarding process for businesses to ensure they understand platform guidelines.


## Tech Risks

- Low user engagement resulting from distrust or misunderstanding of the platform.
- High potential for misuse by companies looking to leverage free labor.
- Difficulty in maintaining a high standard of quality for posted projects, leading to poor user experiences.


## Tech Open Questions

- How will the platform address variations in legal definitions of employment across countries?
- What specific measures will be put in place to mitigate the risk of companies seeking unpaid labor?
- How will the platform handle disputes or dissatisfaction between graduates and companies?


### Growth Summary

The main challenge for SkillBridge lies in building trust between graduates and small businesses while ensuring the quality of projects. The recommended GTM direction is to first engage small businesses to secure project offerings, which will in turn attract recent graduates, leveraging selective outreach and a strong onboarding experience.

## Growth Recommendations

- Develop a clear onboarding guide outlining how both businesses and graduates can benefit from the platform.
- Implement a basic vetting system for project postings to assure quality and credibility.
- Add a feedback mechanism early in the project to encourage honest reviews and insights.
- Create case study templates to outline successful projects to serve as motivation and concrete examples for users.
- Design a clear communication strategy to outline the platform's value in terms of career building for graduates.


## Growth Risks

- Insufficient engagement from small businesses leads to a lack of project offerings.
- High potential for companies abusing the platform to find free labor without providing adequate project work.
- Difficulty in maintaining quality of projects leading to negative experiences for graduates.


## Growth Open Questions

- How will SkillBridge manage the varying legal classifications of project work in different countries?
- What specific metrics will be used to assess engagement from both graduates and businesses effectively?
- Are there particular industries or sectors expected to yield higher interest in project postings?

## Initial PRD

# SkillBridge Product Proposal

## Product Problem
Young graduates and career switchers often lack practical experience, making it challenging for them to secure jobs. Simultaneously, small organizations are in need of assistance with short-term projects but cannot afford to hire full-time employees or expensive agencies. SkillBridge aims to bridge this gap by providing a platform for both parties to collaborate on short-term projects.

## Initial Wedge
The initial wedge focuses on providing a validated experience for graduates and a reliable source of project assistance for small businesses. The objective is to prove that a significant demand exists from both users for short-term projects that can enhance employability while providing valuable contributions to companies.

## First Target User
The first target user for the launch will be recent graduates actively seeking internships or short-term work experience.

## Core MVP Workflow
1. **User Onboarding**: Recent graduates create profiles highlighting their skills and interests.
2. **Project Posting**: Small businesses and startups can easily post short-term projects.
3. **Matching System**: Graduates receive notifications for projects that match their skills.
4. **Application Process**: Graduates apply for relevant projects through the platform.
5. **Project Execution**: Graduates complete projects, submit deliverables, and receive feedback.

## In Scope
- User profiles for graduates and organizations
- Project posting interface for organizations
- Basic matching system based on skills and project requirements
- Messaging system for communication between users 
- Deliverable submission feature
- Feedback and reputation system for users

## Out of Scope
- Complex matching algorithms
- Advanced user profile features (e.g., endorsements, dynamic portfolios)
- Extensive project management tools
- Legal compliance or detailed employment categorization per country
- Marketing tools for businesses

## Critical Assumptions
1. Graduates are willing to engage in short-term projects for minimal or no pay to gain experience.
2. Small businesses are willing to post projects on the platform.
3. The platform can establish a trust-based environment to prevent issues of companies seeking free labor.
4. Graduates will return to the platform for multiple projects.

## How To Test Quickly
- Conduct a survey among recent graduates to gauge interest in short-term projects for experience.
- Recruit a small cohort of businesses willing to post trial projects and observe engagement levels.
- Analyze the interaction levels with project postings (views, applications) during a limited beta release.

## Acceptance Criteria
1. At least 50 recent graduate profiles created within the first month.
2. A minimum of 10 small businesses posting one or more projects by the end of the testing phase.
3. An engagement metric where at least 30% of graduates apply to projects posted.
4. Gather qualitative feedback from both graduates and businesses regarding the platform’s usability and perceived value.

## Risks And Failure Modes
- Risk of low engagement from either recent graduates or small businesses.
- Potential for abuse of the platform by companies seeking free labor.
- Difficulty in maintaining a high-quality experience, leading to poor feedback and reputation.
- Variability in legal status impacting project engagement and user trust.

## Recommendation
Proceed with the project to build the MVP focusing on the core features that facilitate user onboarding, project posting, and basic communication. Prioritize establishing trust and value with initial users to validate the concept. Further investment should be contingent on successful validation of the critical assumptions and metrics set forth in the acceptance criteria.
