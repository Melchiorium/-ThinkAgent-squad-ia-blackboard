# Evaluation Report - Version 15

## Final Decision: REVISE

## Global Note: 5/10

## Executive Verdict

Version 15 is better than the earlier SkillBridge proposal. It introduces an initial wedge, critical assumptions, a supply-first GTM direction, manual feedback, admin/support needs, and a useful "why this could fail" section.

But it is still not investable as a build decision. The proposal correctly identifies major risks, then underreacts to them. The recommendation to proceed with an MVP is premature because the business model, legal guardrails, project quality standards, and supply acquisition economics remain unresolved.

This should move to a concierge pilot, not a full platform MVP.

## Business Viability

The problem is real. Graduates and career switchers need credible experience, and small organizations have short-term needs. The proposal also correctly recognizes that the harder side of the market is securing quality project supply from organizations.

However, the wedge is still too broad. "Recent graduates actively seeking internships or short-term work experience" is not precise enough. The GTM mentions design, marketing, and data analysis, but does not choose one. The project should start with one candidate segment and one category of missions.

The central business risk remains unresolved: the platform depends on low-pay or no-pay projects. That may generate candidate interest short term, but it creates reputational, legal, and ethical risk. If the value exchange is not explicit and protected, SkillBridge becomes a thinly disguised unpaid labor marketplace.

## Profitability Potential

Profitability is still not demonstrated. The proposal contains no pricing model.

Missing business model decisions:

- who pays: business, candidate, school, recruiter, sponsor, or employer
- whether projects are paid, unpaid, stipend-based, or mixed
- platform fee, subscription, posting fee, or success fee
- expected revenue per project
- cost to acquire a posting organization
- cost to vet a project
- support cost per completed mission
- refund, dispute, and legal exposure

The acceptance criteria focus on profiles, businesses, applications, and feedback. These are useful validation metrics, but they do not prove willingness to pay or margin.

The business can only work if organizations repeatedly post good projects and either pay SkillBridge directly or create enough downstream recruiting value to support a monetization model. That is not proven here.

## Execution Complexity

The technical architecture is reasonable: web-first, monolith, simple modules, admin dashboard, reporting, analytics, and manual feedback. This is directionally appropriate for an early product.

But the MVP still includes too much product surface:

- profiles
- project posting
- matching
- notifications
- applications
- messaging
- deliverable submission
- feedback and reputation
- abuse reporting
- admin dashboard

For the current risk profile, the team should not build matching and messaging first. Matching can be manual. Messaging can be email or managed introductions. Feedback can be structured interviews. The product should validate supply quality and outcome value before automating marketplace mechanics.

The hard part is operational: project vetting, anti-abuse controls, scope definition, dispute handling, and legal classification. The architecture names these risks but does not define the workflows deeply enough.

## Go-To-Market Realism

The GTM has improved because it correctly says the supply side must be secured first. Starting with small businesses and organizations is the right instinct.

But the acquisition plan is still vague:

- no exact vertical
- no target geography
- no outreach volume
- no expected conversion rate
- no incentive for organizations
- no partnership names or channels with clear owner
- no definition of a qualified project
- no launch threshold beyond 10 businesses

"Direct outreach, networking events, partnerships with educational institutions, and email marketing" is a list of tactics, not a go-to-market plan.

The plan should be much more specific: for example, recruit 15 local startups needing marketing audits, manually scope each project, require a fixed deliverable template, and validate whether at least 5 organizations would pay to repeat.

## Competitive Risk

Competitive risk is high. SkillBridge overlaps with:

- internships
- freelance marketplaces
- job boards
- university career services
- alumni networks
- micro-internship platforms
- junior agencies
- nonprofit volunteer platforms
- LinkedIn and community-based matching

The product is easy to copy. Defensibility would need to come from verified project quality, trusted institutional partnerships, repeat organization demand, and measurable employability outcomes.

Version 15 does not yet show a clear moat or differentiated acquisition advantage.

## Major Risks

- The platform becomes perceived as unpaid labor extraction.
- Organizations post low-quality or poorly scoped projects.
- Candidate demand outpaces quality supply.
- Legal/employment classification varies by country and is not resolved.
- No clear willingness to pay.
- Matching may create activity without business value.
- Feedback and reputation can be gamed or become low-signal.
- Universities may bring candidates but not paying organizations.
- A full MVP build may consume budget before the riskiest assumptions are validated.

## What Improved

- The proposal now names critical assumptions.
- GTM correctly identifies supply as the side to secure first.
- Architecture is more pragmatic and web-first.
- Manual feedback is correctly proposed before automation.
- Admin/support/reporting needs are now visible.
- The proposal includes a useful failure analysis section.

## What Still Needs Work

- Pick one launch segment and one mission category.
- Define the monetization model.
- Define whether projects are paid, unpaid, or stipend-based.
- Add non-negotiable project quality criteria.
- Define legal/geographic scope.
- Replace vanity acceptance metrics with business validation metrics.
- Do not build matching before manual matching proves repeatable.
- Define anti-abuse and dispute workflows before launch.

## Final Recommendation

Do not approve a full MVP build yet. Approve only a tightly controlled concierge pilot.

Required pilot conditions:

- One country or legal market.
- One candidate segment.
- One project category.
- 10-20 manually vetted organizations.
- Clear rule that projects are paid, stipend-based, or explicitly structured as portfolio-building with strict limits.
- Manual matching before building a matching engine.
- Manual project vetting before public posting.
- Measured willingness to pay or repeat.
- Completion rate above 70%.
- Strong candidate satisfaction and clear portfolio value.
- At least 5 organizations willing to post again or pay for the service.

Version 15 is a better proposal, but the investment decision remains REVISE. The next step is proof, not platform build.
