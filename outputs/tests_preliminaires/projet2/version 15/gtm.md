## Go-To-Market Notes

- **Main Market Bottleneck**: Establishing trust between recent graduates and small businesses regarding the use of the platform to prevent abuse for free labor while ensuring high-quality project offerings.

- **Side of the Market to Secure First**: Start with engaging small businesses and organizations, as their willingness to post projects will validate the concept and attract graduates.

- **Initial Target Audience**: Focus exclusively on recent graduates actively seeking experience, particularly those in specific fields (e.g., design, marketing, data analysis) where projects can be clearly defined and showcased in portfolios.

- **Positioning**: SkillBridge will be positioned as a trustworthy platform that bridges the gap between talented recent graduates seeking practical experience and small businesses needing affordable project assistance, while ensuring quality and professionalism on both ends.

- **First Acquisition Motion**: Utilize direct outreach to local small businesses, networking events, and partnerships with educational institutions to find businesses willing to post projects. This could be supplemented by a small-scale email marketing campaign targeting business owners to introduce the value proposition.

- **First Activation Loop**: Design an engaging onboarding process for both graduates and businesses that showcases successful project outcomes and feedback. Track metrics such as project starts to completed projects ratio, and feedback ratings, which will increase traction and encourage revisits.

- **What Must Exist Before Public Launch**: 
  1. A functional MVP with a streamlined onboarding experience for both graduates and businesses.
  2. At least 10 businesses committed to posting projects with brief outlines of deliverables.
  3. A feedback system to capture experiences and resolve any initial issues.

## Review Summary
The main challenge for SkillBridge lies in building trust between graduates and small businesses while ensuring the quality of projects. The recommended GTM direction is to first engage small businesses to secure project offerings, which will in turn attract recent graduates, leveraging selective outreach and a strong onboarding experience.

## Critical Assumptions
1. Small businesses will post projects on the platform.
2. Recent graduates are willing to engage in short-term projects for minimal or no pay to gain experience.
3. The platform can build a trusted environment to mitigate the abuse of free labor.
4. Feedback mechanisms will effectively improve platform quality based on initial engagements.
5. Graduates will find sufficient value to return for multiple projects.

## Requested Changes
1. Develop a clear onboarding guide outlining how both businesses and graduates can benefit from the platform.
2. Implement a basic vetting system for project postings to assure quality and credibility.
3. Add a feedback mechanism early in the project to encourage honest reviews and insights.
4. Create case study templates to outline successful projects to serve as motivation and concrete examples for users.
5. Design a clear communication strategy to outline the platform's value in terms of career building for graduates.

## Risks
1. Insufficient engagement from small businesses leads to a lack of project offerings.
2. High potential for companies abusing the platform to find free labor without providing adequate project work.
3. Difficulty in maintaining quality of projects leading to negative experiences for graduates.
4. Misalignment of expectations between graduates and businesses regarding project outputs.
5. Legal concerns across jurisdictions regarding project classifications which impact user engagement.

## Open Questions
1. How will SkillBridge manage the varying legal classifications of project work in different countries?
2. What specific metrics will be used to assess engagement from both graduates and businesses effectively?
3. Are there particular industries or sectors expected to yield higher interest in project postings?
4. How can the platform ensure a consistent quality of projects published by businesses?
5. What will the user support process look like once the platform begins to operate?

## Why This Could Fail Even With Good Execution
Even with competent execution, SkillBridge could fail if the underlying assumption that small businesses are willing to post projects and graduates are eager to engage for low or no pay proves incorrect, leading to low user activity and a lack of verifiable traction in the market.
